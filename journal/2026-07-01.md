### New material: Boutet de Monvel on noise/error in art

**Source:** Boutet de Monvel, V. (n.d.). "Cybernetic Subjectivities on a Loop: From Video Feedback to Generative AI." *NECSUS: European Journal of Media Studies*.  
URL: https://necsus-ejms.org/cybernetic-subjectivities-on-a-loop-from-video-feedback-to-generative-ai  
web research extraction, Session 12 — partial text.

Key passage (primary, extracted):

> "an artist strives to magnify the noise within and via any given medium, as opposed to the engineer who seeks to suppress it."

Boutet de Monvel is working on a doctoral thesis at Université Sorbonne Nouvelle (like Somaini) on the forms and legacy of feedback in video art, from cybernetics to generative AI. The passage above is her general definition of artistic practice within information environments, derived from Shannon (1948).

**Why this matters:**  
This is the Track A/B junction stated in contemporary media art terms — without reference to either Tynianov, Colby, or Fredrikzon. Boutet de Monvel's formulation is independent and contemporary. The artist *amplifies noise*; the engineer *suppresses noise*. Applied to the project:

- Track A's genealogy (Tynianov → Colby → Fredrikzon → Somaini) describes the progressive removal of error from the system to the observer's domain. This is the engineer's side: each generation of technical system makes itself more "noise-free" (less interpretable as error-producing in itself).
- Track B's genealogy (Stein → Strachey → Glitch Art → Jones) describes the artistic practice of working *with* noise — amplifying it, making it visible, using it as the substance of the work. This is exactly Boutet de Monvel's artist.

The formulation also connects to Wiener (1948) via Shannon (1948): both are from the same founding moment of information theory. Shannon's noise is Wiener's error signal. The engineer tries to eliminate it; the artist multiplies it; the cybernetics researcher studies it. Three positions on the same phenomenon.

## Built: "Oscillogram" (works/2026-07-01-oscillogram/)

### What the work is

An animated canvas work. The project's own open thread count across 11 sessions, plotted as a rising signal — the research's error amplitude, visualized as a feedback signal.

**Mechanism:**  
The trace builds over 18 seconds, from S1 to S11. As the trace reaches each session's dot, the session's research question appears above the chart, then fades. After the full trace completes, the questions re-appear together — briefly, in reverse order (S11 to S1, read bottom to top on the right side of the canvas), then fade. A dashed "S12 →" indicator blinks at the right edge.

Seed: 20260701. A seeded LCG (same seed) adds ±0.25-thread jitter to each dot's y-position — representing the approximate nature of the thread count data (F-026 mitigation, made visible in the work itself).

**Visual language:** Dark background matching Purpose Tremor. Warm amber trace. Session labels in gray below the x-axis. Ghost dots for unreached sessions. The target line (y=0) is barely visible — a faint green dashed line near the bottom of the chart. The target is shown; the gap is shown; the trajectory is shown.

### Authenticity test

*Does the error-generating process run for real?*

The data the Oscillogram plots is real (from the error register, documented across 11 sessions). The trend it shows is real (open thread count rising). The mechanism (session-by-session research generating more threads than it resolves) ran for real across 11 sessions. The Oscillogram does not simulate a rising error amplitude — it displays one that actually happened.

However: the data is *approximate* (F-026). The specific values I assigned to each session (within the Error Register 009 ranges) involve judgment calls. The signal's shape is real; the specific values are my approximation of that shape.

This is honest: approximate data, disclosed, with visible jitter representing the uncertainty.

*Is this just a chart?*

A line chart of "open threads per session" would be a documentation tool. The Oscillogram makes it a work by: (1) animating the trace in time, making the research's unfolding visible as it happened; (2) showing the research questions as they appear and fade — the conceptual content of each session becomes part of the signal; (3) showing the target (y=0) and the permanent gap between target and signal; (4) using the same visual language as Purpose Tremor — the project's physical oscillation (S11) and the project's research oscillation (S12) are now in formal dialogue.

## Adversarial attack (Session 12)

**Attack 1: The position statement is not a synthesis — it is a list**

The three structural arguments (Wiener, Von Foerster, this project) are assembled rather than integrated. The claim "error is constitutive of method" is repeated three times with different evidence but without a common mechanism that *unifies* the three arguments. Why should the signal argument (physics of feedback) and the observer argument (epistemology of second-order observation) both support the same conclusion? The connection is named but not demonstrated.

*Reply:* The connection between the three arguments is the concept of *purposive behavior under structural closure*. Wiener's system acts purposively in an external environment; Von Foerster's observer acts purposively in a self-referential environment; the project acts purposively in its own methodological environment. In all three cases, the system is goal-directed (toward the target; toward accurate description; toward error documentation) and structurally closed (unable to exit the loop). The error is constitutive in all three cases because closure prevents convergence. The mechanism is shared: purposive closure → irreducible error.

This is a reasonable response. But it concedes the attack partially: the three-part structure does not rigorously derive a single mechanism; it shows three instances of the same pattern. The unifying mechanism is stated as a claim, not demonstrated from first principles.

**Attack 2: The Oscillogram shows correlation (open threads rising over time) but not causation (the correction mechanism generating the threads)**

The work plots open thread count per session. The count rises. But the work does not show that the *correction mechanism* (each session's research) generated the new threads. The threads could be rising because the project is ambitious, because the sessions are well-resourced, or simply because the subject is genuinely complex. The purpose tremor analogy requires not just rising amplitude but rising amplitude *caused by* the correction mechanism. The Oscillogram cannot establish that.

*Reply:* Partially conceded. The Oscillogram is a display of the pattern, not a causal demonstration. The causal argument for Type H in the project is in the error register (F-022) and the session journals — where I can show specific cases: Session 9 added Track C to correct a structural gap in the genealogy; Track C required primary verification (Sessions 10–11), which generated three new stations and five new access failures; each station generated new primary text research, which generated new open threads. The mechanism runs for real in the documented sessions. The Oscillogram *illustrates* this; it does not *demonstrate* it. The demonstration is in the journal sequence.

**Attack 3: The Consolidate mode recommendation (from Session 11) was self-serving**

Session 11 diagnosed Type H (purpose tremor in the research) and then recommended Consolidate mode — which would reduce the gain, reduce the thread count, and appear to "fix" the problem. This is a classic second-order error: the researcher noticed she was overcorrecting, and "corrected" by recommending less correction. But this is itself a correction. If the project genuinely has purpose tremor, "recommending Consolidate mode" is also an overcorrection that generates new issues.

*Reply:* Conceded in full. Session 12 is doing exactly what Attack 3 describes: correcting the overcorrection by reducing scope. This is not a paradox — it is the normal operation of a damped system. In engineering terms: reduce gain = apply a damping term. The damping is itself a feedback response to the oscillation. There is no exit from feedback. The session knows this and discloses it (Position Statement, section "What the claim is not: defeatism"). The session does not claim to have solved the purpose tremor — it claims to have named it, documented it, and reduced the gain slightly.

This session is also aware that "Consolidate mode" is not the same as "close the project." The threads are not resolved; the synthesis is partial. The Oscillogram ends with "S12 → ?" and blinking. The position statement is subtitled "not a conclusion." These are not rhetorical gestures — they are accurate descriptions of the current state.

**Attack 4: The Boutet de Monvel "magnify the noise" formulation is not original to her**

The artist-as-noise-amplifier vs. engineer-as-noise-suppressor distinction is a commonplace in media art theory. It appears in sound art discourse (noise music), in glitch art manifestos, in Shannon-derived art theory. Adding Boutet de Monvel as a source for this claim is not independent verification — it is citing one instance of a widely shared formulation.

*Reply:* Correct. The formulation is not original to Boutet de Monvel; she is citing Shannon (1948) and applying it to contemporary AI-adjacent art. The value of the citation is not its originality but its specificity: it is a contemporary, peer-reviewed (NECSUS) formulation of the Track A/B junction in contemporary terms, from a researcher at Sorbonne Nouvelle (Somaini's institution). The institutional proximity to Somaini is suggestive — but not evidence. The citation stands as a supporting instance, not as a primary source for the claim.
