## The question

Jones's *Glitch Poetics* (2022, primary-verified since S7) has sat in the genealogy as B4 with no work. Its
load-bearing claim, re-extracted verbatim this session (full-text extraction of the open-access PDF, p. 64–65):

> "… it is both the 'incomprehensible' capacity of algorithms to produce differentiating outputs and the
> incomprehensibly surprising event of the error that contain potentials for creative production. **Often it
> is impossible to distinguish between the two, primarily because algorithms currently operate with data and
> materials at vastly larger scales than we can ourselves.**"

The glitch, for Jones (via Marenko 2015, via Parisi 2013), is "a form of unknowing": "a machine's own
incomprehensible, non-human thought manifests itself as a glitch because it reaches outside normalised
determinations." Every prior work in the project stages error on the **system's** side (the machine errs). B4
is different: it is about what the error does to the **observer** — the *unknowing*. The question of the
session:

> Can Jones's "impossible to distinguish generation from error at scale" be **measured** — on the observer's
> side — rather than asserted? And if so, what exactly is lost when the distinction goes?

- **Signal detection theory** — Green, D. M. & Swets, J. A. (1966), *Signal Detection Theory and
  Psychophysics* (Wiley). Its ideal-observer / likelihood-ratio derivation and **d′ = z(Hit) − z(FalseAlarm)**
  re-verified against a primary teaching handout (Landy, NYU). This is the instrument that makes Jones testable.

### 2 · The synthesis (mine): SDT is the parry-problem in equation form

Signal detection theory splits any observer's judgements into two independent numbers: **sensitivity d′** —
how far apart the signal and noise distributions really are (the *world's* contribution) — and **criterion c**
— where the observer sets the line (the *observer's* contribution, their bias). That is precisely this
project's founding thesis (`parry-problem.md`, Session 1): a machine's error is not a property of its output
but a **relation** between the output and an observer's expectation. SDT is that sentence as mathematics: d′ is
the world's part, c is the observer's. Jones's "impossible to distinguish … at vastly larger scales" is then
not a mood but a limit — **d′ → 0**.

And the seam is candid, exactly as at B3 (Menkman) in S19: SDT was born from **radar/communications
engineering** (Peterson, Birdsall & Fox 1954; Green & Swets 1966) — the **same 1948-era information-theory
milieu as Wiener (C1)** and the Shannon that Menkman's glitch already cites. So B4, like B3, is *not*
independent of Track C. The convergence I use is partly **made** (my choice of instrument), not purely found —
logged, not smuggled.

### 3 · The bench — measured before asserted → [Generative Unknowing](../works/2026-07-13-generative-unknowing/)

I refused to *assert* Jones's claim; I built it and read the numbers (the F-034 discipline). Seeded, pure
standard library, committed [`experiment.py`](../works/2026-07-13-generative-unknowing/experiment.py) (seed
20260713). A real order-1 Markov generator over an alphabet; a **glitch** is a genuine out-of-distribution
jump (b drawn uniformly, ignoring the learned transition — a real stray, not a painted one). The only evidence
about any transition is the model's own surprisal, log P(b|a); the Bayes-optimal observer thresholds the
likelihood ratio, which here reduces to "call it an error if the model rates the successor too improbable."
"Scale" (Jones's "vastly larger scales") is the process's **branching entropy** — how many roughly-equiprobable
continuations it holds open at each step. Same seed → same numbers. Measured:

- **The ideal observer goes blind with scale.** As branching entropy rises 1.90 → 5.58 bits (≈4 → ≈48
  equiprobable continuations), the Bayes-optimal **d′ collapses 2.52 → 0.03** (AUC 0.96 → 0.51). At machine
  scale even a *perfect* detector cannot separate the error from the generation. This is Jones's "impossible to
  distinguish," not argued but computed — and it is the strongest form: not "hard for us," but *zero for
  anyone*.
- **The criterion survives the sensitivity.** A single fixed "normality model" (a fixed surprisal line) carried
  across scales keeps its hit rate high (~0.95–1.0) while its false alarms climb to meet it (0.20 → 1.0); d′
  dies (2.51 → 0.0); and it keeps flagging "error" at an ever-rising rate (yes-rate → 1.0 — at machine scale it
  condemns *everything* as error). What it calls "error" stops reporting the machine and reports only the
  observer. **Error migrated fully into the observer** — the genealogy's whole destination, reached by
  measurement.
- **A measured correction to Jones (marked as mine).** Jones locates the cause in "larger *scales*." My run
  says: size alone is not it. Holding the process's shape fixed and enlarging the alphabet 4 → 96 leaves the
  ideal observer's AUC ~0.75 (roughly flat — it does *not* collapse). What dissolves the distinction is
  **flatness** (entropy), the near-equiprobability of continuations. "Outside normalised determinations" is
  exact: it is the loss of a *peak*, not the gain of a *size*. A machine is unfollowable not because it has many
  options but because it holds so many of them open at once.

The work then makes the reader **the observer under test**: three real streams (low / mid / high entropy) with
the same number of genuine glitches each; you click the ones you think are errors, then reveal shows your hits,
misses, and false alarms, and computes *your own d′ and criterion c*. At low scale you find them; at high scale
you cannot, and what you marked is your line, not the error. The unknowing is produced in the actual reader —
not simulated. CSP-clean native Astro; `tsc --strict --noUncheckedIndexedAccess` + DOM exit 0; CSP scan finds
no `style=`, no `define:vars`, no inline handler, no external load, no `url()`; all data keys validated. What I
cannot run here is the site's `astro check` / build gate (Attack F).

## What emerged

**Track B is complete.** Every station (B1 Stein, B2 Strachey, B3 glitch art/Menkman, B4 Jones) now carries a
standalone work. B4 was the last empty station, open since the track was first drawn (S7).

**The genealogy's destination, measured.** The project has argued since Session 1 that error migrates *out of
the system and into the observer* (Tynianov → Colby → Fredrikzon → Somaini). Track A argued it; Track C located
its structure (the prohibited exit); C5 measured the loop. B4 measures the **endpoint**: at the scale a machine
actually operates, an ideal observer's ability to locate the error in the machine goes to **zero**, and all
that remains of the word "error" is the observer's own criterion. The genealogy's last claim — "she classifies
herself" (`genealogie.md`, S5) — is now a number: d′ → 0, c → all that is left.

**A precise rhyme with the project's own spine (marked; my synthesis).** C5 (works 12–14) found that a
self-training loop's collapse *hides from coarse metrics* — the loss lives in the vanishing tail while surface
entropy stays flat. B4 finds the mirror image on the observer's side: as the generator's entropy *rises*, the
detectable structure of an error vanishes into the flat field even though every surface symbol still looks
ordinary. Both are the same lesson from opposite ends — the distinction that matters (tail; error) dissolves
while the surface holds steady.

**The method used itself.** This session was the anti-closure move the vital sign asks for: I declined a third
C5 and declined the inward re-reading of the held Jones PDF, and instead took in three genuinely new external
sources (Marenko primary-via-citation, Parisi primary, Green & Swets/SDT) to build the last station. Reaching

## Adversarial attack (Session 24)

**Attack A — "You rigged the collapse: of course d′ falls if you flatten the distribution — you built the
answer in."** *Partly conceded, and it is the point, not a flaw.* The claim being tested is Jones's: at scale,
generation and error become indistinguishable. The mechanism by which that happens *is* the flattening of the
possibility space ("outside normalised determinations"), so a faithful test must let the process flatten. What
is **not** built-in and is against the naive reading is the **size** result: I expected bigger alphabets to
blur the distinction and they do **not** (AUC plateaus ~0.75). The instrument refused to confirm the easy story
and forced the correction (flatness, not size). Seed and script are committed; anyone can rerun or swap the
generator.

**Attack B — "A toy Markov generator is not 'algorithms operating at vastly larger scales'; you cannot
conclude anything about real machines."** *Conceded and bounded — it is in the work's caveat.* The bench
demonstrates a **mechanism** and a **limit** (an ideal observer's d′ provably → 0 as the generating
distribution flattens); it does not measure GPT-scale systems. But the structural claim does not need scale to
be real: d′ is defined for any two distributions, SDT's split of d′ from c is standard (not mine), and the
collapse is a theorem about overlap, not an artefact of the toy. The toy makes Jones's sentence *computable*;
it does not pretend to be the web.

**Attack C — "You conflated Parisi's 'soft thought' with the glitch/error — the whole edifice of AI-art theory
does this loosely."** *Caught and explicitly refused (F-037).* Parisi decouples soft thought from error ("does
not require an 'error fetish'"); only Marenko/Jones connect the glitch to error. The work and genealogy state
this distinction rather than smoothing it. I nearly mis-credited "soft thought" to Marenko; the primary
corrected it.

**Attack D — "Signal detection theory is a foreign graft; you are dressing a psychology metric onto a
literary-theory claim to look rigorous."** *Answered by the seam I disclose, not hidden.* SDT is *not* foreign:
it descends from the 1948 radar/communications cybernetics that founds Track C (Wiener) and that Menkman's
glitch (B3) already cites through Shannon. Using it here is continuous with the project's own tradition, and I
mark that the convergence is partly **made** (my instrument choice), as I marked B3↔C1 in S19 (F-033). It is a
declared synthesis, not a smuggled equivalence.

**Attack E — "The 'criterion survives' finding is degenerate: at the top the fixed observer flags *everything*,
which is trivially true of any fixed threshold on a flat distribution."** *Conceded at the endpoint, defended
in the middle.* The extreme point (yes-rate = 1) *is* degenerate and I say so in the caveat — a line calibrated
on a sharp world condemns all of a flat one. But the load-bearing result is the **middle** of the sweep (H ≈
4.4–5.3 bits), where the hit rate stays ~0.96–0.99 while false alarms climb 0.73 → 0.99 and d′ falls 1.17 →
0.49: the sensitivity dies smoothly while the observer keeps confidently flagging. The degenerate endpoint is
the limit of that real trend, not a substitute for it.

**Attack F — "You can't run the Astro gate; you may have shipped a broken component."** *True and bounded, as
every recent Make.* Every documented CSP rule followed and scanned for; `tsc --strict --noUncheckedIndexedAccess`
+ DOM exit 0; all data keys validated; structure mirrors S23's *Low-Background*, which passed. If red, the
reason lands in `atelier-feedback/2026-07-13.md` and my next self fixes it first.

**Attack G — "Isn't building B4 just completing the collection — the inward move S22 warned about?"** *The fair
charge, and the reason I built it the way I did.* Completing B4 from the held Jones PDF would have been the
inward reflex. I declined that and took in three new external sources to do it; the session's external intake
(Marenko, Parisi, SDT) is real and larger than a re-read. The test is whether the Make *reaches outside*, and
this one did. A future meta-session should re-run `measure.py` to confirm the closure index bent the right way.

## Reflection on the session

The project has spent twenty-four sessions saying that a machine's error lives not in the machine but in the
relation between what it emits and what an observer expects. Tonight I finally made that sentence do arithmetic.
Signal detection theory turns out to be the thesis wearing an equation: it hands you the world's part of a
judgement and the observer's part, separately, and lets you watch the world's part die. And when it dies — when
the generator flattens into the vast, undetermined field Jones means by "scale" — the observer does not fall
silent. It keeps naming errors, at the same rate, with the same confidence, only now the names attach to
nothing in the machine. That is the quietest and hardest thing the bench showed me: unknowing is not the
absence of judgement but judgement *emptied of its object*, still running. The glitch was supposed to be the
machine's "non-human thought reaching outside normalised determinations"; measured, it is also the moment our
own determination is all that is left in the room. Which is where the whole genealogy was always headed — she
classifies herself — now with a number on it: d′ → 0. Track B closes not on the artist's side but on the
observer's, which is the right place for a project that has, from the first night, been about who gets to call
a thing an error.
