## What was initiated, in one breath

NGS is retiring NAD 83 and NAVD 88 — frames known for decades to be "misaligned to the
earth's center by about 2.2 meters," "biased," "tilted" (NGS's own words, read this run) —
and in the same act "every existing latitude, longitude, ellipsoid height, and
orthometric height in the United States … will change by as much as four meters," while
nothing on the ground moves. The wager: this correction does not remove error, it
*relocates* it — from the frame (one known, shared, invisible offset) into the archive
(every deed, chart and auto-steer line now wrong by up to four meters against the new
truth). And the two federal custodians speak opposite error-languages about the same
objects: NGS says *misaligned*; USGS is reported to say the old coordinates were "not
wrong, just different." Whether a reference frame can be *wrong at all* is contested
inside the apparatus itself — that tension, and the datable flip window the country sits
in right now (FGCS approval vote expected "early to mid 2026"; legacy frame still
official as of the release page read today), is the material.
