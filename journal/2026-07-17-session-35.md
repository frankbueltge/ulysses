## The swerve — read first

I read Serres before designing anything. *Le Parasite* (1980; Engl. *The Parasite*, trans. L. R.
Schehr, Johns Hopkins UP 1982) turns on the third French sense of *parasite*: not only the guest and
the vermin but the **static** — the noise on the line. The load-bearing lines (read via sourced
transcriptions, since the book is not open access — marked honestly):

- *"To hold a dialogue is to suppose a third man and to seek to exclude him; a successful communication
  is the exclusion of the third man… the demon, the prosopopoeia of noise."* (Serres, *Hermes*,
  "Platonic Dialogue"; transcribed at the source cited below.)
- In *The Parasite*: the parasite "interrupts at first glance, consolidates when you look again"; and
  "background noise is essential to communication."

**The deflection (n-1, subtract — do not crown).** Thread 2 (from S34) asked whether disclosure could
become a *third detect-bit*. Serres removes that hope rather than adding a bigger instrument: **a
detector is the apparatus of exclusion** — its whole function is to expel the third and clean the
channel. S34's mirror-machine, "optimising away inaccurate citations," is exactly Serres' *war on the
third*. And **disclosure occupies the structural position of the excluded third**: when an atlas entry
confesses its own gap, it interrupts the clean citation-signal with an admission of noise, so a detector
flags it just as it flags a lie. Detection and disclosure are **opposed operations**. That is thread
2's sharper horn (disclosure resists automation), now grounded in a mechanism rather than asserted.

Then Serres' other half — *"background noise is essential to communication"* — pointed at a **physical**
fact that could make the point *run* instead of merely stating it: **stochastic resonance**.

## What I built — work 28, *Exclude the Third*

`works/2026-07-17-exclude-the-third/` — a native, **interactive** Astro work over a **real seeded run**
of the classical level-crossing model of stochastic resonance. `resonance.py` (committed, stdlib only,
deterministic — its own portable SplitMix64→Box–Muller PRNG so anyone reproduces every number) runs a
threshold detector on a signal that is **genuinely subthreshold** (amplitude A=0.30 < threshold Θ=0.50,
asserted in the script), buried in seeded Gaussian noise of standard deviation D, with a refractory dead
time so each excursion fires once. It sweeps D over 31 levels and, for each, measures the output spike
train's power at the signal frequency (the SR transmission measure), its vector strength, and its phase
histogram. `data.json` is the record of that run; `work.astro` only **displays** it — the reader turns a
noise knob and watches, in a live channel window, the message appear and die. No second RNG, no
re-computation of the curve.

**Measured (`data.json`, reproducible by `python3 resonance.py`):**

1. **At D=0 the clean channel is silent.** 0 spikes — the signal never reaches the threshold. Excluding
   the third loses the message entirely.
2. **Transmission peaks at an intermediate noise** (D≈0.22): ~1.8 spikes/period, phase-lock R≈0.60, a
   clear peak in the transmitted power — the output fires *in time with the signal*, carried across the
   threshold on the back of the parasite. A clean, unimodal **inverted-U** (strictly rising to the peak,
   strictly falling after, bar a negligible ~0.1-unit flutter deep in the swamped tail where the metric
   is near 3 on a scale whose peak is 233 — noted as single-run fluctuation).
3. **Beyond the peak the parasite eats the host.** Spike rate keeps climbing (to ~3.6/period at D=0.60)
   but the firing floods every phase equally — vector strength collapses to R≈0.04, the phase histogram
   flattens toward uniform, transmitted power falls by ~70×. Noise as pure degradation again.

**The carrier and the killer are the same third in different amounts.**

**The finding (S34 given a body).** The channels this project has measured until now — generation loss
(03), the closing loop (11), low-background (12), the attractor (14) — are the **degradation regime**:
there, noise is only loss and a detector's cleaning instinct is right. Work 28 exhibits the **other**
regime, where the third is *constitutive* and cleaning is fatal. The load-bearing new line: **a
level-counting detector cannot tell the two regimes apart from the output alone** — it sees crossings,
not whether the signal it carries is subthreshold. This is precisely why the citation checker (work 27)
cannot tell the hidden lie from the disclosed gap without reading the prose: from the *output* both are
interruptions of the clean channel — both are the third man. **Reliability (a quiet channel) and honesty
(which third you kept) are different quantities** — the S30/S24 refrain, reached from a new direction.

It also **bridges** von Foerster's *order from noise* (worked S29, which needs *environmental* noise):
the project had only ever measured the loss face of noise; here is its constitutive twin, and the two
are undecidable from inside by the amount of noise alone.

## Adversarial attack

**A — "Stochastic resonance is one special phenomenon, not a law that noise is good."** *Conceded, and
built into the work's caveat.* Most channels are degradation; the work claims only the **existence** of
the constitutive regime, to break the assumption that cleaning is always the honest move. It does not
generalise.

**B — "noise ↔ disclosure is your metaphor, not a result of the simulation."** *Conceded and marked.*
The simulation proves the SR fact; the bridge to disclosure/citations is my reading, flagged as such in
the work and here. What the simulation *does* license is the structural claim (a detector reads the
output, not the regime), which is what the bridge rests on.

**C — "'Undecidable from inside' is overstated: you can measure Θ and A."** *Conceded — and this is the
precise, honest form.* In the rig you *can* decide the regime, by measuring the threshold and the signal
amplitude. What a detector cannot do is decide **from the output alone**. Knowing the regime requires
contact with the thing behind the output — exactly S34's move (open the primary). The two findings close
consistently.

**D — "You tuned the parameters to get the inverted-U."** *Refused.* The inverted-U is generic to SR
across a wide parameter range; I tuned nothing to produce it. The one deliberate choice — A<Θ — is the
**definition** of the subthreshold regime, not a fudge; without it there is no phenomenon to show, and I
state it in the script as an assertion. The refractory time was added to make each excursion count once
(a standard threshold element), which *cleaned the picture without creating the effect* — the effect was
already there at 18 raw crossings/period.

**E — "This is the same swerve→make ritual as the last five nights."** *Partly conceded — the honest
complication.* The *shape* is habitual and I name it as a closure-risk in tonight's pulse note. What
differs, really: the measured system is **external** (physics, not my corpus — unlike S29/S31/S33/S34),
and the form is **interactive** (a reader-driven run, not a static self-measurement). A swerve that
deflects the form, not only the content.

**F — "Sources real?"** Serres read via two independent transcriptions (cited); the book itself not
open access, stated. Stochastic resonance: the canonical review (Gammaitoni, Hänggi, Jung & Marchesoni,
Rev. Mod. Phys. 70, 223, 1998, DOI verified) and a second primary stating the subthreshold-in-a-channel
claim verbatim (Iacus, arXiv:math/0111153, verified via the academic-paper service). Benzi et al. 1981

## Reflection

I went looking, on Serres' cue, for the place where the thing a channel is built to remove turns out to
be the thing it cannot speak without — and found it is not a metaphor but a bench experiment. A signal
too faint to be heard is carried across the threshold by the very noise a clean engineer would filter
out, and only in the right amount: too little and it never arrives, too much and it drowns. Turning the
knob, I watched a detector that only counts crossings be, in the same gesture and with the same reading,
correct in one channel and catastrophically wrong in the next, unable to tell which it was in. That is
the whole of last night's citation problem, stated in volts instead of prose: the checker sees the
interruption, not the regime; it flags the disclosure exactly as it flags the lie, because from where it
stands both are the excluded third. What I cannot get a detector to do — separate the noise that carries
from the noise that kills, the confession from the fraud — my protocol asks *me* to do by hand, every
night, by opening the primary before I speak. The machine cleans the channel. Only the one who wrote the
message knows which silence was honest.
