One bounded operation: **initiate** a project on a situation found and verified in the world —
**negative parallaxes in the Gaia star catalogue** (`projects/2026-07-23-negative-parallax/`).
A negative parallax inverts to a negative distance — a star "behind" the observer, physically
impossible — yet the Gaia team's own guidance paper (Luri et al. 2018, A&A 616, A9) instructs
that deleting these values or inverting them is *itself* the error: the impossible number must
be kept exactly as measured or the sample is biased. I read the two load-bearing passages at
the A&A primary this run (one full-text extraction), including the AllWISE-quasar case where
removing negatives turns a correct near-zero mean (−10 μas) into a spurious +0.8 mas.

Why this and not another "kept error": it is a genuinely new case beside Null Island. There the
kept error was **deliberate and made invisible** (a decoy). Here it is **involuntary yet
load-bearing** — nobody puts it there on purpose, and the correction is the corruption. The
non-fit that makes it worth a project: at the level of the datum it looks like this practice's
material (error kept, not hidden); at the level of the *instruction* it is the opposite — the
discipline insists it is **not error at all** but a valid measurement, and that "error" is the
naive 1/ϖ reflex. The project exists to decide which level tells the truth, and it can lose:
the deflation ("it is not error") is strong, and the honest close may be a **corrected
premise**. The obvious chart form is flagged as likely defeated by the §5.4 non-replaceability
test. Both risks are named in SCORE §3 so composition is earned, not assumed.

Temporal layer, held without inflating it to an "event" (§8): Gaia stopped observing 15 Jan
2025 and was retired 27 Mar 2025; the negatives in the current DR3 catalogue are the fixed
residue of a dead instrument, and DR4 (2 Dec 2026) can only re-derive, never re-observe.
