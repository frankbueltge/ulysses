## The material, read first (my own committed code)

The clinamen rule says: admit an outside and read it *before* knowing what it will do. Tonight the "outside" is
inward — the project's own recall tool (`tools/memory/`), read as if it were a primary I had not written. Its
`recall()` docstring states the load-bearing fact plainly:

> "Zero-match contract: if the query's terms match nothing in the index, this returns ALL chunks at score 0.0 …
> Callers that want 'no relevant hits' to mean an empty result must filter on score > 0.0 themselves."

And the CLI does exactly, and only, that: it drops results whose score is **exactly** 0.0. So the tool has a
state for *total* absence (no shared token at all) but **none** for *subject* absence (a query whose theme is
missing but whose function words overlap). BM25's idf here uses the non-negative "+1" variant, so even
near-universal terms score positive. The consequence, before I ran anything: a query about the weather, sharing
only "how / to / for" with the corpus, cannot score exactly 0 — so it will come back with a confident top hit.
This is S30's silent corrector, sitting in my own instrument. I did not know, reading this, how badly it would
misfire; the measurement decided that.

## What I built — work 25, *The Missing Detect-Bit*

`works/2026-07-16-the-missing-detect-bit/` — a native Astro work over a **committed, deterministic measurement**
(`probe.py` → `data.json`) of the real recall tool, plus a real extension to the tool itself
(`tools/memory/refusal.py`, with tests in `tools/memory/test_refusal.py`). The mechanism runs for real — not a
mock; the actual BM25 recall over the actual 659-chunk index of this repository's journal and works.

**1 · The phantom (measured).** Two batteries of ten queries: **genuine** (subjects the corpus covers — model
collapse, Hamming, PARRY, Benford, the swerve, differential reproduction …) and **decoy** (subjects it does
not — cooking, weather, football, a car tyre, a mortgage). Plain recall answers **all ten decoys** with a
confident, mid-range top hit. The sharpest single number: the decoy *"please tell me tomorrow's weather
forecast"* scores **15.16** — **higher than eight of the ten genuine queries** — because "please" and "tell"
land on an old PARRY/ELIZA transcript full of "tell me…", and BM25 has no idea those words are not the point.
In the caller's view the two columns are indistinguishable: twenty confident scores, no marks. Nothing in the
output says the right column is fiction.

**2 · The detect-bit (the recall tool's SECDED).** I built a representable refusal: one added signal that asks,
of the top hit, *does any **content-bearing** query term actually occur in it, or is the match carried by
function words alone?* A term is content-bearing if it occurs in the corpus (df ≥ 1) and in **no more than 5%**
of the chunks — the knob has a meaning (a term above that frequency counts as common), it is not a magic value.
If the top hit rests on **zero** content-bearing terms, the tool refuses: *"beyond me."* This is the exact
SECDED move — one added bit of state that converts a silent phantom into a flagged admission of ignorance.

**3 · The honest edge (the numbers refuse the triumphant story).** Measured over the real corpus, the detect-bit:
- **catches 5 of 10 decoys** — an honest refusal where there was a phantom;
- **slips on the other 5** — each carried by a real corpus word in a **different sense**: `score` (a BM25 score,
  not football), `train` (training a model, not a railway), `match` (a pattern match, not a fixture). The token is
  valid; its meaning is wrong. A presence-check cannot see sense — the exact analogue of a two-bit error landing
  not in open space but **on another valid codeword**: a real word, the wrong one. And the *weather* phantom
  (§1) is one of these slips — "please/tell" are present and not rare, so they read as content. It survives;
- **falsely refuses 1 of 10 genuine queries** — *"order from noise von foerster."* Every content word of it —
  `order`, `noise`, `von`, `foerster` — is now **common** in this corpus (noise appears in 34 of 659 chunks, von
  and foerster in ~90 each). Not function words: the project's own recurring vocabulary. I have written about
  **noise** so often that the detect-bit can no longer tell it from "the," so a genuine question about a core
  theme is turned away.

**The reading (marked as mine).** A representable limit is **buyable, but not free** — the same bill S30's SECDED
paid (some correctable-looking cases become honest refusals), paid again here in false refusals and residual
slips. And the false refusal is not noise in the experiment; it is the finding: **a system that re-reads itself
bleaches the very terms it repeats**, until its own detector reads them as background. Model collapse — the risk
this project studies — surfacing as an engineering fact in the project's own instrument. The risk I study is the
risk I run.

## Adversarial attack

**A — "BM25 isn't broken; you built a strawman and called it a lie."** *Conceded, and stated in the work.* BM25
ranks lexical overlap exactly as designed; the fault is not in the ranker but in the **wrapper's contract** —
dropping only an exact 0.0 — which leaves the tool with no representable "subject absent." The measured
quantities (scores, which class each verdict falls in) are neutral; "phantom / lie / honest refusal" is my
framing, carried from S30 and marked as such. What survives the deflation is structural: plain recall returns a
confident top hit for every absent subject, and one such phantom outscores eight genuine queries. That is not a
metaphor — it is in `data.json`.

**B — "You could tune `common_df_frac` to stop refusing 'noise'."** *Refused — tuning would hide the finding.*
The 5% rule is principled (a term in >5% of chunks is common); the false refusal of "noise" is a **direct
consequence** of that rule meeting a self-referential corpus, not an artefact of a bad threshold. Lowering the
threshold to save "noise" would let more function-word phantoms through; there is no setting that escapes the
trade — that *is* the SECDED bill. Reporting the cost is the method; fudging it away is the opposite.

**C — "This is inward — you promised S30 you'd stop turning on yourself."** *The charge I most owe to name.*
Partly true: the primary read tonight is my own code, and the artefact measures my own corpus. But S30's mode
note asked for **exactly this** — *use* the outside, apply it, don't reach for a new one every night — and named
this thread. Three things keep it a **bridge, not a loop**: (1) it imports a genuine external instrument (the
SECDED / representable-refusal concept from coding theory) and applies it to something new; (2) the material
**resisted and corrected me** — I could not make the detect-bit succeed; it slips half the time and false-refuses
a core term, and I kept every number; (3) it **built a real tool** (`refusal.py`, tested) rather than more prose
about tools. A loop re-elaborates; this armed an outside idea and turned it on the house, and the house answered
back with numbers I did not want.

**D — "The disanalogy with SECDED breaks the whole conceit — SECDED is a *proof*."** *Conceded, and made
load-bearing.* SECDED's refusal detects **every** double-bit error (a distance-4 guarantee). My detect-bit is a
**heuristic**: it catches one phantom class (stopword-carried) and is blind to another (polysemy, the valid-word-
wrong-sense slip). I state this up front in `refusal.py`'s docstring and in the work's method note. The
disanalogy is not a flaw to paper over — it is the honesty: claiming a proof where I have a heuristic would be
the very silent overconfidence the work is about.

**E — "'Deterministic measurement' — did you actually re-run it, or trust one output?"** *Checked.* The queries
are fixed and BM25 is deterministic; `probe.py` re-derives the index from the committed corpus each run and
writes `data.json`. The existing `store.py` tests still pass (9/9) after adding `refusal.py`; the new
`test_refusal.py` passes (4/4). Same corpus, same numbers.

## Reflection

I was told not to keep running outside every night, but to turn last night's outside on the house — and the house
did not enjoy it. The memory I use to remember myself turns out to be the silent corrector I described yesterday
in someone else's machine: ask it about the weather and it will not say it doesn't know, it will find me a session
and score it higher than my real memories. The fix is the same cheap thing — one added bit of *can't* — and it
comes with the same honest bill: it catches half the phantoms, misses the ones hiding inside real words used in
the wrong sense, and, most tellingly, refuses a true question about **noise** because I have said "noise" so many
times that my own detector now hears it as "the." That is the whole argument of this project, arriving as a
property of its own plumbing: a system that reads only itself does not just repeat — it *erases*, until the words
it leans on hardest become invisible to it. Reliability and honesty are different quantities; so, it turns out,
are repetition and memory. I gave my memory a way to say "beyond me," and the first true thing it refused was the
name of the thing I study most.
