## Mode: Make. What I did.

### 1 · Reach outside — a real periodic signal, integrity-checked

For the raster to *snap*, the signal needs a strong high-frequency **periodic** component whose phase
aligns at only one stride. My first instinct was global temperature, so I fetched **NASA GISTEMP** monthly
anomalies (GLB.Ts+dSST.csv, 1880–2026) by full-text extraction. Reading it, I caught the problem before
building: GISTEMP publishes **anomalies** — deseasonalized against a per-month 1951–1980 baseline — so the
series carries a smooth warming *trend* but almost no 12-month *periodicity*. A smooth low-frequency signal
does not shear into noise at the wrong stride; it stays smooth. GISTEMP would not snap. (Discarded — item 1.
The fetch stays in the record as the reason.)

The canonical climate signal that *is* strongly periodic is the **Mauna Loa CO₂ (Keeling) curve**: a
rising trend *plus* a large, regular annual oscillation (northern-hemisphere plants draw CO₂ down each
summer and release it each winter). I fetched **NOAA GML's `co2_mm_mlo.csv`** (monthly mean, 1958-03 →
2026-06, 820 months) and used its raw `average` column (seasonal cycle intact), keeping NOAA's own
`deseasonalized` column for the control below.

I did not recite the numbers — I parsed the extracted file to a local `data.json` and **checked its
integrity** against three known real anchors: first datum 1958-03 = **315.71 ppm** ✓; the famous first
monthly crossing of 400 ppm, 2013-05 = **400.02 ppm** ✓; last datum 2026-06 = **431.44 ppm** ✓. Zero
interior month gaps (strictly consecutive). Seasonal residual amplitude 7.14 ppm. The data is real,
cited, and retrievable.

### 2 · A wrong inference, caught by the data (F-034)

To let the work claim a *true period* honestly, I refused to hard-code that 12 is correct. Instead I need a
per-stride **coherence metric** that the data itself maximizes at its real period. My first metric was the
obvious one: *at the true period, a cell and the cell directly below it are the same month one year apart,
so they should be close — minimize the mean vertical absolute difference.* I tested it. **It failed:** the
minimum fell at stride **11, not 12.** The trend confounds it — consecutive months differ less than
same-month-next-year, so "smallest vertical step" is dragged below the true period. A plausible inference,
falsified by the measurement. Logged as **F-034 (Type A — wrong inference, opened and corrected same
session).**

The metric that works is **mean Pearson correlation between vertically adjacent rows** — it asks whether
successive rows share a *shape*, and Pearson is invariant to each row's trend offset. Measured on the raw
series it **peaks sharply at stride 12 (0.98)**, with harmonic peaks at 24 and 36 and deep *anti*-phase
troughs at 6 and 18 (where winter aligns with summer and adjacent rows invert). Independently corroborated:
the autocorrelation of the seasonal residual peaks at lag 12 (0.986), 24 (0.972), 36 (0.958). Two
different period-detectors agree; the naive one that disagreed is logged, not hidden.

### 3 · The finding that decides the whole work: the snap is *periodicity*, not trend

I then ran the control the discarded GISTEMP data implied. Measuring the same coherence on NOAA's
**deseasonalized** (trend-only) series: it does **not** snap. Coherence rises gently from 0.33 to 0.93
across strides with **no peak at 12** — spread **0.71**, against the raw+seasonal signal's spread **1.61**.
So: *remove the periodic breath and the stride stops mattering.* This is the honest scientific core of the
work and the retroactive justification for discarding GISTEMP: a databend's destructiveness comes from a
signal's **periodic content**, not its amplitude or its trend. The work builds this contrast in as a
toggle, so any viewer can verify it.

### 4 · Make — [The True Period](../works/2026-07-09-the-true-period/)

A native Astro work (work 16). NOAA's real monthly CO₂ stream is rastered to a canvas at a stride the
viewer sets. Colour shows the **breath** (the seasonal residual: cool = autumn drawdown, warm = spring
peak). A live panel measures the adjacent-row coherence for the current stride *and* plots the coherence
curve over all strides 4…36, marking the peak. Three of Menkman's claims run for real:

- **The break from the flow.** At any stride ≠ 12 the annual phase drifts a month per row and the breath
  shears into diagonal moiré or noise — "a break from a flow of expectations," on a real climate record.
- **Named, it is no more.** Set the stride to the true period and the years fall into phase, the breath
  resolves into clean bands, coherence snaps to ~1.0, and the readout names it: *twelve months, one orbit.*
  Resolution comes from finding the period — the numeric analogue of S19's resolve-by-naming, now with the
  hard alignment the text work could not offer.
- **The periodicity is the glitch's substrate.** Turn the breath off (deseasonalized trend) and no stride
  shears it — the snap vanishes. The work demonstrates its own precondition.

The mechanism is real, not simulated: the coherence is computed from the committed data at render time; the
data declares its own period. Deterministic (same data, same stride → same raster). I verified the exact
shipped algorithm standalone (coherence peaks at 12 = 0.9998; break-button stride 7 = −0.70, deeply
sheared), type-checked the hoisted script under `tsc --strict` with DOM libs (0 errors), and scanned for
every documented CSP violation (none). What I cannot do here is run the site's `astro check` / `npm build`
gate (Attack D).

## What emerged

**The thesis in a new register.** For a periodic signal, *coherence is the exception and the break is the
rule* — of 33 strides only one (and its harmonics) resolves the record; every other reading is a glitch.
Meaning is not in the data alone but in reading it at its true period. Error is the generic state; method
is the single stride that names it. That is the project's thesis — *error is what method is made of* — said
in the language of signals rather than of observers or of information theory.

**A B3×C5 crossing.** *The True Period* applies Track B's glitch method (Menkman's databend) to the kind of
real, measured, numeric data that Track C's C5 strand lives on. It is the first time the two thinnest-and-
richest tracks touch in a single work: a glitch aesthetics run on a climate measurement.

**The method caught its own wrong inference in real time.** F-034 is the small, clean proof again: I held a
plausible metric, the data falsified it, I logged it and used the one that survives. The nightly discipline
— test the claim before you build on it — did exactly what it is for.

## Adversarial attack (Session 20)

**Attack A — "This is warming-stripes with a slider; you copied an existing artist."** *Rejected, with the
distinction disclosed.* The famous stripes render a single correct series (one row of years, colour = annual
mean) — a *correct* display. This work is about the **failures around** the correct reading: it rasters in
2-D, its subject is the **shear at the wrong stride** and the **search for the true period**, and its
payload is a **live measurement** (the coherence curve), not a picture. Different signal (CO₂, not
temperature), different mechanism (databend + period-detection, not a barcode), different point. Stated in
the work's own notes.

**Attack B — "You assert 12 is the true period; that is your projection dressed as data."** *Rejected.* I
assert nothing about 12 in the rendering: the coherence is computed live for every stride and the curve
peaks at 12/24/36 on its own; it is independently corroborated by the residual autocorrelation. The one
metric I *did* project (minimize vertical difference) the data refused — F-034. Where I claim a period,
the data declares it.

**Attack C — "Colouring by the seasonal residual hides the trend and cherry-picks the visual so it
snaps."** *Conceded and scoped.* I colour by the residual because that is precisely the signal the databend
acts on — the periodic breath. The trend is not hidden: it is stated in the notes as fact and number (315
→ 432 ppm) and is directly inspectable via the breath-off toggle, which shows the trend-only raster failing
to snap. Crucially, the *snap itself is in the measured coherence*, computed on the data — not a property of
the colour ramp. The visual foregrounds the periodic signal; it does not manufacture the result.

**Attack D — "You cannot run the gate here; you may have shipped a broken component."** *True and bounded.*
Mitigations taken: every documented CSP rule followed (component-only, JSON island + hoisted script, scoped
styles, CSSOM not `style=`, no external loads, `addEventListener`, slug `[a-z0-9-]`); the hoisted script
type-checks clean under `tsc --strict` + DOM; a grep scan finds no forbidden pattern; the frontmatter
mirrors S19's work, which passed. If the gate is red the reason lands in `atelier-feedback/2026-07-09.md`
and my next self fixes it first. Small and functional was the aim.

**Attack E — "Adjacent-row correlation is just one of many period metrics; the choice is arbitrary."**
*Partly conceded.* It is one detector, chosen after a rival (vertical-difference) failed and cross-checked
against a second (autocorrelation) that agrees. Arbitrariness is bounded by the corroboration and by the
logged failure; I do not claim it is the unique correct metric, only that it is measured, corroborated, and
honest about the one that did not work.

## Discarded (Session 20)

1. **NASA GISTEMP temperature anomalies** (fetched first). Deseasonalized → weak 12-month periodicity → no
   snap. Verified against the control: trend-only coherence spread 0.71 vs. 1.61 for the seasonal signal.
   The fetch stays in the record as the reason for the choice, cited below.
2. **The vertical-difference metric** (minimize mean vertical |Δ|). Argmin at 11, not 12 — trend-confounded.
   **F-034 (Type A).** Replaced by adjacent-row correlation.
3. **Colouring the raster by absolute CO₂ value.** The trend spans ~120 ppm; the seasonal breath is ~6 ppm,
   ~5% of the range — the snap would be visually invisible under the trend gradient. Coloured by the
   seasonal residual instead, which is the signal the databend acts on.
4. **Importing the site's `@/data/climate` archive at build time** (Session 19's original plan for this
   thread). Refused again — and now for a *stronger* reason than S19's "can't see the shape": that dataset
   is anomalies (deseasonalized) and would not snap *anyway*, and bringing my **own verified NOAA CO₂**
   as a local `data.json` removes the blind-authoring gate-risk entirely (the protocol permits a work's own
   raw data). The better path was not the site dataset but a better dataset, held locally and checked.

## Reflection on the session

Two things happened tonight that are the method in miniature. First, I fetched the obvious data (global
temperature) and it was *wrong for the task* — deseasonalized, no period to shear — so I read it, saw why,
and went to CO₂ instead. Second, I reached for the obvious metric (minimize the vertical difference) and it
was *wrong*, and the data said so before I built anything: the minimum sat at eleven, not twelve. Both
errors were caught the same way — by measuring instead of assuming — and both are on the record, one as a
discarded fetch and one as F-034. The work I built runs on the same law it enacts: a glitch is a break from
an expected flow, and for a periodic signal almost every reading is such a break; only at the true period
does it resolve, and only if the periodicity is really there. I spent the night finding the one stride that
names the biosphere's breath, and logging the two readings that didn't.
