The test was not against the practical shift story (auto-steer, readiness, transformations —
that was conceded pre-empted from initiation). It was against the one semantic claim T-002 had
left standing: that 2026 makes "wrong" newly sayable by re-anchoring the US frame to a physical
referent instead of a monument.

Two things came back, and the second is the one that matters.

First: the distinction is textbook. Geodesy already has a name for exactly this — *definition
versus realization*. A reference system (ITRS) is defined as geocentric; a frame (an ITRF) is
the realization that approximates it with residual error; there is no single ground-truth frame,
only better realizations of the same physical definition. The distinction survives the
counterposition test (T-002) but not the pre-emption test as a contribution: it is first-chapter
geodesy.

Second, and the honest sting: the pre-emption reading corrected the project's own T-002 sharpening.
NAD 83 was *intended to be geocentric* (GRS 80 ellipsoid, geocentric origin) and missed the
geocenter by ~2.2 m with 1980s technology — verified at source (gisgeography, corroborated by
NGS's own "non-geocentric by about 2.2 meters"). So NAD 83 was never a convention-object; it
already aimed at the physical target and missed. The 2026 change is a better shot at the same
target, not a change from target-free to target-bound. The two agencies' opposite vocabularies
don't track convention-vs-referent (as T-002 said); they track two different transitions — USGS's
"not wrong, just different" is about NAD 27 → NAD 83, where NAD 27 genuinely was a non-geocentric
convention, and USGS is exactly right there; NGS's "misaligned/biased/tilted" is about NAD 83 →
modernized, a realization that aimed and missed, and "wrong" is sayable there. Both correct;
different frames. The clean statement is not "convention becomes referent in 2026" but "'wrong'
is sayable exactly when a frame aims at a physical target — and NAD 83 already did."

That correction is registered in TRACE T-003 next to the T-002 claim it corrects, not over it.

What this leaves: one un-pre-empted kernel — non-fit 1's first half, error as *relocated, not
removed*: the correction conserves error and moves it from the shared frame origin (bounded,
invisible in use) into every particular archived coordinate (live where a number meets the
world). No one in the discourse frames it that way; they say "coordinates change, apply a
transformation." Whether that reading survives the §5.4 non-replaceability test — or reduces to
exactly "the numbers change and you run a transformation" — is now the single deciding question,
and its honest default is doubt. That test is the next operation and most likely the project's
Judge point. Op #3 (time-dependence at the Blueprint, non-fit 2) remains separately owed.
