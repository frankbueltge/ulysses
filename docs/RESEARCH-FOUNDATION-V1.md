# Ulysses Research Foundation v1

## Status

This document anchors the active protocol in the completed five-tranche Research Foundation. It is intentionally concise. The full source dossiers, position maps, casebooks, requirements and bibliography remain separate research records.

## Integrated proposition

> Ulysses is a situated, human-accountable and machine-operative artistic research practice organised through bounded delegation. It constructs materially and conceptually specific problems; maintains enough determination for traces and differences to matter; leaves consequential relations open to resistance; and composes forms through which changes in objects, apparatuses and possible actions become perceptible, contestable and publicly answerable.

## Theoretical constellation

The protocol is derived from a conflict-aware constellation rather than one sovereign theory:

- debates on research through art, practice as inquiry and exposition;
- Mersch’s aesthetic epistemology and critiques of methodological and institutional imitation;
- Rheinberger’s experimental systems and epistemic things;
- transposition as a situated operation that changes what moves;
- Deleuzian difference, repetition, event, assemblage and cartography;
- Derridean trace and iterability;
- Simondonian individuation and Baradian apparatus and agential cuts;
- situated knowledge and relational agency;
- histories and critiques of machine authorship and computational creativity;
- feminist, decolonial, Indigenous, labour, data and infrastructure correctives.

The constellation remains revisable. No concept functions as a universal method.

## Operational consequences

1. The project is the unit of practice.
2. Artistic and research registers remain coupled but non-identical.
3. Underdetermination must be controlled rather than simulated through random output.
4. Work must encounter material, external or formal resistance.
5. Claims remain typed, limited and correctable.
6. Aesthetic form must be materially specific and non-replaceable by explanation.
7. Machine agency is real but bounded, distributed and asymmetrical.
8. Human responsibility, rights and affected publics remain explicit.
9. Maps are local and disclose their lens and exclusions.
10. Publication is a human judgement, not a pipeline consequence.

## Foundation records

The complete Foundation package is the normative interpretation source for Protocol v4. Where this short note and the complete Foundation conflict, the later dated human-approved decision governs and the conflict must be documented rather than silently resolved.
