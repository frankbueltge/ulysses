# Casebook
## Machine-participatory artistic practices

## 1. Purpose

The cases below are not models to copy. They are used to compare how agency, authorship, data, labour, embodiment and public framing are organised in actual practices.

Each case is read through six questions:

1. What is the machine’s role?
2. Where does the data come from?
3. What remains under human control?
4. What changes through the relation?
5. How is authorship framed?
6. What should Ulysses learn or avoid?

## 2. Harold Cohen and AARON

### Arrangement

AARON was developed by Harold Cohen over decades, not deployed as a generic service. The system and Cohen’s artistic questions co-evolved. Its rules, drawing knowledge and material outputs changed through sustained artistic and technical work.

### Machine role

- custom generative system;
- technical object and long-duration research partner;
- producer of drawings within Cohen’s evolving formal world.

### Human role

- system designer and programmer;
- artist who constructed the visual problem;
- interpreter of system behaviour;
- selector of materials, display and historical framing.

### Strong lesson

The interesting object is not a single “autonomous” drawing. It is the decades-long relation through which a system, an artist and a theory of drawing were transformed.

### Ulysses correction

Autonomy should be evaluated through the practice’s history and specific delegated capacities, not through a one-off output. A generic model runtime with a persona is not equivalent to AARON’s long co-development.

## 3. Anna Ridler — *Myriad (Tulips)* and *Mosaic Virus*

### Arrangement

Ridler photographed and manually labelled ten thousand tulips, displayed the dataset as a material installation and used it to train a generative model whose output was linked to the fluctuating price of Bitcoin.

### Machine role

- generative transformation inside a larger authored system;
- one component connecting archive, speculation, disease and image change.

### Data relation

The dataset is not invisible fuel. Its collection, labour, classification and physical form are part of the work.

### Strong lesson

Dataset authorship can itself be aesthetic and epistemic. Building a smaller, situated corpus can expose what large scraped datasets conceal.

### Ulysses correction

Where data is load-bearing, the project should treat collection and annotation as form, not only provenance documentation. The use of general-purpose models needs an explicit reason when a project-controlled corpus would materially strengthen the inquiry.

## 4. Sougwen Chung — Drawing Operations Unit

### Arrangement

Chung’s multi-generational D.O.U.G. systems draw with the artist in embodied performance. Different generations explore mimicry, memory, collectivity, biofeedback and spatiality. The systems use custom robotic and machine-learning components and, in some versions, the artist’s historical drawing data or present bodily signals.

### Machine role

- kinetic instrument;
- responsive performer;
- generator of marks and temporal resistance;
- participant in a feedback loop.

### Human role

- painter, performer, system designer and trainer;
- bodily participant who responds to machine movement in real time;
- author of the long research trajectory.

### Strong lesson

Agency becomes legible through reciprocal timing and embodied consequence. The machine is neither a hidden generator nor a narrative persona; its material action changes what the human can do next.

### Ulysses correction

A claim of collaboration should identify a feedback loop in which machine action changes human action and vice versa. Mere iterative prompting does not automatically establish collaboration.

## 5. Holly Herndon, Mat Dryhurst and Spawn / *PROTO*

### Arrangement

Spawn was trained through a deliberately constructed vocal corpus involving Herndon, Dryhurst, developers, an ensemble and public training ceremonies. The project personified the model as a child while also foregrounding provenance, collective vocal labour and platform politics.

### Machine role

- custom vocal model;
- compositional generator;
- research figure for questions of training, imitation and identity.

### Human and collective role

- bespoke data creation;
- choral performance and teaching;
- software development;
- curation and album composition;
- strategic public framing.

### Strong lesson

Personification can be critically productive when the work simultaneously exposes the human activity and care that “raises” the system.

### Risk

The child metaphor can naturalise model development, imply innocence or social reciprocity, and conceal asymmetry if detached from the disclosed ensemble and technical labour.

### Ulysses correction

The Ulysses persona can remain only if the apparatus is equally visible. The persona must not replace contribution records or make changing runtime instances appear naturally continuous.

## 6. Stephanie Dinkins — *Not the Only One*

### Arrangement

Dinkins developed a voice-interactive AI memoir from multigenerational family material and framed it as a culturally situated experiment addressing the underrepresentation of Black and brown communities in technology.

### Machine role

- conversational interface;
- evolving container and transformer of family narrative;
- object through which bias, memory and representation are tested.

### Community relation

The work is grounded in a specific family and broader questions of community accountability, rather than a universal model of identity.

### Strong lesson

Culturally situated AI cannot be evaluated only by technical performance. Representation, relation, training material and accountability are part of the work’s success and failure.

### Ulysses correction

When a project uses community stories or identities, it needs more than citation. Authority, consent, ongoing relation and correction must alter the apparatus.

## 7. Memo Akten — *Learning to See*

### Arrangement

*Learning to See* uses neural networks trained on constrained visual categories and applies them to a live camera feed. Ordinary materials are transformed into what the model has learned to see.

### Machine role

- perceptual and classificatory apparatus;
- live transformer;
- materialisation of a learned visual vocabulary.

### Strong lesson

The work does not merely state that models are biased. It makes the model’s training world perceptible as an active visual condition.

### Ulysses correction

An apparatus critique is strongest when the classification system’s learned limits organise the viewer’s experience, not when bias is explained in an accompanying essay or dashboard.

## 8. Artist-guided neural-network practices

The 2024 collection *Artificial Intelligence — Intelligent Art?* documents practices in which artists adapt, train and repurpose models across image, text, form and translation. The strongest examples do not ask whether AI is independently creative. They ask how technical limitations, datasets, interfaces and production processes alter artistic practice.

### Strong lesson

The artist’s role may move from direct fabrication toward corpus construction, system design, interface design, parameterisation, interpretation and public composition.

### Ulysses correction

This expanded role must be documented precisely. “The AI made it” and “the human made it” are both too coarse.

## 9. Negative institutional case — Stephen Thaler and *A Recent Entrance to Paradise*

### Arrangement

Thaler sought copyright registration for an image attributed to a “Creativity Machine” and named the machine as author, claiming ownership as the machine’s owner. The U.S. Copyright Office and subsequent courts rejected registration under the human-authorship requirement.

### Why it matters

The case does not decide whether the image is art. It exposes the institutional weakness of treating machine causation as sufficient for authorship.

### Ulysses correction

Do not design the public identity around a maximal autonomy claim that conflicts with the actual chain of concept formation, system configuration, selection and publication. Legal authorship is only one register, but it usefully tests whether the project’s attribution model can survive external scrutiny.

## 10. Current Ulysses as a case

### Existing strengths

- the protocol already discloses scheduler, model instance, tools, repository memory, human roles and site gate;
- errors, sources and corrections remain visible;
- the archive provides real longitudinal material;
- some works have allowed source evidence to overturn earlier model interpretations.

### Existing weaknesses

- top-level public language still describes a singular autonomous machine artist;
- nightly scheduling is treated as the practice’s natural rhythm;
- sessions are framed as unedited autonomous research despite human-authored constitutional conditions;
- `ulysses/*` branches auto-land without a work-level judgment gate;
- routine website integration turns operational completion into public visibility;
- default Astro output and artefact expectations overdetermine form;
- model provider and version are intentionally unnamed, reducing apparatus precision;
- hidden labour, source-corpus limits, resource use and affected publics are under-described.

### Case conclusion

Ulysses has already produced a valuable archive of configured machine practice. Its next phase should stop claiming that the archive proves an autonomous artificial artist. The stronger work is the changing apparatus itself and the concrete projects it can now construct under a more honest model.

## 11. Cross-case comparison

| Case | Machine role | Data relation | Human relation | Main lesson |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AARON | custom generative system | encoded and artist-developed | decades-long co-development | autonomy is historical and relational |
| Ridler | generative transformer | self-built dataset is visible work | collection, classification, composition | data labour can be form |
| Chung | embodied responsive instrument | artist history and biofeedback | real-time reciprocal performance | agency needs consequence and feedback |
| Herndon/Spawn | custom vocal model and persona | bespoke communal corpus | ensemble, developers, composition | personification requires provenance |
| Dinkins | conversational cultural memoir | family and community material | situated accountability | representation requires relation |
| Akten | perceptual classifier | constrained visual training sets | live viewer/material encounter | learned world becomes aesthetic apparatus |
| Thaler | claimed machine author | system output | ownership claim after generation | causation is not sufficient authorship |
| Ulysses v3 | LLM research persona and runtime | web/repo corpus | human constitution, curation and publishing | public autonomy claim exceeds actual governance |
