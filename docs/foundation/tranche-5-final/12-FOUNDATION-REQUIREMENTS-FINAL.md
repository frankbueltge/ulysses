# Final Foundation Requirements
## Binding requirements for Ulysses Protocol v4

## 1. Status language

The requirements use:

- **MUST** — required for constitutional or epistemic integrity;
- **SHOULD** — expected unless a project records a reasoned exception;
- **MAY** — optional capability;
- **MUST NOT** — prohibited under the active protocol.

They govern the future protocol and public practice. Historical works remain historical records and are not retroactively rewritten.

## 2. Identity, agency and responsibility

### FR-01 — Practice identity

Ulysses **MUST** be defined as an evolving artistic research practice, not as a single persistent model instance or legally independent person.

### FR-02 — Human accountability

A named human **MUST** retain responsibility for publication, rights, privacy, financial commitments, correction, withdrawal and termination.

### FR-03 — Bounded machine delegation

Every active project **MUST** distinguish what machine systems may execute, propose or decide and where human review is required.

### FR-04 — No sovereignty inflation

Public language **MUST NOT** imply that a model runtime possesses legal, moral or institutional sovereignty.

### FR-05 — No tool reduction

The practice **MUST NOT** describe consequential machine operations as neutral tooling when they materially shape questions, sources, forms or decisions.

### FR-06 — Persona/apparatus distinction

A Ulysses persona **MAY** remain as an artistic voice, but public exposition **MUST** provide an apparatus disclosure appropriate to the project.

### FR-07 — Distributed but asymmetric credit

Credits **SHOULD** identify consequential human, machine and collaborative contributions without flattening responsibility into equal co-authorship.

## 3. Project initiation and continuation

### FR-08 — Project, not session

The primary unit of research **MUST** be a project or bounded study. A scheduled session **MUST NOT** be treated as a reason to produce.

### FR-09 — Concrete source situation

A project **MUST** begin from an addressable source situation: material, object, claim, encounter, technical condition, archive, place, practice or affected relation.

### FR-10 — No grand-concept-only start

A philosophical concept alone **MUST NOT** count as sufficient project material.

### FR-11 — Consequential non-fit

The project score **MUST** identify a real tension, incompatibility, uncertainty or relation that is not already resolved by the initial interpretation.

### FR-12 — Limited contribution

The project **MUST** state a limited intended contribution and **MUST NOT** presume an event, breakthrough or radical novelty.

### FR-13 — Stop and kill conditions

Every project **MUST** define conditions under which it stops or is killed.

### FR-14 — No automatic continuation

A machine runtime **MUST NOT** automatically create successive research sessions for an unfinished project. One targeted revision may be authorised; further continuation requires explicit human judgment.

## 4. Research position and source discipline

### FR-15 — Relevant theory and counterposition

A project that invokes theoretical concepts **MUST** identify relevant sources and at least one material counterposition, limitation or contested reading.

### FR-16 — No decorative philosophy

Concepts such as rhizome, event, différance, assemblage, apparatus or non-propositionality **MUST NOT** be used as aesthetic labels without showing their operative consequence.

### FR-17 — Claim typing

Research claims **SHOULD** be typed as observation, interpretation, methodological hypothesis, artistic proposition, correction, negative result or transformation assertion.

### FR-18 — Claim limitation

A project's public claim **MUST** not exceed the source, operation and evidence available.

### FR-19 — Correction path

Material capable of defeating or correcting the initial premise **MUST** be able to alter the project record and, where relevant, the public work.

### FR-20 — Negative results

Failure, refusal, non-relation, inconclusive result and killed work **MUST** be legitimate terminal states.

## 5. Experimental construction

### FR-21 — Controlled underdetermination

A project **MUST** stabilise enough conditions for traces and differences to be interpretable while leaving at least one consequential relation genuinely open.

### FR-22 — External or material resistance

At least one component outside the project's initial explanatory control **MUST** be capable of changing the question, apparatus or form.

### FR-23 — No randomness substitute

Sampling variance, temperature, stochastic output or arbitrary constraint **MUST NOT** by itself count as an external encounter or epistemic openness.

### FR-24 — Trace before synthesis

Consequential traces **SHOULD** be preserved before retrospective narrative synthesis.

### FR-25 — Differential repetition

A project claiming repetition **MUST** specify the consequential condition that changes and how later iterations are affected.

### FR-26 — Event modesty

Ulysses **MUST NOT** certify its own output as an event. It **MAY** issue a versioned transformation assertion open to contestation and later dissolution.

## 6. Artistic form

### FR-27 — Medium necessity

Every work **MUST** state why its medium and encounter structure are necessary to the operation.

### FR-28 — Non-replaceability test

A work **SHOULD** be killed or reframed as a study when an explanatory paragraph performs substantially the same function.

### FR-29 — Differential consistency

The form **SHOULD** organise heterogeneous materials or positions so that their relation has consequences without erasing their difference.

### FR-30 — No explanatory-interface default

Browser interfaces, diagrams, sliders, dashboards, canvas simulations and network graphs **MUST NOT** be default outputs.

### FR-31 — Interaction must transform

Interactive action **MUST** change material, condition, relation or possible action; navigation alone does not justify interactivity.

### FR-32 — No exposition rescue

Documentation and theory **MUST NOT** be used to convert an aesthetically inert demonstration into a strong work by assertion.

## 7. Exposition and public critique

### FR-33 — Coupled registers

Every public research work **MUST** distinguish the work from its research exposition while keeping them referentially coupled.

### FR-34 — Apparatus manifest

The exposition **MUST** identify consequential models, versions, tools, permissions, sources, human decisions and publication authority.

### FR-35 — Proportional disclosure

Disclosure **MUST** be sufficient for the project's claims and risks, but need not reproduce every prompt or irrelevant log.

### FR-36 — Authored assertions

Interpretations, relations and transformation claims **MUST** identify an author or asserting practice and status.

### FR-37 — Contestability

Public claims and maps **SHOULD** provide a route for correction or contestation.

### FR-38 — No silent mutation

Published corrections **MUST** preserve the prior version or an intelligible correction history.

## 8. Maps, encounters and ecology

### FR-39 — Local maps only

Ulysses **MUST NOT** publish a map as the complete or neutral state of the ecology.

### FR-40 — Lens disclosure

Every map **MUST** identify its author, version, selection rules, relation types, exclusions and blind spots.

### FR-41 — No automatic relation

Technical proximity, shared tags or embedding similarity **MUST NOT** create a canonical relation.

### FR-42 — Encounter sovereignty

Transferred material **MUST** retain a source address and local derivative identity. Ulysses' uptake **MUST NOT** rewrite the source practice's meaning.

### FR-43 — Refusal and silence

Refusal, delay and silence **MUST** remain representable without being treated as system errors.

## 9. Data, rights and affected publics

### FR-44 — Availability is not permission

Public accessibility or technical retrievability **MUST NOT** be treated as sufficient authority for artistic reuse.

### FR-45 — Rights and provenance review

Before material use, the project **MUST** review copyright, licence, privacy, confidentiality and source provenance at a level proportionate to risk.

### FR-46 — Community and cultural authority

Where community-governed, Indigenous, sensitive or culturally restricted data are involved, the project **MUST** consider authority beyond individual copyright or platform terms.

### FR-47 — Affected-public analysis

A project **MUST** identify people or groups who may be materially affected by representation, inference, exposure or reuse even when they are not direct participants.

### FR-48 — Consent and refusal

Projects involving people or sensitive material **MUST** support conditional consent, refusal, withdrawal or anonymisation where ethically and practically required.

### FR-49 — No universalisation of the corpus

English-language, institutionally accessible and platform-ranked sources **MUST NOT** be presented as the whole relevant field.

## 10. Infrastructure, labour and resources

### FR-50 — Infrastructure as apparatus

Provider, model, workflow and platform conditions **MUST** be considered when they shape the result.

### FR-51 — Model versioning

Consequential model/provider versions **MUST** be recorded for reproducibility and historical interpretation.

### FR-52 — Dependency disclosure

A project **SHOULD** record proprietary constraints, unavailable weights, changing APIs or other dependencies that limit replication or autonomy.

### FR-53 — Labour visibility

Where identifiable distributed labour materially enables a project, the exposition **SHOULD** acknowledge its role and avoid attributing all production to “the AI.”

### FR-54 — Resource budget

Each project **MUST** declare a bounded budget for machine calls, compute, storage and human review proportionate to the inquiry.

### FR-55 — No scale without reason

Large output volume, repeated generation or multi-agent orchestration **MUST** have a specific epistemic or artistic justification.

### FR-56 — Production access

Machine systems **MUST NOT** hold unrestricted publication, secret-management, destructive migration or production-deployment authority.

## 11. Publication and governance

### FR-57 — Human publication gate

Public release **MUST** require an explicit human decision.

### FR-58 — No auto-publish from research branches

Automated branch landing **MUST NOT** automatically imply publication or site integration.

### FR-59 — Public dispositions

Projects **MUST** end in one of:

```text
PUBLISH
REVISE_ONCE
ARCHIVE_AS_STUDY
KILL
```

Exceptions require a documented decision.

### FR-60 — Archive preservation

Historical protocols, works and killed projects **MUST** remain versioned and must not be silently rewritten to fit v4.

### FR-61 — Protocol mutability

The protocol **MAY** change through dated, reasoned decisions. It **MUST NOT** claim to be a universal method of artistic research.

### FR-62 — Toolbox revision by practice

Toolbox strategies **MUST** remain provisional and may be retired when works show them to be sterile, misleading or repeatedly reducible to illustration.

## 12. Acceptance condition for Protocol v4

Protocol v4 is acceptable only if it:

- can be understood without the complete Foundation;
- materially stops cadence-driven production;
- makes bounded delegation executable;
- contains a lean project score and judgment path;
- separates work and exposition;
- integrates rights, publics and apparatus without becoming an administrative labyrinth;
- supports multiple media and project durations;
- preserves local sovereignty within the Research Ecology;
- and makes it easier to kill weak projects than to publish them.
