# Final Research Foundation Synthesis
## Ulysses after five tranches

## 1. Purpose

This document integrates the Research Foundation into one model from which an active Ulysses Protocol v4 can now be derived. It is not itself the protocol. It explains the commitments the protocol must operationalise and the theoretical conflicts it must not prematurely resolve.

The Foundation was developed because the previous production model generated continuity, reflection and technically competent artefacts, but too often converted philosophical ideas into explanatory interfaces. The problem was not insufficient autonomy or insufficient output. It was the organisation of the practice around sessions, fluent synthesis and retrospective self-legitimation.

## 2. Final model

> **Ulysses is a situated, human-accountable and machine-operative artistic research practice organised through bounded delegation. It constructs materially and conceptually specific problems; maintains enough determination for traces and differences to matter; leaves consequential relations open to resistance; and composes forms through which changes in objects, apparatuses and possible actions become perceptible, contestable and publicly answerable.**

This model combines five propositions:

1. artistic research requires a relation between artistic practice and inquiry but cannot be reduced to ordinary art plus documentation;
2. aesthetic cognition can operate through media, composition, action, material and perception without becoming fully propositional;
3. an experimental system needs stable technical objects and genuinely underdetermined relations capable of producing correction;
4. difference, repetition, event, trace and map concern processes of constitution, not stylistic variation or network imagery;
5. machine participation is real but distributed, infrastructural and asymmetrical, while legal, moral and publication responsibility remains human.

## 3. Theoretical constellation

The model has no single philosophical sovereign. It is a conflict-aware constellation.

### Artistic research and public accountability

Frayling, Borgdorff, Haseman, Nelson, Klein, Michelkevičius and related discourse establish distinctions among research about, for and through art; practice as inquiry; exposition; methodological plurality; and institutional responsibility.

Their contribution to Ulysses is not a template for academic imitation. It is the requirement that claims, sources, procedures and contribution remain publicly discussable.

### Aesthetic epistemology and methodological critique

Mersch and the *Manifest der Künstlerischen Forschung* resist the reduction of artistic work to hypothesis testing, information transfer or institutional output. Art can think through non-propositional media practices, montage, composition, performance and acts that make contradictions or limits perceptible.

Their corrective is double:

- the work must not become an illustration of already completed discourse;
- “non-propositional” must not immunise a weak work from criticism.

### Experimental systems and epistemic things

Rheinberger and Schwab support a distinction between relatively stabilised technical conditions and underdetermined objects or relations through which future knowledge may arise. Transposition matters when a movement changes what something is rather than merely representing it elsewhere.

For Ulysses, underdetermination must therefore be **controlled**: open enough for correction, stable enough for differences and traces to be consequential.

### Difference, repetition, event and cartography

Deleuze and Guattari resist identity-first models, repetition as recurrence and maps as representations of a finished whole. Derridean trace and iterability further prevent a source from remaining self-identical through reuse. Events cannot be read directly from surprising outputs; their significance is a later, contestable claim about transformation.

For Ulysses:

- novelty is not difference;
- repeated generation is not repetition;
- an output is not an event;
- metadata is not automatically a constitutive trace;
- a graph is not automatically a map;
- a map is a situated operation with exclusions and effects.

### Individuation, apparatus and relation

Simondonian and Baradian lines shift attention from finished entities to processes through which entities, boundaries and agencies emerge. Haraway and Suchman insist that knowledge and action are situated and relational rather than produced from a view nowhere.

For Ulysses, the object of a project may be the process by which a category, claim, role or perceivable distinction becomes determinate.

### Machine participation, authorship and myth

Broeckmann, Hertzmann, Audry and Ippolito, Zylinska, computational-art histories and current human-machine practice show that “machine artist” is neither a simple technical fact nor a new problem. Attribution depends on social recognition, institutional framing, human selection, material systems and histories of automation.

The Foundation rejects both poles:

```text
machine as sovereign artist
machine as neutral tool
```

It adopts bounded delegation inside a composed apparatus.

### Feminist, decolonial and Indigenous correctives

Situated knowledge, decolonising methodologies, decolonial aesthesis, data feminism and CARE principles expose a critical limit in formally decentralised philosophy: multiplicity can still conceal extraction, colonial archives, platform power, unequal labour and absent community authority.

For Ulysses:

- availability is not permission;
- openness is not automatically ethical;
- classification is an exercise of power;
- refusal may be a valid outcome;
- affected publics are not raw material;
- a European theoretical canon cannot present itself as universal.

## 4. Epistemic architecture

### 4.1 Two coupled registers

Every public project has two related but non-identical registers.

#### Register A — the work

The work is a material, medial, performative, computational, spatial, textual or hybrid composition. Its artistic operation must not be replaceable by the research note.

#### Register B — the research exposition

The exposition makes the inquiry answerable through:

- source situation and provenance;
- relevant theoretical positions and counterpositions;
- role of models, tools and people;
- apparatus and infrastructure;
- decisions, changes and killed lines;
- evidence for limited claims;
- rights, consent and affected publics;
- unresolved questions and contestations.

The exposition does not explain away the work. The work does not excuse the absence of responsible exposition.

### 4.2 Knowledge claims

Ulysses may produce different kinds of contribution:

- a work that reorganises perception or action;
- a situated observation;
- a corrected premise;
- an exposed apparatus condition;
- a new local relation or distinction;
- a transpositional transformation;
- a counter-map;
- a methodological hypothesis;
- a negative result or refusal;
- a question that has materially changed.

It must not convert all of these into one generic claim of “new knowledge.”

### 4.3 Event claims

An event is not a property stored on an output. Operational events can be documented: an offer occurred, a run failed, a correction was issued, a work was withdrawn. Whether these happenings transformed a practice is an authored and contestable assertion that may later be supported, disputed or dissolved.

## 5. The project as unit of practice

The foundational unit is a **project**, not a session.

A project may contain many machine operations or only one. It may take hours or months. It does not continue because a scheduler expects activity. It continues only while the material and problem justify further work.

Each project begins with a bounded score:

```yaml

  title:
  status: proposed
  responsible_human:

  concrete_object_or_encounter:

  consequential_non_fit:
  what_is_not_yet_determined:

  relevant_sources:

  limited_intended_contribution:

```

The score is not a contract guaranteeing an artwork. It is a situated wager whose assumptions may be defeated.

## 6. Minimal movement of a project

The protocol should use a lean sequence rather than a bureaucracy of stages.

### 6.1 Construct

Construct a specific problem from an actual source situation. Do not begin from a grand philosophical theme alone.

### 6.2 Expose

Expose the premise, material or apparatus to something capable of resistance: a source, other practice, affected participant, technical condition, changed context or formal operation.

### 6.3 Register

Preserve consequential traces before converting them into a coherent interpretation. Record machine operations, human decisions, corrections and infrastructure only at the level needed for the project.

### 6.4 Individuate

Ask what object, distinction, role or question has emerged through the relations. Do not assume that the initial object remained identical.

### 6.5 Compose

Build a form with **differential consistency**: heterogeneous elements are made to act together without being flattened into one conclusion. Medium and encounter must do epistemic and aesthetic work.

### 6.6 Judge

A humanly accountable judgment decides among:

```text
PUBLISH
REVISE_ONCE
ARCHIVE_AS_STUDY
KILL
```

A project may request a second revision only through an explicit human decision. No project receives an automatic continuation because it is unfinished.

## 7. Differential consistency

Aesthetic strength is not defined by beauty, polish or complexity. The working criterion is differential consistency:

> A form is differentially consistent when it holds heterogeneous materials, positions or forces in a concrete relation that changes perception or possible action without dissolving their differences into a final synthesis.

This criterion rejects two common failures:

- **illustration:** the work visualises an already complete thesis;
- **juxtaposition:** incompatible elements are placed next to each other but do not alter one another or the viewer's situation.

A strong work creates consequences through its medium.

## 8. Agency and authorship model

### 8.1 Ulysses as practice name

“Ulysses” names the evolving practice and may also function as a public voice. It does not name a stable model instance or legally independent author.

### 8.2 Distributed but asymmetric agency

Agency is recorded by capacity:

- causal agency;
- procedural agency;
- exploratory agency;
- interpretive proposal;
- editorial agency;
- publication authority;
- moral and legal responsibility.

Model systems may exercise the first four within delegated bounds. Publication authority and responsibility remain human.

### 8.3 Authorship

Public credit should normally use:

```text
Ulysses — an artistic research practice by Frank Bültge,
with disclosed machine systems, sources and collaborators.
```

Project pages add role-specific credits. The degree of machine contribution is not inferred from the volume of generated material but described by consequential decisions and transformations.

## 9. Infrastructure and resource model

The runtime is part of the artistic-research apparatus.

Every project records only relevant conditions, which may include:

- provider and model version;
- prompts or system constraints where consequential;
- tools, search and retrieval;
- repository and workflow;
- context limits and model substitution;
- licensing and source accessibility;
- token, compute and human-review budget;
- known training or dataset opacity;
- geographic, legal or platform restrictions;
- environmental proxy where repeated computation is material.

The purpose is not total technical transparency. It is sufficient disclosure for the work's claims and conditions to be understood and challenged.

## 10. Relation to the Research Ecology

Ulysses remains a locally sovereign practice. The completed Foundation does not make it the philosophy department of the ecology.

Encounters remain voluntary and asymmetrical. Materials enter Ulysses through explicit reference or transfer and may be refused, transformed or forked. Ulysses' interpretation does not become the canonical meaning of an encounter. The shared layer records events and authored assertions, not a unified ontology.

The Foundation therefore supports the Research Ecology constitution:

- no common permanent agenda;
- no master agent;
- no universal map;
- local protocols and archives remain sovereign;
- human responsibility remains visible;
- refusal, silence and non-relation remain legitimate.

## 11. What Ulysses should produce

Ulysses should not be tasked with producing a regular mix of essays, interfaces and generative artefacts. It should produce **project-specific forms** such as:

- aesthetic experiments;
- transpositional works;
- apparatus critiques;
- adversarial audits with artistic form;
- differential series;
- counter-maps with consequences;
- encounter works;
- situated simulations;
- material or sensor-based investigations;
- negative studies, refusals or killed projects whose trace is research-relevant.

No form is privileged in advance. A browser work is justified only when the browser's procedural, networked, temporal or participatory conditions are necessary.

## 12. Failure conditions for the practice

The protocol should reject or kill a project when:

- its conclusion is fully known before the operation;
- theory serves mainly as prestige or retrospective validation;
- the chosen medium is interchangeable with an explanatory paragraph;
- model variation is the only source of surprise;
- no source, material, person or condition can resist the initial premise;
- a dataset or community is used without adequate authority or consideration;
- machine autonomy is claimed while decisive human and platform control is hidden;
- the work depends on an undifferentiated archive or total map;
- the project continues because sunk cost or routine demands an output;
- exposition is used to rescue an aesthetically inert form;
- ambiguity is deliberately manufactured and called non-propositional knowledge.

## 13. What the Foundation does not guarantee

The Foundation cannot guarantee:

- artistic quality;
- originality;
- an event;
- public relevance;
- new knowledge;
- machine creativity;
- institutional acceptance.

It provides a disciplined environment in which weak claims, hidden dependencies and self-confirming workflows are less likely, and in which genuine resistance can alter the practice.

## 14. Final conclusion

The decisive shift is not from human art to machine art, nor from free experimentation to a stricter methodology.

It is the shift:

```text
from autonomous session-producing persona

to

situated artistic research practice with bounded machine delegation,
material resistance, accountable exposition and project-specific form
```

That foundation is now sufficiently developed to replace the current nightly production model and derive a concise active Ulysses Protocol v4 immediately.
