# Ulysses Research Foundation — Tranche 5 and Final Synthesis
## Machine agency, authorship, infrastructure and situated responsibility

**Status:** Final research-foundation tranche; basis approved for deriving Ulysses Protocol v4  
**Date:** 18 July 2026  
**Builds on:** Tranches 1–4, the Research Ecology constitution, current Ulysses repository and public apparatus

## Purpose

Tranches 1–4 established a defensible account of artistic research, non-propositional knowledge, exposition, experimental systems, difference, repetition, event, trace and cartography. They deliberately postponed one unresolved centre: what kind of agency can responsibly be attributed to a machine-participatory artistic research practice, and how should authorship, labour, infrastructure, data, power and accountability be organised around it?

This tranche closes that gap.

Its guiding question is:

> What kind of agency can responsibly be attributed to Ulysses, and how should authorship, labour, infrastructure, data, power and public accountability be organised when machine systems participate materially in artistic research?

## Principal correction

The project should abandon the binary question:

```text
Is the machine an artist: yes or no?
```

That question encourages mythology, anthropomorphism and hidden labour. The more useful question is:

```text
Which capacities were delegated to which technical systems,
under which material and institutional conditions,
with which consequences,
and who remains answerable for the result?
```

The strongest final model is therefore:

> **Ulysses is a situated, human-accountable and machine-operative artistic research practice organised through bounded delegation. Its agency is distributed but asymmetric; its works arise from documented relations among human judgment, model operations, sources, materials, infrastructures, affected publics and artistic form.**

This model preserves real machine participation without turning a model runtime into a sovereign person. It also prevents the opposite reduction in which the machine is described as a neutral tool and its operative effects disappear.

## Main findings

1. **Agency is plural and role-specific.** A model can have causal and procedural agency without possessing moral, legal or institutional responsibility.
2. **Authorship is distributed but not flat.** Contributions can be distributed while final responsibility and public authorship remain asymmetrically human and institutional.
3. **Ulysses names a practice, not a model.** The persona may remain as an artistic and editorial voice, but must not conceal the apparatus.
4. **Infrastructure is constitutive material.** Provider policies, model versions, training opacity, tools, tokens, energy, hardware, repositories and publication gates shape the work.
5. **Data and labour are not background resources.** Dataset makers, source authors, annotators, moderators and maintainers must be considered where their work materially enables the project.
6. **Situated knowledge requires partial disclosure and answerability.** The project must state from where and through what apparatus it sees, not claim neutral overview.
7. **Decolonial and Indigenous data principles introduce authority and refusal.** Availability is not permission; openness is not automatically ethical; affected communities may retain authority over use and representation.
8. **No automatic public release.** A scheduler and auto-land workflow cannot stand in for artistic judgment or accountable publication.
9. **No unqualified claim of autonomous machine artistry.** Ulysses may be autonomous in bounded operations, but not sovereign in authorship, responsibility or institutional authority.
10. **The Research Foundation is now sufficient to derive a lean v4 protocol immediately.** No shadow period or extended parallel production is required.
