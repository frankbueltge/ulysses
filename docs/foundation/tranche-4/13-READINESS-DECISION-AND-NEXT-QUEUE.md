## 2. What is now mature

The Foundation can now defend the following positions with reasonable theoretical and operational precision:

- artistic research is neither ordinary art practice plus documentation nor scientific research with aesthetic presentation;
- aesthetic and research registers must remain coupled but distinguishable;
- underdetermination must be controlled enough for traces and differences to matter;
- difference is not synonymous with novelty or variation;
- repetition is not recurrence or parameter sampling;
- evental significance is a contestable transformation claim, not an output property;
- trace and context are constitutive where they alter identity, interpretation or action;
- maps are situated operations rather than total representations;
- graph, model, diagram, map, counter-map and interface require different claims;
- openness and multiplicity require power and exclusion analysis;
- artistic form can be described as differential consistency.

These positions are sufficient to reject many weak proposals and to build a bounded pilot score.

## 3. Why the final protocol is not ready

The model still has an unresolved centre: the status of machine agency.

Without another tranche, the project could still:

- treat a model runtime as a coherent nonhuman actor;
- obscure training data and distributed human labour;
- understate provider and platform control;
- reproduce a European philosophical canon as if universal;
- invoke posthumanism without affected publics or material accountability;
- describe human curation as occasional when it is structurally decisive;
- ignore energy, hardware and maintenance as part of the apparatus;
- confuse automated execution with artistic initiative.

## 4. Tranche 5

**Machine agency, authorship, infrastructure and situated responsibility**

### Guiding question

> What kind of agency can responsibly be attributed to Ulysses, and how should authorship, labour, infrastructure, data, power and public accountability be organised when machine systems participate materially in artistic research?

## 5. Pilot policy before Tranche 5

One pilot may be designed, but only under these restrictions:

- no public claim of autonomous machine artistry;
- no automatic publication;
- no nightly sequence;
- one concrete source situation;
- one primary toolbox strategy;
- declared human and machine roles;
- explicit source and rights review;
- review against the Tranche 4 failure tests;
- no requirement to rescue or publish the result.

The purpose of such a pilot would be to test the Foundation's diagnostic value, not to validate Ulysses v4 prematurely.
