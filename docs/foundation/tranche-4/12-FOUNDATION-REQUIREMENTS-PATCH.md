# Foundation Requirements Patch
## Tranche 4 — FR-23 to FR-39

These requirements extend the Foundation requirements from Tranches 1–3. They are conceptual requirements for the final model, not an implementation backlog.

## FR-23 — Difference must be generative or consequential

A project must identify a relation that helps produce or transform an object, category, apparatus or field of action. Output variation alone is insufficient.

## FR-24 — Repetition requires a differential condition

A repeated operation must preserve enough continuity for attribution, alter a load-bearing condition and allow the result to affect a later operation, category or judgment.

Scheduled recurrence is not a research justification.

## FR-25 — Event and occurrence remain distinct

The system must distinguish mechanically recorded events from authored claims that an occurrence transformed a problem or practice.

No practice may certify its own work as an event through a global status or score.

## FR-26 — Transformation assertions are contestable

Claims of changed question, method, object or practice must identify their author, supporting traces, affected objects, limits and alternative readings. They must support contestation, dissolution and supersession.

## FR-27 — Encounter requires possible resistance

An encounter must permit refusal, divergence, silence or material contradiction. Importing an external source is not enough.

## FR-28 — Trace is not synonymous with logging

A project must distinguish documentary records from traces that materially affect identity, interpretation, evidence or action.

## FR-29 — Context and version are active conditions

Where marks, prompts, claims, images, scores or datasets travel, the project must record context and version changes that can alter their force.

## FR-30 — No pure-origin narrative

Research exposition must not imply that a source, dataset or work had a complete identity prior to its technical, historical and institutional conditions.

This does not remove the obligation to cite and preserve source records.

## FR-31 — Map roles must be declared

An artefact must state whether it functions primarily as graph, model, diagram, map, counter-map or interface. Multiple roles may coexist but must not be hidden behind generic cartographic language.

## FR-32 — Every public map requires a lens manifest

The manifest must identify authorship, version, sources, relation types, transformations, filters, known exclusions, blind spots, infrastructure and contestations.

## FR-33 — No canonical aggregate map

The system must not produce a privileged total graph or endpoint combining all local maps and assertions into one authoritative view.

## FR-34 — Non-relation is a legitimate state

Refusal, incompatibility, silence, absent evidence and unknown relation must be representable without automatic conversion into failure, influence or future work.

## FR-35 — Closure scores are prohibited as active quality metrics

No scalar measure may claim to represent the openness, rhizomaticity, health or closure of Ulysses or the ecology.

Historical scores may remain archived as part of the project's development.

## FR-36 — Lines of flight require a capture review

Claims of rupture, decentralisation or escape must identify the new structures, authorities and exclusions produced by the change.

## FR-37 — Aesthetic form must create differential consistency

A public work must hold a specific differential relation active through its medium. It must not rely on explanatory text to manufacture the relation after the fact.

## FR-38 — Political and infrastructural conditions are internal to the apparatus

Where materially relevant, projects must identify provider power, hidden labour, dataset conditions, affected parties, rights, environmental costs and unequal capacities to contest or withdraw.

## FR-39 — The toolbox remains non-canonical

Toolbox strategies are versioned methodological hypotheses. A project may reject them, combine them cautiously or propose another strategy. Theoretical vocabulary must never become a mandatory house style.

## Consolidated acceptance test

A final Ulysses protocol cannot be accepted unless it:

- implements or clearly operationalises FR-01 through FR-39;
- removes mandatory nightly production;
- separates historical archive from active cartographic authority;
- preserves human accountability;
- limits machine self-certification;
- supports local, contestable maps and transformation assertions;
- includes kill and refusal states without narrative rescue;
- makes theory a revisable research resource rather than an automatic legitimating layer.
