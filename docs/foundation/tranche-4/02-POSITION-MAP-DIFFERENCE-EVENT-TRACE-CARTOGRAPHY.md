# Position Map
## Difference, event, trace, iterability and cartography

## 1. Purpose

This map does not reconcile the positions into a single theory. It identifies tensions that Ulysses must preserve when translating them into practice.

## 2. Main axes

### Difference

| Position | Difference is… | Risk for Ulysses |
|---|---|---|
| Representational comparison | a distinction between already identifiable things | producing variant grids and calling them difference |
| Deleuzian genetic difference | the differential relation through which identities are individuated | turning a difficult ontology into a visual effect |
| Derridean différance | spacing and deferral that prevents full self-presence and final contextual closure | using “ambiguity” as poetic decoration |
| Baradian diffraction | patterns of difference produced through apparatus-specific intra-actions | treating every configuration change as politically equivalent |
| Critical cartography | a difference in visibility, authority, classification and possible action | aestheticising power without redistributing it |

### Repetition

| Position | Repetition does… | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| Routine execution | reproduces a procedure on a schedule | nightly activity mistaken for research |
| Statistical sampling | draws outputs from a distribution | model variance mistaken for aesthetic discovery |
| Differential repetition | reactivates a genetic condition and individuates differently | decorative parameter sweeps |
| Iterability | allows a mark to function again while altering its effect in new contexts | assuming the first intention remains sovereign |
| Re-enactment | stages historical or procedural conditions in another present | nostalgia or literal reconstruction |

### Event

| Position | Event means… | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Operational ledger | a documented occurrence such as offer, correction or publication | mechanically verifiable but semantically limited |
| Statistical anomaly | an improbable observation | not automatically meaningful |
| Deleuzian event | a transformation in the problem-field and its possible actualisations | significance is not identical with the physical occurrence |
| Artistic event | a reorganisation of perception, relation or action through form | must remain contestable and historically situated |
| Institutional event | a change in protocol, rights, categories or public consequences | persistence matters more than spectacle |

### Trace

| Position | Trace is… | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Log entry | a recorded state change | necessary but not sufficient |
| Evidence | material supporting or weakening a claim | requires provenance and scope |
| Derridean trace | constitutive relation to what is absent, prior and still to come | no pure origin or context-free identity |
| Artistic remainder | material that exceeds the intended account | cannot be manufactured by vague omission alone |
| Technical residue | latency, compression, loss, error, cost, version drift | becomes research material only when consequential |

### Map and cartography

| Form | Primary operation | Typical misuse |
|---|---|---|
| Graph | formalises nodes and edges | network aesthetics presented as openness |
| Model | selects and organises features for explanation or simulation | hidden assumptions presented as reality |
| Diagram | relays forces, relations or possible transformations | decorative abstraction |
| Map | creates a situated field of orientation and action | totalising overview |
| Counter-map | contests an authoritative distribution of visibility or agency | alternative styling without political consequence |
| Interface | enacts access, sequence, permission and possible action | neutral container rhetoric |

## 3. Productive tensions

### A. Genesis versus non-closure

Deleuze helps Ulysses ask how a new object, relation or problem is generated. Derrida prevents that genesis from becoming a pure origin or complete presence.

Operational consequence:

> A project should identify a differential condition that can produce change while preserving the traces and contextual dependencies that prevent the result from becoming a self-identical origin story.

### B. Event versus operational record

The Research Ecology needs a reliable event ledger, but evental significance cannot be stored as an unquestionable property of a record.

Operational consequence:

```text
operational event = something recorded as having happened
transformation assertion = someone claims that it changed a problem or practice
contestation = someone challenges that claim
```

### C. Openness versus rigor

A map can remain revisable and incomplete while still declaring its lens, sources, exclusions and responsible author. Openness without such declarations becomes obscurity rather than multiplicity.

### D. Multiplicity versus power

A many-node graph may remain centralised through:

- its schema;
- ranking rules;
- data access;
- editorial selection;
- hosting infrastructure;
- the person or model authorised to create edges.

The relevant question is not how many centres are drawn, but which acts of authorisation and exclusion make the map possible.

### E. Form versus explanation

A form can hold heterogeneous relations together without reducing them to a proposition. Yet a work cannot invoke nonpropositionality to avoid explaining its sources, apparatus or public claims.

The resulting two-register model remains valid:

```text
AESTHETIC REGISTER
- the work makes the relation active and perceptible

RESEARCH REGISTER
- the exposition makes conditions, sources, cuts and claims contestable
```

## 4. Rejected equations

```text
novel output = difference
randomness = outside
surprise = event
archive = trace
interactive graph = map
absence of hierarchy in layout = decentralisation
many perspectives = political plurality
opacity = nonpropositional knowledge
model autonomy = nonhuman agency
```

## 5. Interim synthesis

Tranche 4 supports a model in which artistic form is not a container for research content. It is a **differential consistency**: a concrete holding-together of heterogeneous materials and series in which their relations remain active, their identities can shift, and no final synthesis removes the consequential difference.

This gives Ulysses a more precise aesthetic requirement than “the medium must be necessary.” The form must organise a real differential relation and expose what changes when the relation is actualised, repeated or transferred.
