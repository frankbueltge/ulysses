# Source Review and Access Status
## Tranche 4

## 1. Method

This tranche combines four forms of reading:

1. **Primary-text reading** where full texts were available in the project corpus.
2. **Chapter-level reading** where only selected chapters or excerpts were available.
3. **Authoritative secondary reconstruction** where primary texts were unavailable.
4. **Repository and interface audit** of Ulysses' actual technical and public artefacts.

Claims are limited accordingly. No source is treated as fully mastered merely because a searchable extract or summary was available.

## 2. Primary sources read directly

### Gilles Deleuze and Felix Guattari — *A Thousand Plateaus*

**Access:** Full English edition in the project corpus.  
**Sections used most closely:** Introduction: Rhizome; assemblage and abstract-machine passages; territoriality and refrain; smooth/striated space; conclusion on concrete rules and abstract machines.

**Relevant propositions:**

- a map is an experiment in contact with the real, not a reproduction of a pre-existing structure;
- maps are open, reversible, detachable and modifiable;
- multiplicities are defined by relations to an outside and by lines through which they change nature;
- assemblages connect heterogeneous material, semiotic and social flows;
- rupture and deterritorialisation are not automatically emancipatory because they can be followed by reterritorialisation and capture;
- consistency means holding heterogeneous elements together without reducing them to homogeneity.

**Limit:** *A Thousand Plateaus* is not a direct methodology manual for artistic research. Its concepts cannot be converted into software features without loss and distortion.

### Jacques Derrida — “Différance”

**Access:** Full English translation consulted.  
**Relevant propositions:**

- différance names neither a stable concept nor a present cause;
- differing and deferring are inseparable;
- signs are constituted through relations and traces rather than positive self-presence;
- no context or origin can provide final semantic mastery;
- the trace is not an accidental residue added after meaning; it participates in the constitution of meaning.

**Limit:** The essay does not provide an artistic method. Any operational translation must remain explicitly interpretive.

### Michael Schwab, ed. — *Transpositions: Aesthetico-Epistemic Operators in Artistic Research*

**Access:** Full volume in the project corpus.  
**Chapters used most closely:** Schwab's introduction; Braidotti; Kirkkopelto; Arlander; Pirrò; Rutz; Schwab; Rheinberger; Malaspina; de Assis; Mersch; Elo.

**Relevant propositions:**

- transposition is not neutral transfer or representation;
- identities and positions can be altered or produced through movement;
- transpositions are situated, recursive, differential and resistant to complete integration;
- data are not simply given but produced through apparatuses, questions and operations;
- transpositions function as methodological hypotheses rather than reusable recipes;
- aesthetic and epistemic aspects are mutually implicated in successful transpositional work.

### Existing Research-Ecology constitution and cartographic documents

**Access:** Full project documents.  
**Relevant propositions:**

- no canonical total map or global ontology;
- maps are local, authored, versioned and contestable;
- event and interpretation must remain distinct;
- The Middle is a contact zone rather than a command centre;
- lens manifests and declared exclusions are necessary;
- `n - 1` is an anti-sovereignty principle, not a visual deletion effect.

## 3. Partial or mediated sources

### Gilles Deleuze — *Difference and Repetition*

**Access:** No complete primary text was supplied in this tranche. The reading relies on established scholarly reconstructions, the project research dossier and authoritative reference material.

**Provisional interpretation used:**

- difference is genetic rather than a comparison between already constituted identities;
- identity and resemblance are effects of differential relations;
- productive repetition repeats a differential condition and can individuate differently;
- problems are not exhausted by their solutions.

**Restriction:** Specific technical terms such as the dark precursor, passive syntheses and Ideas/multiplicities remain provisional until direct primary-text study is completed.

### Gilles Deleuze — *The Logic of Sense*

**Access:** Secondary reconstruction and selected authoritative summaries.  
**Provisional interpretation used:**

- the event is irreducible to the bodily state of affairs in which it is actualised;
- sense emerges at the relation among series rather than as a hidden essence;
- singularities organise a problem-field before stable subjects and predicates.

**Restriction:** This tranche does not claim a complete reading of the book's series or Stoic framework.

### Gilles Deleuze and Felix Guattari — *What Is Philosophy?*

**Access:** Secondary reconstruction.  
**Use:** The distinctions among concepts, functions, percepts and affects inform the warning against turning philosophy into explanatory content for art.

**Restriction:** Not treated as a complete or uncontested taxonomy of philosophy, science and art.

### Deleuze and Artistic Research volumes

**Access:** Full introduction/excerpt for *Machinic Assemblages of Desire* and secondary material from the DARE sequence.  
**Use:** Assemblage as a changing multiplicity of heterogeneous flows; warning against constructing a fixed “Deleuzian method.”

**Restriction:** Individual chapters outside the supplied extract are not represented as fully read.

## 4. Critical cartography and situated correctives

This tranche also draws on established critical-cartography arguments:

- maps do not simply represent reality; they participate in producing objects, identities and fields of action;
- critique must address both map content and the conditions under which mapping gains authority;
- participatory or open mapping does not automatically eliminate power;
- categories, omissions, interfaces and infrastructure are political operations.

These arguments are used as a corrective to any purely formal celebration of multiplicity, openness or network structure.

## 5. Ulysses materials directly audited

As of 18 July 2026, the following were directly inspected:

- the public Atelier working sheet;
- the material shelf;
- the Atlas data and its admission vocabulary;
- the Pulse/Rhizome graph;
- vital-sign and closure records;
- the current repository structure and automated session model;
- the Tranche 3 toolbox and interim model.

The public working sheet already contains an important self-diagnosis: Ulysses' `swerve -> make` sequence can receive an outside source only to subordinate it again to its own corpus, thesis, pulse and genealogy. The working sheet aptly describes this as `n + 1` wearing the name `n - 1`.

## 6. Corpus limits

The tranche remains limited in five ways:

1. **Derrida is still underrepresented** relative to Deleuze and Mersch in the wider Ulysses shelf.
2. **Concrete artwork analysis remains thinner than theory.** More primary documentation of actual works is required.
3. **Decolonial and Indigenous critiques are not yet integrated deeply enough.**
4. **Machine agency and platform infrastructure require their own focused tranche.**
5. **The project is still largely digital.** Material, bodily, spatial and institutional practices need stronger representation.

## 7. Evidentiary status

The conclusions in this tranche are classified as follows:

```text
SUPPORTED
- directly grounded in primary texts and audited artefacts

PROVISIONAL
- grounded in reliable secondary reconstruction but awaiting primary reading

OPERATIVE HYPOTHESIS
- a proposed translation from theory into artistic-research practice

REJECTED SHORTCUT
- a common but unsupported equivalence the tranche advises against
```

No philosophical concept becomes a Ulysses method merely by appearing in the toolbox. Each remains a situated operative hypothesis subject to failure, correction and abandonment.
