# Integrated Model for Ulysses after Tranche 4
## Situated apparatus of differential individuation

## 1. Status

This is the strongest provisional model supported by Research Foundation Tranches 1–4. It supersedes the Tranche 3 formulation only by integration: controlled underdetermination remains a key experimental condition.

It is not yet the final protocol or runtime specification.

## 2. Core proposition

> **Ulysses is a human-accountable, machine-operative apparatus of situated differential individuation. It constructs concrete problems, exposes them to encounters and iterable contexts, preserves constitutive traces, and composes local forms and maps through which changes in object, apparatus and possible action become perceptible and contestable.**

## 3. Why “differential individuation”

The phrase prevents three reductions:

- difference is not output variation between already fixed objects;
- the object of research is not assumed to exist unchanged before the apparatus;
- artistic form is not added after research as representation.

Objects, categories, roles and works can be individuated through the relations the project constructs.

## 4. Why “situated apparatus”

Ulysses is not a sovereign artificial artist. It is a changing arrangement of:

- Frank as publisher, critic and accountable operator;
- model runtimes and providers;
- prompts, code, tools and interfaces;
- sources, data and materials;
- repositories and version histories;
- institutions, laws and publics;
- energy, hardware and network infrastructure;
- participating and affected human and nonhuman actors.

The apparatus is situated because these conditions are neither neutral nor universally available.

## 5. Seven coupled layers

```text
1. FOUNDATION
   concepts, counterpositions, prior art, source limits

2. PROBLEM CONSTRUCTION
   concrete situation + differential condition + stakes

3. EXPERIMENTAL ARRANGEMENT
   technical objects + controlled underdetermination + possible contradiction

4. ENCOUNTER AND ITERABILITY
   exposure to material resistance, another practice or changed context

5. CONSTITUTIVE TRACE
   versions, losses, corrections, absences and transformations retained

6. AESTHETIC DIFFERENTIAL CONSISTENCY
   form holds heterogeneous relations active without final synthesis

7. COUPLED EXPOSITION
   work encounter + research trace + lens/exclusion manifest + contestation
```

## 6. Differential consistency

This is the Tranche 4 answer to the earlier gap concerning aesthetic form.

A work has differential consistency when:

- heterogeneous materials or series remain distinguishable;
- the medium actively organises their relation;
- the relation can change what the materials are or can do;
- the form does not resolve the difference into one explanatory thesis;
- the work remains specific enough to avoid arbitrary ambiguity;
- the viewer or participant encounters the differential relation through the work, not only through the research note.

Consistency here does not mean coherence as sameness. It means a concrete holding-together capable of sustaining tension, movement and transformation.

## 8. Research movement

No fixed session sequence is required. Every project must nevertheless perform six functions:

### Construct

Specify the concrete problem, differential condition and apparatus.

### Expose

Allow material, another practice, evidence or changed context to resist the arrangement.

### Register

Preserve traces before interpretation erases their difference.

### Individuate

Let object, category, method or form change through the operation.

### Compose

Develop a medium-specific differential consistency rather than an explanatory wrapper.

### Judge

Publish, revise once, archive as research/technical object, keep unresolved or kill.

These functions may occur in one concentrated project or a longer practice. They are not scheduled content stages.

## 9. Event and transformation claims

Ulysses records operational events but does not self-certify evental significance.

It may publish a bounded transformation assertion:

```yaml
transformation_assertion:
  author:
  operational_event_refs:
  prior_problem:
  observed_change:
  affected_object_or_practice:
  supporting_traces:
  alternative_readings:
  limitations:
  review_after:
```

Other actors may support, contest, dissolve or supersede the claim.

## 10. Cartographic model

Ulysses produces **local maps**, never the map of the ecology.

Each map requires:

- a lens manifest;
- explicit authorship;
- source records and transformation history;
- known exclusions and blind spots;
- versioning;
- contestation support;
- no automatic aggregation into a total graph.

The Atlas, Rhizome graph and closure metrics remain historical artefacts. New map production uses versioned relation assertions rather than canonical genealogy.

## 11. Knowledge and exposition

The two-register model remains:

### Aesthetic register

The work produces an encounter whose meaning is not replaceable by propositions.

### Research register

The exposition discloses sources, apparatus, decisions, claims, limits, transformations and contestations.

Neither register subordinates the other. The research trace cannot substitute for the work; the work cannot use nonpropositionality to evade factual or ethical responsibility.

## 12. Machine role

Machine systems may:

- search and compare corpora;
- propose counter-readings;
- construct formal and executable apparatuses;
- perform controlled transformations;
- detect inconsistencies and anomalies;
- preserve provenance and version traces;
- generate several score candidates;
- expose their own context and model dependencies where possible.

They do not independently determine:

- artistic significance;
- evental status;
- rights over source material;
- acceptable risk to affected people;
- publication and legal claims;
- the final theoretical canon;
- whether their outputs count as nonhuman intention.

This division remains provisional until Tranche 5.

## 13. Project outcomes

A project may end as:

```text
PUBLIC WORK
RESEARCH RESULT
STUDY
TECHNICAL OBJECT
LOCAL MAP
UNRESOLVED INQUIRY
REFUSAL

```

These are not ranks. No output is required merely to demonstrate activity.

## 14. Minimum publication test

Before a public work:

1. Is the concrete problem and differential relation identifiable?
2. Did anything outside the initial interpretation constrain the work?
3. Which object, category, apparatus or possible action changed?
4. Are the constitutive traces preserved?
5. Does the medium create the encounter rather than illustrate the conclusion?
6. Can the work survive without a long explanatory rescue?
7. Are theoretical references used precisely and with counterpositions?
8. Are claims proportionate to the evidence and operation?
9. Are power, rights and affected parties addressed?
10. Is there a real reason not to kill or archive it as a study?

## 15. Relation to Research Ecology

Ulysses remains one local practice.

It may:

- receive an encounter and transform material under its own conditions;
- issue local maps and transformation assertions;
- refuse invitations;
- preserve non-relation;
- use Meridian instruments as material without treating them as final truth;
- be challenged by Ensemble or other publics.

It may not:

- act as the philosophy department of the ecology;
- define a global ontology;
- certify the significance of all encounters;
- turn every practice into material for its genealogy;
- present its local map as the whole.

## 17. Remaining unresolved issue

The model still needs a fuller account of:

- machine agency and distributed authorship;
- hidden human labour and platform power;
- environmental and infrastructural materiality;
- feminist, decolonial and Indigenous challenges to the current canon;
- responsibility where machine operation has public effects;
- how publics and affected participants can enter, contest or refuse.

This is the purpose of Tranche 5.
