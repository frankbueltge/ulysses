# Ulysses Research Foundation — Tranche 1

**Status:** working foundation, not a new Ulysses protocol  
**Date:** 2026-07-18  
**Question:** What distinguishes artistic research from art with supporting research, and from scientific research with aesthetic presentation?

## Purpose

This package begins the theoretical and methodological foundation for a later Ulysses model. It deliberately does **not** convert philosophical concepts into a finished toolbox yet.

The first tranche compares foundational, institutional, aesthetic-epistemological and transpositional positions in artistic research. The aim is to identify genuine conflicts, not to manufacture consensus.

## Core interim finding

There is no stable consensus that can simply be implemented as an artistic-research workflow.

The reviewed positions cluster around a productive conflict:

- **Research-legitimation positions** require intention, originality, contextualisation, methods, documentation and public dissemination.
- **Aesthetic-difference positions** argue that artistic cognition cannot be reduced to propositions, methods, outcomes or scientific validation.
- **Practice-transformational positions** treat artistic research as a situated operation that changes its objects, media and sometimes the practice itself.
- **Institutional-critical positions** warn that artistic research loses its distinctive capacity when it imitates academic research and its output regime.

A credible Ulysses model must preserve this conflict instead of choosing one side prematurely.
