# Next reading queue

## Tranche 2 — knowledge, non-knowledge and exposition

Priority texts:

1. Sarat Maharaj — *Know-how and No-How*
2. Robin Nelson — *Practice as Research in the Arts*
3. Michael Schwab & Henk Borgdorff — *The Exposition of Artistic Research*
4. Kathleen Coessens, Darla Crispin, Anne Douglas — *The Artistic Turn*
5. Julian Klein — related work on aesthetic experience
6. Dieter Mersch — *Was heißt, im Ästhetischen forschen?*
7. Dieter Mersch — *Aesthetic Difference*
8. Dieter Mersch — *Nichtpropositionalität und ästhetisches Denken*
9. relevant chapters from *Künstlerische Forschung. Ein Handbuch*

Questions:

- How can tacit, embodied, felt or non-propositional knowing be made public without being translated away?
- What is exposition, and how does it differ from documentation?
- What kinds of criticism remain possible?
- When does “no-how” resist method, and when is it only vagueness?

## Tranche 3 — experiment and epistemic things

1. Hans-Jörg Rheinberger — *Toward a History of Epistemic Things*
2. Rheinberger — *Experimental Systems: Difference, Graphematicity, Conjuncture*
3. Michael Schwab — *Experimental Systems: Future Knowledge in Artistic Research*
4. selected chapters in *Transpositions*
5. Mika Elo — *Ecologies of Artistic Research Practice*
6. Karen Barad — apparatus, intra-action and diffraction
7. Gilbert Simondon / transduction through relevant artistic-research texts

Questions:

- When is “experimental system” a useful transfer, and when does it scientise art?
- What makes an object epistemically underdetermined rather than merely unclear?
- How can repetition remain differential rather than serial output production?

## Tranche 4 — difference, event and cartography

1. Deleuze — *Difference and Repetition*
2. Deleuze & Guattari — *A Thousand Plateaus*
3. Deleuze & Guattari — *What is Philosophy?*
4. Derrida — différance, iterability, trace and supplement
5. Rancière — distribution of the sensible
6. Serres — parasite, noise and relation
7. relevant DARE volumes, especially *The Dark Precursor* and *Machinic Assemblages of Desire*

Questions:

- What is genuinely operationalisable, and what must remain philosophical orientation?
- How do we prevent decorative Deleuzianism?
- How does an event differ from novelty, surprise or output?

## Tranche 5 — machine participation, authorship and labour

1. Aaron Hertzmann — *Can Computers Create Art?*
2. Sofian Audry — *Art in the Age of Machine Learning*
3. Joanna Zylinska — *AI Art*
4. Andreas Broeckmann — *The Machine as Artist as Myth*
5. *Artificial Intelligence — Intelligent Art?*
6. artist-guided neural networks and hybrid creativity chapters
7. work on machinic surrogacy, hidden labour and distributed authorship
8. computational-creativity evaluation literature

Questions:

- Which forms of machine agency are real and which are rhetorical?

- How should attribution and responsibility be exposed?

## Corpus gaps already identified

- Derrida is underrepresented on the current Ulysses shelf.
- The shelf contains more theoretical definitions than sustained artwork criticism.
- Feminist, decolonial and indigenous critiques are present but not yet integrated into the core model.
- Machine-art literature needs stronger attention to labour, infrastructure and institutional attribution.
- German-language and Anglophone artistic-research debates need comparison rather than silent fusion.
