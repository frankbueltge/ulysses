# Interim implications for Ulysses

## 1. The previous v4 proposal is not ready for adoption

The earlier operator model contained plausible concepts — transposition, differential repetition, de-synthesis, apparatus reversal, infrathin amplification and counter-mapping — but it selected and operationalised them before the foundation was complete.

They remain **candidate operators**, not the method of Ulysses.

## 2. What can already be retained

### Ulysses is an apparatus, not a sovereign artificial artist

This is compatible with situated, embodied and transpositional accounts of research. Machine systems can have operational agency without carrying autonomous authorship or final authority.

### Practice must be constitutive

A project cannot qualify merely because Ulysses researches a topic and then produces a digital artefact. The artistic operation must participate in what is discovered, disclosed or transformed.

### Theory cannot arrive only after the work

Theory and prior work are needed to avoid naive reinvention. But theory must not determine the result in advance. It should function as a contested resource, conceptual material or methodological hypothesis.

### Documentation and work must remain distinct

Documentation is necessary for source integrity, apparatus disclosure and research accountability. It is not evidence that the artwork succeeds.

### Human judgment remains legitimate and necessary

The reviewed literature provides no reason to delegate artistic judgment to the system. Human curation is not a defect in machine-involved practice; hiding it would be the defect.

## 3. What must be suspended

Until later tranches are complete, do not freeze:

- a definitive list of Ulysses operators;
- a universal one-page score;
- a single model of knowledge production;
- a compulsory “non-fit” starting condition;
- a claim that every work must transform its initial problem;
- a fixed relation between work, documentation and public exposition;
- `Both Stand` as proof of the model.

`Both Stand` can remain a candidate case, but it should be re-evaluated after the case corpus and toolbox are developed.

## 4. A more accurate architecture for the future model

The literature suggests four layers rather than one workflow.

### A. Foundation

- corpus;
- concept dossiers;
- counterpositions;
- histories and institutional critique;
- case analyses.

### B. Project constellation

Each project selects only the concepts, cases and materials relevant to its situation. No total theoretical context is loaded by default.

### C. Artistic strategy

The project formulates a situated strategy or methodological hypothesis. It may use an existing operator, alter one or invent another.

### D. Exposition and contestation

The work, process, apparatus, theoretical relation and claims become publicly inspectable without being collapsed into one explanatory statement.

## 5. The key design tension

Ulysses must satisfy two requirements that cannot be fully reconciled:

1. **External intelligibility and seriousness** — sources, context, prior work, claims and apparatus must be visible enough for informed criticism.
2. **Aesthetic irreducibility** — the work cannot be reduced to its documentation, theory or a set of research findings.

The future model should not solve this tension. It should organise it productively.

## 6. Immediate operational decision

Do not restart automatic Ulysses production while the foundation is being built.

This does not require a long shadow experiment. It simply prevents the current weak production mode from continuing and prevents a premature replacement protocol from becoming active.

The research foundation itself can be built through targeted reading and case analysis without recurring nightly sessions.
