# Next Reading Queue
## Tranche 4 — Difference, event, trace, iterability and cartography

## Leitfrage

How can an artistic research apparatus produce and hold open a consequential difference without reducing it to novelty, error, network imagery or a concept already known in advance?

## Priority A — primary philosophical texts

1. Gilles Deleuze — *Difference and Repetition*
   - difference in itself;
   - repetition beyond identity;
   - dark precursor;
   - problem and event.

2. Gilles Deleuze — *The Logic of Sense*
   - event;
   - surface, series and sense;
   - singularities.

3. Gilles Deleuze and Félix Guattari — *A Thousand Plateaus*
   - rhizome and map;
   - assemblage/agencement;
   - diagram and abstract machine;
   - refrain, territory and deterritorialisation;
   - concrete rules and abstract machines.

4. Jacques Derrida — selected primary texts
   - différance;
   - trace;
   - iterability;
   - supplement;
   - archive where relevant.

5. Gilles Deleuze and Félix Guattari — *What Is Philosophy?*
   - distinctions and relations among philosophy, science and art;
   - percepts, affects, functions and concepts.

## Priority B — artistic-research mediation

- DARE 2015 — *The Dark Precursor*
- DARE 2017 — *Aberrant Nuptials*
- DARE 2019 / *Machinic Assemblages of Desire*
- Michael Schwab — transposition and experimental systems
- Simon O'Sullivan — art encounters Deleuze and Guattari
- Erin Manning and Brian Massumi — research-creation and thought in the act
- Vytautas Michelkevičius — diagrammatic knowing

## Priority C — critical correctives

- feminist and decolonial critiques of nomadism, assemblage and universalised becoming;
- Rosi Braidotti on transpositions and situated cartographies;
- Karen Barad on diffraction and accountability;
- Haraway on situated knowledges;
- critical cartography and data feminism;
- software/platform studies concerning diagrams, interfaces and algorithmic cuts.

## Case studies

1. Robert Smithson — Non-Sites and site/non-site relation.
2. Bureau d'Études — political cartographies.
3. Forensic Architecture — models, evidence and public contestation.
4. Research Catalogue expositions using non-linear or diagrammatic form.
5. One failed “rhizomatic” digital interface as a negative case.
6. Ulysses' existing Atlas, Pulse and graph structures as self-audit material.

## Questions to resolve

- What distinguishes an event from an unexpected output?
- How can a work hold incompatible series in relation without synthesising them?
- When is a trace constitutive rather than documentary?
- What does iterability add to differential repetition?
- How can a map act without claiming totality?
- What is the difference between diagram, model, graph and cartography?
- Can a software interface produce a line of flight, or does it only encode available routes?
- Which political exclusions are hidden by formal multiplicity?
