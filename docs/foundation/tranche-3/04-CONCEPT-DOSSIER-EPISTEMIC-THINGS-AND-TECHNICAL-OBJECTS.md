# Concept Dossier: Epistemic Things and Technical Objects

## 1. Why this distinction matters

The vocabulary of **epistemic things** and **technical objects** is attractive for artistic research because it seems to offer a way to describe open-ended inquiry without reducing it to hypothesis testing. It is also easy to misuse.

An epistemic thing is not simply:

- an object that looks mysterious;
- a concept that has not yet been clearly formulated;
- a generative output whose exact appearance was unpredictable;
- an artwork whose meaning remains open;
- or a topic about which the model has insufficient information.

The distinction becomes useful only inside an experimental arrangement in which some components are sufficiently stabilised to let another component remain materially and conceptually underdetermined.

## 2. Technical objects

Technical objects are the relatively stabilised parts of an inquiry. Depending on the project, they may include:

- instruments and sensors;
- source corpora and versioned datasets;
- protocols, prompts and selection rules;
- code, models and runtime configurations;
- media formats and storage systems;
- calibration procedures;
- classification schemes;
- repeatable scores or performance instructions;
- legal, spatial and institutional conditions.

They are not epistemically neutral. A technical object embodies prior knowledge, exclusions, tolerances and conventions. Yet it has to become reliable enough for a difference elsewhere in the system to become legible.

For Ulysses this means that the following cannot remain vague when they bear on a result:

```text
model and model version
context supplied to the model
source selection
code revision and random seed
transformation rule
measurement or classification criterion
human selection and rejection
publication interface
```

Calling the whole apparatus fluid or entangled does not remove the need to specify these conditions.

## 3. Epistemic things

An epistemic thing is a materially engaged research object whose decisive properties are not yet fully known and may change through the process of inquiry.

Three features are central.

### 3.1 It is not simply absent information

The task is not merely to reveal a fact already fixed somewhere else. The inquiry may alter what the object can be said to be. The object gains provisional identity through traces, interventions, comparisons and models.

### 3.2 It is tied to an apparatus

The epistemic thing does not exist as a pure unknown outside all mediation. It becomes researchable through a concrete experimental system. Its appearance is therefore inseparable from the conditions under which it is made to leave traces.

### 3.3 It can shift roles

What is epistemic in one phase can become technical in a later phase. A newly characterised process may be stabilised as an instrument for investigating something else. Conversely, a formerly reliable technical object may become epistemic when its adequacy, calibration or classification logic is called into question.

This role mobility is particularly important for Ulysses. A model, interface or archive should not be assigned one permanent epistemic status.

## 4. Role changes as research events

A strong inquiry often contains a role reversal such as:

```text
instrument -> object of investigation
classification rule -> contested assumption
source corpus -> evidence of exclusion
interface -> producer of the phenomenon it claims to display
model output -> trace of the runtime rather than representation of the world
```

Examples from the present ecology include:

- a verifier becoming the object of scrutiny after misreading its own result;
- a repository audit turning an earlier critique into a partially refuted claim;
- a trust configuration becoming the decisive variable rather than the nominal signal being measured.

These cases are epistemically stronger than a closed generator because they can displace the identity of what the project thought it was studying.

## 5. Can an artwork be an epistemic thing?

The literature does not justify a simple equation:

```text
artwork = epistemic thing
```

At least four configurations must be distinguished.

### A. The artwork as technical object

The work is a stable apparatus that allows a visitor or researcher to encounter and vary another underdetermined object.

### B. The artwork as trace

The work records a process of inquiry but is not itself the principal epistemic object.

### C. The artwork as epistemic object

The identity, form or function of the work remains genuinely underdetermined and is transformed through inquiry. This is possible, but cannot be asserted merely because interpretation remains open.

### D. The artwork as exposition

The work organises access to traces, claims and unresolved differences. It may be epistemically active without itself being the thing researched.

A future Ulysses project should state which configuration it proposes and permit this status to change.

## 6. The problem of model-generated unknowns

Large language and generative models readily produce outputs that were not individually predicted. This does not make every output an epistemic thing.

Model variation is epistemically weak when:

- the space of possible outputs is defined by the model and prompt;
- the project selects surprising samples retrospectively;
- novelty is measured only against the project's own generated population;
- no material consequence follows from the variation;
- the initial framing cannot be disconfirmed;
- the model remains a black-box source of attractive differences.

Model participation becomes more research-relevant when:

- outputs are treated as traces of a specific apparatus rather than autonomous inventions;
- competing configurations expose a previously unnoticed dependence;
- generated results are tested against external material or practice;
- a model's failure changes the research object or technical arrangement;
- the selection process and discarded outputs remain inspectable where relevant.

## 7. Criteria for Ulysses

Before using the term `epistemic thing`, Ulysses should answer:

```yaml
epistemic_candidate:
  current_identity:
  what_is_not_yet_determined:
  why_this_is_not_merely_missing_information:
  technical_objects_that_make_it_researchable:
  traces_it_can_leave:
  possible_role_change:
  possible_disconfirmation:
  external_or_material_resistance:
  limits_of_the_claim:
```

A convincing answer must identify a real uncertainty in the object or relation, not only uncertainty about whether the work will look good.

## 8. Misuse warnings

### Poetic inflation

Using “epistemic thing” to dignify ambiguity, opacity or unfinishedness.

### Technical invisibility

Treating models and interfaces as neutral carriers while assigning epistemic agency only to the artwork.

### Retrospective naming

Calling whatever proved interesting an epistemic thing after the fact, without showing how the system made it investigable.

### Closed-world novelty

Generating and measuring novelty inside one artificial world, then presenting the result as new knowledge about a wider reality.

### Permanent openness

Refusing all provisional stabilisation. An object that can never become more determinate cannot support a research trajectory.

## 9. Provisional consequence

For Ulysses, the concept should function less as a label for works and more as a design question:

> What in this arrangement is stable enough to work, what remains materially underdetermined, and what could change roles as the inquiry proceeds?

That question can inform a project. It must not predetermine that the project has already produced future knowledge.
