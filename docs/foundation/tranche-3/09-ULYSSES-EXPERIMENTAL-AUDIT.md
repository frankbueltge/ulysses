# Ulysses Experimental Audit
## Reassessment after Tranche 3

## 1. Audit question

Which existing Ulysses works actually operate as research experiments, and which are better described as conceptual works, demonstrations, simulations or research notes?

The categories are not a quality ranking. A demonstration can be an excellent artwork. The purpose is to prevent inflated epistemic claims.

## 2. Audit criteria

A project is closer to a research experiment when it contains:

1. a concrete, bounded research object or relation;
2. stable technical conditions;
3. a load-bearing uncertainty not fully encoded by the project;
4. trace production separable from interpretation;
5. a plausible disconfirmation or correction route;
6. recursive feedback into question, instrument or practice;
7. an epistemic claim limited to the apparatus;
8. an aesthetic form not reducible to explanation.

No single criterion is sufficient.

## 3. *Differential Reproduction*

### Existing claim

The work presents a mutation-driven artificial-life simulation and treats persistent novel motifs as epistemic things produced through differential reproduction.

### What is genuinely strong

- The implementation is explicit and reproducible.
- The artificial world has clear viability and mutation rules.
- The fixed seeds make comparison possible.
- The work identifies a meaningful distinction between mere mutation and persistence within its model.

### Where the experimental claim overreaches

The decisive categories are set in advance:

- what counts as a genome;
- what counts as viable;
- what counts as a motif;
- what counts as novelty;
- what counts as persistence.

The simulation can produce unanticipated configurations, but it cannot substantially redefine its object without a change made from outside the model. Its novelty events are findings **about the consequences of the programmed world**, not yet epistemic things in the stronger Rheinberger sense.

### Revised classification

```text
primary: exploratory simulation
secondary: conceptual demonstration
possible artistic status: interactive computational work
not yet supported: external empirical discovery or robust epistemic thing
```

### What would strengthen it

- compare the model against an external process or corpus;
- let anomalous results force revision of viability or novelty criteria;
- preserve a chain from external trace to model and back;
- invite a second practice to contest the ontology;
- expose the selection mechanism aesthetically rather than presenting novelty as a self-evident event.

## 4. *The Third Pile*

### Existing operation

A critique of the Ulysses archive is tested against the repository itself.

### Experimental strength

- the corpus exists independently of the later audit;
- categories and counts can be inspected;
- the result can refute the initial reading;
- the project records that the stronger accusation did not survive measurement;
- the finding changes Ulysses' account of its own production.

### Remaining limitation

The audit's research strength exceeds the aesthetic operation. It is a strong inquiry and correction, but not automatically a strong artwork.

### Revised classification

```text
primary: adversarial repository audit
secondary: reflexive research instrument
artistic status: unresolved / dependent on exposition
```

## 5. *Inaccurate Citations*

### Existing operation

A bounded reference set is checked. Two disclosed gaps are flagged, while no fabricated references are found. The system initially misreads one flag and corrects itself through primary-source review.

### Experimental strength

- primary records resist the system's first interpretation;
- the checker becomes an object of inquiry;
- technical and epistemic roles change;
- the correction is not hidden;
- the research question shifts from citation inaccuracy to classification of evidence gaps and verifier adequacy.

### Revised classification

```text
primary: recursive verification experiment
secondary: apparatus critique
artistic status: unresolved / potentially expositional
```

## 6. What the comparison reveals

The works that best support a research claim are not those with the richest generative output. They are those where:

- material predates the interpretation;
- the initial claim can lose;
- a tool can become the problem;
- primary traces survive the explanatory layer;
- correction changes later practice.

This is an important inversion of Ulysses' earlier self-image. Its strongest research capacity may be **disciplined adversarial rereading of its own and others' apparatuses**, not autonomous ideation.

## 7. Implications for work development

A future Ulysses project should declare its primary mode:

```text
AESTHETIC EXPERIMENT
ADVERSARIAL AUDIT
EXPLORATORY SIMULATION
TRANSPOSITIONAL STUDY
APPARATUS CRITIQUE
CONCEPTUAL DEMONSTRATION
```

Modes may overlap, but one should be primary. This prevents a simulation from borrowing the authority of empirical research and prevents an audit from being published as art without a separate aesthetic judgment.

## 8. Immediate conclusion

Do not discard *Differential Reproduction*. Reclassify it accurately and use it as evidence of a category error the Foundation has now identified.

Do not automatically elevate *The Third Pile* or *Inaccurate Citations* into masterpieces. Use them as evidence that Ulysses can create strong research conditions when the apparatus is exposed to material correction.

The next model should preserve this capacity and add a stronger account of aesthetic form.
