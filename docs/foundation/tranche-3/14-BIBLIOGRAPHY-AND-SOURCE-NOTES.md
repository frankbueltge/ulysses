# Bibliography and Source Notes
## Tranche 3

## Primary and closely read sources

### Rheinberger, Hans-Jörg

“Transpositions: From Traces through Data to Models and Simulations.” In Michael Schwab (ed.), *Transpositions: Aesthetico-Epistemic Operators in Artistic Research*. Leuven University Press, 2018, pp. 215–224.

**Use in this tranche:** experiment versus demonstration; trace; data; model; simulation; infra-experimentality; apparatus mediation.

### Schwab, Michael

“Introduction.” In *Transpositions: Aesthetico-Epistemic Operators in Artistic Research*. Leuven University Press, 2018.

**Use:** experimental systems in artistic research; technical objects and epistemic things; concepts as methodologically productive but underdetermined.

### Arlander, Annette

“Calling the Dragon, Holding Hands with Junipers: Transpositions in Practice.” In *Transpositions*, 2018.

**Use:** repetition with difference; relocation and recreation of performance practice; Baradian mediation.

### de Assis, Paulo

“Transduction and Ensembles of Transducers: Relaying Flows of Intensities.” In *Transpositions*, 2018.

**Use:** Simondon, transduction, individuation, non-human participation and performative actualisation.

### Mersch, Dieter

*Was heißt, im Ästhetischen forschen?* 2015.

**Use:** aesthetic experiment, Duchamp, singularity, exactness, contingency and reflexivity of measurement.

### Elo, Mika

“Ecologies of Artistic Research Practice.” 2020.

**Use:** situational practice, incongruence and research ecology.

**Status in Tranche 3:** selective use and prior research dossier. Full conceptual work on repetition, assemblage and cartography is deferred to Tranche 4.

### Barad, Karen

*Meeting the Universe Halfway*. Duke University Press, 2007.

**Status:** mediated through Arlander and established secondary use in the current corpus. Strong claims about Barad's full argument are deferred until direct primary-text review.

### Simondon, Gilbert

Works on individuation and technical objects.

**Status:** mediated principally through Paulo de Assis and the *Transpositions* volume. Direct primary-text review remains necessary.

## Source discipline

This tranche distinguishes:

- direct reading;
- targeted chapter reading;
- mediated conceptual reconstruction;
- case interpretation;
- project inference.

Terms such as `epistemic thing`, `differential reproduction`, `intra-action` and `transduction` are not treated as interchangeable. Where the primary source has not been fully reviewed, the limitation is preserved rather than concealed by fluent synthesis.
