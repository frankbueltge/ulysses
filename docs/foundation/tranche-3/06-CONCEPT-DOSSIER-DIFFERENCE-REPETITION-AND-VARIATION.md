# Concept Dossier: Difference, Repetition and Variation

## 1. Three notions that must not be collapsed

Ulysses has repeatedly used variation, mutation and repeated execution as if they were interchangeable with philosophical difference or experimental differential reproduction. They are not.

### Variation

A change in parameter, form, sample or output.

### Differential reproduction

An experimental system reproduces enough of its organisation to maintain continuity while producing differences that can become epistemically consequential.

### Deleuzian repetition

Repetition is not the recurrence of an identical original. It is a productive process in which difference is internal to what repeats. Its philosophical scope is broader than an experimental protocol and cannot be reduced to running the same code several times.

## 2. Repetition in an experimental system

Experimental repetition needs both continuity and deviation.

Too much stability:

```text
same setup -> same expected result -> demonstration
```

Too little stability:

```text
uncontrolled variation -> incomparable outputs -> noise without attribution
```

A productive middle condition is:

```text
reproduced organisation
+ traceable alteration or perturbation
+ retained consequences
+ feedback into later intervention
```

The word `differential` therefore concerns the organisation of research, not merely the statistical diversity of outcomes.

## 3. Deleuze as a philosophical resource

Deleuze's critique of identity-first thinking is relevant because it resists treating variation as a deviation from an already complete original. Repetition can generate what counts as the identity of the repeated series.

Operational consequences for Ulysses may include:

- do not assume a stable original before the repetitions;
- examine how each iteration redefines the series;
- track conditions that make a difference consequential;
- avoid ranking variants only by resemblance to an initial target;
- allow a repeated operation to alter its own rule.

These are consequences, not a claim that a software loop implements *Difference and Repetition*.

## 4. Mutation is not novelty

A mutation operator creates difference by definition. It does not establish that the difference matters.

At least four thresholds should be separated:

1. **formal difference** — outputs are not identical;
2. **systemic persistence** — a difference survives or propagates;
3. **functional consequence** — the difference changes behaviour or relation;
4. **epistemic consequence** — it revises a question, category, instrument or explanation.

Many generative artworks stop at level 1. Artificial-life simulations may reach levels 2 or 3 inside their world. Level 4 requires an explicit relation to inquiry.

## 5. Chance and indeterminacy

Chance procedures can remove or redistribute authorial control. They can expose a system to unforeseen combinations. But chance is not externality by itself.

A random seed remains part of the apparatus. Its outputs occur within a programmed possibility space. The relevant research questions are:

- What is delegated to chance?
- Which constraints remain fixed?
- Who selects among outcomes?
- What can the random operation disconfirm?
- Does the result change the rule, or only instantiate it?

Cage and Duchamp are important not because they used randomness, but because their procedures changed relations among authorship, measure, performance, singularity and event.

## 6. Repetition as artistic operation

A repetition can become aesthetically and epistemically productive when it creates an encounter with difference rather than displaying a parameter sweep.

Possible structures:

### Same score, changed situation

A stable instruction is enacted in materially different environments. The work concerns what the score cannot preserve.

### Same material, changed apparatus

A source passes through different models, sensors, legal regimes or presentation formats. The work concerns how the apparatus produces the object differently.

### Same apparatus, changed participant

Different embodied or institutional positions expose hidden assumptions in access, classification or response.

### Recursive repetition

Each result modifies the conditions of the next iteration. The series has a memory and can change direction.

## 7. Anti-patterns for Ulysses

- mutation-rate sliders presented as philosophy;
- many model outputs shown as evidence of openness;
- identical code run with several random seeds and called repetition with difference;
- selecting the most surprising output while hiding the population;
- describing any deviation as an event;
- claiming differential reproduction without a stable research organisation;
- using Deleuze to legitimate lack of comparison or criteria.

## 9. Provisional conclusion

For Ulysses, repetition should be treated neither as production cadence nor as a visual motif. It is a research relation:

> A repeated arrangement becomes productive when it preserves enough continuity for a difference to be attributable and allows that difference to modify what is repeated.

This criterion is stricter than generative variation and more modest than claiming a direct implementation of Deleuzian ontology.
