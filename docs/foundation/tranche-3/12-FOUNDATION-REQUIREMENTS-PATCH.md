# Foundation Requirements Patch
## Requirements added after Tranche 3

The following requirements extend FR-01 to FR-10 from Tranche 2.

## FR-11 — Experiment and demonstration must be distinguished

A project whose relevant outcome is fully determined by its setup may be a valid artwork or demonstration, but must not claim the epistemic status of a research experiment.

## FR-12 — Technical and epistemic roles must be explicit

Each project identifies which components are treated as relatively stable technical objects and which object or relation remains epistemically underdetermined. Role changes are recorded.

## FR-13 — Underdetermination must be load-bearing

Uncertainty about visual quality, model phrasing or random output is insufficient. The open relation must be capable of changing the question, category, instrument or practice.

## FR-14 — Traces precede interpretation

Where a research claim depends on an interaction, the resulting traces must be preserved in a form distinguishable from later selection, modelling and narration.

## FR-15 — Trace, data, model and projection remain distinguishable

Projects document the transformations through which traces become data, models, simulations and public forms when those transformations materially affect the claim.

## FR-16 — Simulation claims are scoped

An exploratory simulation primarily supports claims about its model and assumptions. Claims about external phenomena require an explicit return loop or independent grounding.

## FR-17 — Variation is not novelty

Formal difference, persistence, functional consequence and epistemic consequence must not be collapsed. Model sampling or mutation is not by itself evidence of new knowledge.

## FR-18 — Repetition requires attributable difference

A repeated operation must preserve enough continuity to compare outcomes and enough openness for a consequential difference to emerge. More iterations do not automatically strengthen the work.

## FR-19 — Apparatus and cuts are constitutive

Object boundaries, agency distributions, exclusions, prompts, models, interfaces and human selection are part of the research situation and must be exposed at an appropriate level.

## FR-20 — Distributed agency does not erase responsibility

Machine, material, human and institutional agencies may be entangled or co-constitutive. Legal, ethical and publication responsibility remains specifically assigned.

## FR-21 — Research mode limits the claim

Each project declares a primary mode such as aesthetic experiment, adversarial audit, exploratory simulation, transpositional study, apparatus critique or conceptual demonstration.

## FR-22 — Research and artwork receive separate judgment

A strong research operation is not automatically a strong artwork. A compelling artwork is not automatically a research experiment. Publication decisions must assess both registers without requiring both in every output.
