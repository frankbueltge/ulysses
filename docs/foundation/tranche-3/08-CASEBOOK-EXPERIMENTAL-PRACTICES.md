# Casebook: Experimental Practices

## Purpose

This casebook does not rank works by importance. It asks how different practices organise stability, openness, trace, repetition, apparatus and epistemic claim.

## 1. Hans-Jörg Rheinberger — laboratory experimental systems

### Configuration

- stable preparations, instruments and protocols;
- epistemic object not yet fully characterised;
- repeatable trace production;
- recursive movement among trace, data, model and intervention.

### What it contributes

A rigorous account of how the unknown becomes materially researchable without being fully specified in advance.

### Limit for Ulysses

The model comes from experimental science. Artistic work cannot inherit scientific validity through formal resemblance to a laboratory.

### Transferable question

> Which part of the artistic arrangement functions as a technical object, and which relation remains genuinely open to transformation?

## 2. Marcel Duchamp — *Trois Stoppages Étalon*

### Configuration

Three threads of equal length are dropped from a fixed height and preserve their contingent curves as new measures.

### Stability

- specified thread length;
- specified height;
- repeated procedure;
- preserved outcomes.

### Openness

The fall creates singular curves that cannot be predetermined individually.

### Research effect

The work does not discover a universal law. It turns the standard, measurement and experimental procedure themselves into aesthetic problems.

### Lesson

Exactness and contingency are not opposites. A procedure can be precise while exposing the arbitrariness of measure.

### Misuse warning

The lesson is not “add randomness.” It concerns the transformation of a standard through repeated singular events.

## 3. John Cage — chance and indeterminacy

### Configuration

Cage's procedures redistribute compositional decision through chance operations, scores and performer situations.

### Stability

- carefully specified procedures or scores;
- bounded materials and durations;
- institutional conditions of performance.

### Openness

- particular sonic events;
- performer decisions;
- environmental sound;
- temporal encounter.

### Research effect

The practices test authorship, listening, intention and the boundaries of music.

### Lesson

Chance can be a disciplined redistribution of control. It is not valuable because it produces surprising samples.

### Misuse warning

A random generator with retrospective curation may reinforce rather than displace authorship.

## 4. Annette Arlander — *Animal Years*

### Configuration

Repeated performances for camera are relocated and recreated across time and place. Arlander uses the series to ask why some practices transpose more readily than others.

### Stability

- recurrent score and camera practice;
- identifiable performance lineage;
- archived repetitions.

### Openness

- weather, growth, season and site;
- bodily and temporal change;
- different relations between performance and environment.

### Research effect

Transposition becomes a question arising from practice after the fact, not a concept merely illustrated by it.

### Lesson

A repeated artistic score can become research when the differences among situations alter how the practice is understood.

## 5. Paulo de Assis — transductive musical practice

### Configuration

Musical works are treated not as fixed forms but as fields of pre-individual potentials actualised through performers, materials, technologies and intensities.

### Contribution

The practice challenges both formalism and human-centred subjectivity. Work and performer individuate together through performance.

### Lesson

The relevant unit may be the process of individuation rather than a stable object being reproduced.

### Misuse warning

The concept becomes empty if every performance is called transductive without specifying disparity, interface and transformation.

## 6. Ulysses — *Differential Reproduction*

### Configuration

A deterministic artificial-life simulation uses a fixed codebase, mutation-rate sweep and internally defined motif criteria to generate novelty events.

### Strengths

- explicit rules and deterministic seeds;
- inspectable implementation;
- reproducible parameter space;
- a clear conceptual relation among mutation, viability and persistence.

### Limits

- the artificial world, viable forms and novelty metric are all defined by the project;
- unexpected results remain consequences of the encoded system;
- “epistemic thing” is assigned to internally detected events without a demonstrated role change or external return;
- the simulation largely illustrates an interpretation of differential reproduction.

### Classification

**Strong conceptual simulation and demonstration; weak evidence for an epistemic thing.**

### Productive revision

The work could become more research-oriented if a result returned to an external corpus, biological case, software ecosystem or participating practice and forced revision of the model.

## 7. Ulysses — *The Third Pile*

### Configuration

A repository-level claim is subjected to a measured audit.

### Stability

- defined corpus;
- explicit categories;
- inspectable records and counts.

### Openness

The audit can contradict the starting diagnosis.

### Result

Part of the initial critique is refuted rather than rhetorically rescued.

### Classification

**A strong adversarial research probe.** It permits real disconfirmation and changes the project's own account.

### Artistic question

The epistemic operation is stronger than the aesthetic form. A future work would need to make the altered relation perceptible without merely displaying the audit.

## 8. Ulysses — *Inaccurate Citations*

### Configuration

A citation-checking instrument examines a bounded set of references. The initial machine interpretation misreads a result and is corrected through primary-source inspection.

### Strengths

- primary materials constrain interpretation;
- the instrument can expose both source gaps and its own reading error;
- correction is preserved as part of the record;
- the problem shifts from “fabricated citations” to the adequacy of the checker and the distinction between disclosed gaps and false references.

### Classification

**A strong recursive experimental situation.** Technical object and epistemic object partially exchange roles.

### Artistic question

As with *The Third Pile*, the research event does not automatically constitute a compelling artwork. The case is valuable evidence for Ulysses' strongest inquiry mode.

## 10. Casebook conclusion

Ulysses' promising direction is not simply to imitate laboratory science or abandon procedure for artistic openness. It is to combine:

- exact, inspectable apparatuses;
- a question that can be materially contradicted;
- preservation of traces and corrections;
- a form that exposes the altered relation aesthetically;
- and a limited, publicly contestable research claim.
