# Interim Model for Ulysses after Tranche 3
## Apparatus of Controlled Underdetermination

## 1. Status

This is the strongest provisional model supported by Tranches 1–3. It is not yet a final protocol, runtime design or automatic method.

## 2. Core proposition

> Ulysses is a human-accountable, machine-operative artistic research apparatus that constructs controlled situations of underdetermination, preserves the traces produced by those situations, and develops aesthetic forms through which changes in object, apparatus or problem can be encountered and contested.

The model contains five coupled but distinguishable layers.

```text
1. FOUNDATION
   concepts, counterpositions, cases, source limits

2. EXPERIMENTAL ARRANGEMENT
   technical objects + open epistemic relation + possible disconfirmation

3. TRACE CHAIN
   interaction -> trace -> data -> model -> interpretation

4. AESTHETIC INDIVIDUATION
   material and medium produce a form not reducible to the research note

5. COUPLED EXPOSITION
   work encounter + research trace + contestation
```

## 3. Controlled underdetermination

`Controlled` does not mean centrally scripted. It means that enough of the apparatus is explicit and stable for differences to become attributable.

`Underdetermined` does not mean vague. It means that one or more load-bearing relations cannot be decided in advance and can be transformed by material, experiment, encounter or correction.

A project fails this model in two opposite ways:

### Overdetermination

The work demonstrates a thesis already encoded in its rules.

### Indeterminate looseness

Nothing is stable enough for the result to count as a trace of anything specific.

## 4. Project constitution

A project should begin with a **research constellation**, not a nightly session or abstract topic.

```yaml
research_constellation:
  concrete_situation:
  prior_art_and_research:
  theoretical_positions:
  counterpositions:
  technical_objects:
  provisional_epistemic_object:
  load_bearing_uncertainty:
  trace_form:
  possible_disconfirmation:
  apparatus_and_agential_cuts:
  aesthetic_problem:
  ethical_and_political_conditions:
```

This is not a grant proposal. Fields can be concise. Their purpose is to expose category errors before production.

## 5. Project modes

Every project declares one primary mode:

- `AESTHETIC_EXPERIMENT`
- `ADVERSARIAL_AUDIT`
- `EXPLORATORY_SIMULATION`
- `TRANSPOSITIONAL_STUDY`
- `APPARATUS_CRITIQUE`
- `CONCEPTUAL_DEMONSTRATION`

The declaration limits claims. A conceptual demonstration is not inferior by definition; it is simply not presented as empirical discovery.

## 6. Research movement

The model does not prescribe a fixed number of phases. It does require four functions:

### A. Construct

Stabilise the minimum apparatus and specify the open relation.

### B. Expose

Let material, evidence, another practice, a participant or a technical reality act on the arrangement.

### C. Register

Preserve traces before they are absorbed into interpretation.

### D. Recompose or stop

Allow the result to modify the question, apparatus, work or project status. Stop if nothing consequential changed.

These functions may happen in one concentrated intervention or across a longer material practice. They are not automatic sessions.

## 7. Aesthetic requirement

Research strength does not guarantee artistic strength.

The work must still answer:

- Why does this require this medium and form?
- What becomes perceptible or actionable through the work that the research note cannot replace?
- Does the form keep the relevant difference active rather than illustrating it?
- How is the viewer or participant positioned by the apparatus?
- What remains unresolved without being artificially mystified?

A strong audit can remain a research result and never become a public artwork.

## 8. Human and machine roles

### Machine-operable functions

- corpus search and comparison;
- source checking;
- formal modelling and simulation;
- controlled variation;
- code and apparatus construction;
- anomaly and inconsistency detection;
- generation of counter-readings;
- maintenance of trace and provenance records.

### Human-accountable functions

- defining acceptable risks and boundaries;
- selecting and refusing materials;
- deciding which claim is publicly made;
- aesthetic judgment and publication;
- rights, ethics and affected persons;
- acceptance or rejection of model-produced interpretations;
- terminating a weak or misleading project.

Machine initiative may be substantial inside a project, but responsibility and publication are not delegated.

## 9. Minimal judgment model

A project can end as:

```text
WORK
RESEARCH RESULT
STUDY
TECHNICAL OBJECT
UNRESOLVED INQUIRY
KILLED
```

These are not a ladder. A useful technical object may later support another project. An unresolved inquiry may remain private. A research result need not be forced into an artwork.

## 10. What Ulysses should produce

Under this model, Ulysses may produce:

- materially specific aesthetic experiments;
- research instruments that can fail or be corrected;
- adversarial audits of claims and apparatuses;
- simulations with explicit scope and return loops;
- transpositional works that make losses and transformations consequential;
- local counter-maps with declared cuts;
- research traces and concept dossiers;
- works emerging from actual encounters rather than thematic prompts.

It should not produce regular content simply to appear active.

## 11. Consequence for the existing archive

Existing works should not be rewritten retroactively. They can receive a non-ranking research-mode annotation where useful.

For example:

```text
Differential Reproduction -> exploratory simulation / conceptual demonstration
The Third Pile -> adversarial audit
Inaccurate Citations -> recursive verification experiment
```

The archive becomes evidence of conceptual development, including earlier category errors.

## 12. Remaining gap

The model now has a stronger experimental epistemology than aesthetic theory of form. Tranche 4 must therefore deepen:

- difference and repetition;
- event and encounter;
- trace and iterability;
- cartography and diagram;
- how form can hold a difference open without turning into explanatory interface design.

Only after that should a final Ulysses protocol and first new work be specified.
