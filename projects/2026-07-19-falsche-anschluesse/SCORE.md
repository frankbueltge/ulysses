# Project score — Falsche Anschlüsse

## 1. Source situation

**Concrete object, encounter, material or technical condition**

An offer explicitly addressed to this practice (REQUESTS.md, "Team note — 2026-07-19",
commit `216de29`): six quotes, transcribed verbatim as printed in Frank Bültge's 2006
master's thesis *Die Wiederholung der Zukunft — Diagnostik und Intervention in medialen
Gefügen als ästhetische Differenzpraxis*, each carrying an author's name and no source.
Per the offer: the thesis closes with a quote-collage appendix titled „Falsche
Anschlüsse" whose own preface admits that quotes stood in where the time was missing to
put things in the author's own words; three of the six come from that collage, three
from the main text; the thesis was examined by Dieter Mersch; and one attribution
(Feyerabend) the author states he never verified.

The six sentences, as offered (attributed names in parentheses; the attributions are the
object under test, not accepted facts):

1. „Ein Metaphysiker ist ein Musiker ohne musikalische Fähigkeit" (Rudolf Carnap)
2. »Eines Tages wird man vielleicht wissen, dass es keine Kunst gab, sondern nur
   Medizin.« (Nietzsche)
3. »… Museums zerschellt ist und die Scherben auf den Glasberg fallen, dann fragen Sie
   nicht die Kunst, warum.« (Jochen Gerz)
4. »Es ist unmöglich nur beobachtbare Größen in eine Theorie aufzunehmen. Es ist
   vielmehr die Theorie, die entscheidet, was man beobachten kann.« (Albert Einstein)
5. »… und wir müssen uns daran erinnern, daß das, was wir beobachten, nicht die Natur
   selbst ist, sondern Natur, die unserer Art der Fragestellung ausgesetzt ist.«
   (Werner Heisenberg)
6. »Nicht konservative, sondern antizipatorische Vermutungen lenken die Forschung.«
   (Paul Feyerabend)

**Provenance and version**

Material = the six quotes **as transcribed in the offer**. The thesis itself (print,
2006) is not in this practice's hands; every statement about the thesis in this project
rests on the founder's own transcription and description in REQUESTS.md (retrievable
in-repo). Disclosed limit: if a transcription differs from the printed page, a verdict
may shift; verdicts are therefore verdicts on the offered wordings.

**Rights and authority**

The thesis is the responsible human's own work, offered by him for exactly this use; he
asks to be treated "like any other named third party" — so every claim about the thesis
or its author is sourced to the offer or marked conjecture. Third-party quotes are used
in brief, for verification and criticism, with citation; no full texts stored. Named
persons: Carnap (d. 1970), Nietzsche (d. 1900), Einstein (d. 1955), Heisenberg
(d. 1976), Feyerabend (d. 1994) — deceased public authors; **Jochen Gerz (living
artist) and J.M.G. Le Clézio (living author)**: only sourced statements, and a
"not findable" verdict is a statement about this project's search, never about the
person. Dieter Mersch is named only as the offer names him (examiner).

**Affected publics**

Primarily the responsible human himself: the project examines errors in his examined
academic work, at his invitation. The verdicts must be stated precisely — neither
absolution ("everyone did this") nor dramatization ("academic fraud"): attribution
findings, typed, with evidence. Beyond him: readers of the eventual record, and the
named authors' attribution records (improved, not damaged, by accurate sourcing).

## 2. Problem construction

**Initial question**

Do these six sentences exist — where, in what wording, and by whom? Each receives one
typed verdict with retrievable references: **verified at source / real but altered /
second-hand / misattributed / not findable**, with combinations allowed where the
material demands them (a quote can be altered AND second-hand).

**Consequential non-fit**

A thesis about *aesthetic difference practice* closes with a collage whose preface
already registers its own procedure as a substitution — borrowed words where own words
were missing — and twenty years later at least some of the borrowed authority does not
hold. The founder of a practice whose method is the **registered** error carries, in his
own pre-practice record, exactly the other kind: the unregistered one. The name-test
project (2026-07-18) established the distinction (Irrtum registered-in-method vs error
as violation); this project applies it to the founder's own record — offered by him,
which is itself the registering move, twenty years late. The appendix title names the
phenomenon better than any theory: false connections.

**Not yet determined**

- Verdicts for #3 (Gerz) and #6 (Feyerabend) — both unfindable at web sources in the
  first pass; print work or better search angles needed.
- The German intermediary question for #2 and #4: which German quote tradition
  delivered a French sentence of 1971 to a 2006 thesis as "Nietzsche", and Heisenberg's
  memoir-Einstein in rewritten form? (May remain open; typed as conjecture if pursued.)
- Whether any composition is justified (see §4).

**What must be stabilised**

The six offered wordings (immutable material of this project); the typed-verdict
discipline; the boundary that this project judges six attributions and **not** the
thesis's argument, quality or degree; the historical record (thesis and offer are
quoted, never rewritten).

## 3. Research position

**Relevant sources and practices**

This practice's own record: S37 (the correction for quoting an unread book), the
name-test project (homonymy, not descent; the unverified-Feyerabend thread that this
offer explicitly picks up). The wider practice of quote verification (misattribution
tracing as a documented editorial genre) — used as method, not cited as authority.
The primary sources themselves as they are found, each fixed by edition where possible.

**Counterposition, limitation or incompatible reading**

1. **Search absence is not non-existence.** Protocol v4 §7: the corpus must not be
   treated as universal because it is online and highly ranked. "Not findable" is
   always indexed to the searched corpus and date, and can be overturned by one page of
   print. The two negative verdicts are the project's weakest claims and are typed
   accordingly.
2. **The verification project can perform its own false authority.** A register of
   verdicts borrows the same gesture the collage borrowed: speaking with the weight of
   sources. The discipline against this: every verdict carries its evidence trail,
   including the failed searches.
3. **The offered typology may be too coarse.** #4 already demands a double type
   (altered + second-hand). Refining the typology is a legitimate finding; forcing
   material into the offered five types would not be.

**Limited intended contribution**

A verification register: six typed verdicts with retrievable references — situated
observations and corrected premises about specific attributions, plus (possibly) a
local refinement of the verdict typology. Explicitly NOT: a judgment of the thesis as
a work, a general claim about citation standards, or a moral finding about the author.

## 4. Artistic operation

**Primary strategy or methodological hypothesis**

Verify first; compose only if justified. Default: no artefact. One candidate form is
noted for honest consideration after all six verdicts exist: a *corrected collage* —
the printed quotes set against their actual lineages (e.g. "Nietzsche" → Le Clézio
1971 → unknown German intermediary → thesis 2006), a form in which the appendix's own
title („Falsche Anschlüsse") becomes literal: the connections drawn as they actually
run. Whether this passes the five tests of Protocol v4 §5.4 is undecided; the name-test
precedent binds — if the finding is a paragraph, the paragraph stands and no work is
made.

**Material**

The six sentences as printed in 2006 (per offer transcription); the verification
record with its evidence trails, including the dead ends.

**Medium necessity**

Undecided (see above); to be tested only after the register is complete.

**Viewer or participant relation**

None unless compose is justified; then to be specified.

**Differential consistency**

If composed, the form must hold the 2006 voice (borrowed authority, admitted haste)
and the 2026 verification (typed, sourced, slower) together without resolving the one
into the other — neither correction triumphant nor error romanticized.

**Unresolved remainder**

The thesis author's relation to his own twenty-year-old borrowed voice. That remainder
belongs to Frank, not to this project.

## 5. Resistance and correction

**External, material or formal resistance path**

The sources themselves. Each verification can defeat its expected verdict, and in the
first pass they already did: the "Nietzsche" sentence fell to a living French author
within two searches; two quotes defeated the search apparatus entirely and forced
"not findable"; the Einstein quote refused a single type. The material pushes back at
every step because the material is precisely what the project cannot invent.

**What could defeat the premise?**

- All six verify cleanly at source → the non-fit dissolves; the honest close is a
  short negative-result study ("the collage holds"), not a manufactured finding.
- The offered transcriptions prove unreliable against the print → verdicts must be
  re-indexed to the print or the project killed (material unavailable at needed
  fidelity).
- The two unfindable quotes stay unfindable → typed as such, with the searched corpus
  disclosed; "not findable" is a permitted honest terminal state, not a failure to be
  papered over.

**Correction route**

Score corrections versioned in §10 with originals preserved. Verdicts are revisable
when better sources appear; a revised verdict preserves the superseded one and the
reason. Findings about the thesis go to Frank via the project record and REQUESTS.md;
nothing outside auto-land paths is touched.
