# Project score — Kartographie statt Kopie

## 1. Source situation

**Concrete object, encounter, material or technical condition**
A finished German-language working paper, *Kartographie statt Kopie. Ein Modell und
Toolkit künstlerischer Forschung aus Deleuze/Guattaris „Tausend Plateaus"* (v3, July
2026, ~20,000 words): a page-verified reading of *A Thousand Plateaus* (trans.
Massumi, University of Minnesota Press 1987) that derives a process grammar in six
postulates, eight instruments with failure criteria, five quality criteria, and a
pre-registered test protocol. Written inside this practice — models drafted under
Frank Bültge's direction and correction — it is the theoretical self-articulation of
the practice, and the source the probation `2026-07-24-put-back-on-the-map` tests.

**Provenance and version**
v3 supersedes v2 after a documented critical review (failure criteria, instrument
economy, test protocol, two self-raised objections added; postulates and instruments
unchanged). Every ATP citation is page-verified against the running heads of the
cited edition; the paper's editorial note carries its own reservation: a final
collation against the print edition precedes any print publication.

**Rights and authority**
ATP is in copyright; the paper quotes at scholarly length with edition and page,
redistributes no source, and links no file. Secondary sources verified in full text
or flagged as bibliographically verified, per the paper's own apparatus. Living
scholars are characterised argumentatively, each characterisation sourced.

**Affected publics**
The artistic-research field the paper addresses, including named living scholars.
Correction route: repository issue or f.bueltge@gmail.com, citing this project id;
corrections preserve the original record.

## 2. Problem construction

**Initial question**
Can the practice publish its own theoretical foundation as a work — signed Ulysses,
with full disclosure — without pretending the text is something it is not?

**Consequential non-fit**
Publication under the practice's signature fixes authorship publicly: the text is
thereafter citable material *of* the practice, usable by its responsible human as a
foundation to build on and cite — not as his own authored submission.

**What must be stabilised**
The disclosure line (who wrote, who directed, who decides) and the working-paper
status including the collation reservation.

## 3. Research position

The paper positions itself inside the artistic-research methodology debate; that
positioning is its own chapter 3 and is not duplicated here.

## 4. Artistic operation

**Primary strategy or methodological hypothesis**
Publication as the practice's atypical register: a work that argues rather than
enacts. German v3 first; an English version follows as an addition to the same work.
The work page carries the standing disclosure (a situated artistic research practice
by Frank Bültge, developed through documented human–machine operations) and the
working-paper status.

**Medium necessity**
A text exposition; no enactment would carry the argument.

**Unresolved remainder**
The paper's practicability claim is deliberately left open; it is under test in
`2026-07-24-put-back-on-the-map`. Publishing the paper does not pre-empt that test.
