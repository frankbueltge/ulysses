# Exposition — Kartographie statt Kopie

**What is published.** A German-language working paper (v3, July 2026, ~20,000
words) deriving a process grammar, eight instruments and five quality criteria for
artistic research from a page-verified reading of Deleuze/Guattari's *A Thousand
Plateaus*. An English version will be added to the same work.

**Who wrote it, who decides.** The text was drafted by the practice's models (Fable 5;
one revision pass by Opus 4.8) under the direction and documented correction of
Frank Bültge, who holds the publication decision and responsibility. It is signed
**Ulysses** — the practice's disclosed signature, not a concealing pseudonym: this
site states throughout that Ulysses is a machine-participatory artistic research
practice by Frank Bültge, developed through documented human–machine operations.
The paper thereby performs its own subject: a methodology of machine-participatory
research, produced machine-participatorily and signed as such.

**Status and reservations.** Working paper. Every ATP citation is page-verified
against the running heads of the cited edition (Massumi 1987); per the paper's own
editorial note, a final collation against the print edition precedes any print
publication. Sources that could be verified only bibliographically are cited without
page numbers and flagged. The paper's practicability claim is under live test in
project `2026-07-24-put-back-on-the-map`; publication does not pre-empt that test.

**Affected publics.** Living scholars of the artistic-research field are
characterised argumentatively; each characterisation is sourced. Anyone who considers
their position mischaracterised may use the correction route: a repository issue or
f.bueltge@gmail.com, citing this project id. Corrections preserve the original
record. No personal, participant or otherwise sensitive data is involved.

**Rights.** ATP is in copyright (University of Minnesota Press); the paper quotes at
scholarly length by edition and page, redistributes no source file and links no file
location.
