## 2. Problem construction

**Initial question**

Where does the "Irrtum" in *irrtum-als-methode* actually come from? `name-test` closed one
candidate ancestry (Feyerabend: foil, not source) and, by that book's own footnote, pointed
here. So: is Mach's *Erkenntnis und Irrtum* the ancestor the name-test's foil pointed to —
does this practice's "error as method" descend from Mach's coupling of *Erkenntnis* and
*Irrtum* in a *psychology of research* — or is Mach a **third homonym**, a distinct sense of
"Irrtum" joined to this practice's only by the shared word?

**Consequential non-fit**

`name-test` established a shape: the practice's banner-word keeps turning out to be a
homonym of its assumed ancestor rather than a descendant of it (first "swerve"/clinamen in
S43, then the whole name vs Feyerabend). The Mach lead is the one place where the foil's own
text hands over a *positively cited* predecessor — the strongest ancestry candidate the
record has produced. If Mach's "Irrtum" is error as a **genetic-psychological stage** in the
natural history of inquiry (a description of how researchers err and correct), that is not
this practice's "Irrtum" — error deliberately *disclosed and registered as an artistic
operation*, a normative method, not a psychology. Then the name descends from *neither*
Feyerabend nor Mach, and "error as method" is a modern coinage whose resemblance to both is
homonymous. If, on the contrary, Mach frames error as something a researcher can *adopt*,
cultivate or method-ise, the ancestry may partly hold — and with it recurs exactly the
tension name-test left standing: Mach's methodology is, in the foil's own words, "elastic,"
the opposite of this practice's **absolute** prohibitions.

**Not yet determined**

- What Mach actually argues about *Irrtum* and *Forschung*, at the primary (deliberately
  unread; the whole point of the reading operation).
- Whether "Irrtum als Methode" as a German phrase has an attested modern origin (art/design
  research, epistemology, essayism) independent of Mach — a bounded provenance question, not
  yet checked.
- Which typed outcome is honest: a corrected premise about the name's genealogy; a local
  distinction between Mach's sense of *Irrtum* and the practice's; a documented
  incompatibility exhibited rather than resolved; or a negative result (Mach is not the
  ancestor either).

**What must be stabilised**

The practice's prohibitions and verifiability rule are not up for revision by this project —
protocol amendment is human-only. The historical record stays untouched. If the reading
produces evidence bearing on whether the practice should be renamed, that is material for
Frank's decision (public authorship model), never an act of this project. The Mach seed's
"uncharacterised, unread" status stays honest until the reading operation actually happens.

## 3. Research position

**Relevant sources and practices**

Already worked in this corpus: the `name-test` study (Feyerabend as foil; the AM3→Mach
footnote); Rheinberger (error as productive difference in experimental systems — the
practice's most-worked sense of "Irrtum"); Hamming (error as correctable distance);
Lucretius via S43 (the swerve is not the clinamen); the disclosed-vs-hidden-error thread
(S34–S36). The atlas seed `mach-erkenntnis-und-irrtum` (my own, verified, unread). Noted but
not budgeted: the neo-Kantian / early-positivist context Mach wrote into, to be consulted
only if the primary demands it.

**Counterposition, limitation or incompatible reading**

1. **Orthogonality (the default counterposition).** Mach's *Irrtum* is most likely a
   descriptive, psychological-genetic category — error as a natural phase of inquiry — not a
   *deliberate method* of disclosure. If so, the honest close is a negative result: the name
   descends from Mach no more than from Feyerabend, and its two most plausible ancestors are
   both homonyms. This must be tested at the primary, not assumed — asserting it now would
   repeat the exact failure (characterising an unread book) this practice was corrected for
   in S37.
2. **The absoluteness tension recurs.** Even if the ancestry partly holds, AM3's own gloss
   calls Mach's methodology "elastic and historically informed" — the register the
   PROHIBITIONS explicitly reject. A Mach ancestry would therefore not comfort the name; it
   would re-pose name-test's unresolved point one ancestor deeper.
3. **The phrase may be modern.** "Irrtum als Methode" may be an independent 20th/21st-century
   coinage; then Mach is neither ancestor nor foil but a coincidence, and the genealogical
   question dissolves into a provenance fact.

**Limited intended contribution**

One typed claim (Protocol v4 §3), explicitly limited: a corrected premise, a local
distinction, or a negative result — about the relation between this practice's name and
Mach's book. Explicitly NOT: a new governing frame for the corpus, not a protocol amendment,
not a renaming (Frank's alone). Scale is not seriousness; this is a reading, not a program.
