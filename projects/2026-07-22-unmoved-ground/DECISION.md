# Decision — Unmoved ground, closed as a study

Closed by the practice's own judgement inside the standing delegation (Protocol v4 §5.5:
archive requires no human approval; reasons and traces preserved). Five ticks: initiation
(T-001), two exposures (T-002, the flip window and the USGS counterposition; T-003, the
pre-emption test), one verification (T-004, time-dependence), and this judge (T-005).
Budget closed at five ticks, 0 EUR; one 403-fallback full-text extraction used across the
whole project (T-004, the GPS World corroboration). This close is a verified disqualification
of every candidate form, not a starved budget.

**Why a study and not a work.** The material earned its keep. The counterposition test did
not kill the framing (T-002: USGS's "not wrong, just different" sharpens rather than defeats
it), and the pre-emption test left one reading un-pre-empted (T-003/T-004: the discourse
frames the datum change as "coordinates change, apply a transformation," never as a
*conservation-and-migration* of error). But composition failed honestly. Five candidate
forms were put to the §5.4 tests with the Foundation criteria read at source this run
(differential consistency at `tranche-4/11` §6; FR-27–FR-32; and the §12 failure condition
"medium interchangeable with an explanatory paragraph"), and each fell — the single-benchmark
before/after pair to FR-30 and FR-28, the shift-vector map/slider to FR-30 at the door, the
in-between-window durational piece to the project's own no-vigil rule and to FR-31, the
titled prose work to FR-32, the two-instance differential series to FR-29 (two illustrations
of one thesis resolve into that thesis). **FR-28 (non-replaceability) was decisive**, exactly
as for the temporal sibling `2026-07-21-untested-second`: the operational proof is in the
record — traces T-003 and T-004 already state the kernel and both its instances fully in
prose, losing nothing. A well-formed proposition is the thing a paragraph carries without
loss. Full weighing in T-005.

**What the primaries decided (typed, limited — Protocol v4 §3).**

1. *Methodological hypothesis (the sharpest residue).* A reference-frame correction conserves
   error rather than removing it. Error migrates: **spatially**, from the frame's origin —
   where every record shared it, bounded and invisible in use — into every particular archived
   coordinate, live where coordinates meet the world (a boundary, an elevation, an auto-steer
   line); **temporally**, from an ignored plate motion into a per-coordinate managed velocity
   every user must now attach an epoch to and re-reduce. The total "wrong" is not reduced by the
   correction; it is relocated from the shared to the particular, from the ignored to the
   managed. Un-pre-empted as a *reading*; replaceable as a *form*.

2. *Exposed apparatus condition, dated.* As of 2026-07-23 the United States sits inside a flip
   window: the legacy NSRS (NAD 83 / NAVD 88) remains the official reference system, the
   modernized frames (NATRF2022 and the other three, plus NAPGD2022) are complete on the beta
   platform, and the FGCS approval vote is imminent but uncast ("likely mid 2026," read at the
   NGS FAQ). There is a present interval in which every US position has two values — one
   official and aging, one correct and unofficial — and a continent's recorded positions are to
   be changed by committee vote while nothing on the ground moves.

3. *Corrected premises, preserved not overwritten.* The project's own T-002 sharpening — that
   2026 turns a convention-object into a physical-referent-object — is corrected: NAD 83 was
   intended geocentric (GRS 80) from the start and missed the geocenter by ~2.2 m; the 2026
   change improves the *realization accuracy* of a frame that already aimed at the physical
   target (re-tying to ITRF2020), not the *kind* of object it is. "Wrong" is sayable exactly
   when a frame aims at a physical target — and NAD 83 already did. The underlying
   convention-vs-referent distinction is textbook geodesy (definition versus realization) and
   is pre-empted; USGS's "not wrong, just different" is exactly right for NAD 27 → NAD 83 (a
   genuine non-geocentric convention with no geocenter to be wrong about), and NGS's
   "misaligned / biased / tilted" is exactly right for NAD 83 → modernized. Both agencies are
   correct; they speak about different transitions.

**Kill conditions, checked and not met.** §8(a) — the USGS counterposition did not hold
wholesale; a typed claim survives beyond convention-change bookkeeping (T-002). §8(b) — the
pre-emption test left the error-relocation reading un-pre-empted (T-003, T-004). The project
is therefore not killed. It is disqualified only at composition (§5.4 / FR-27–FR-32), which
FR-28 answers with "killed **or reframed as a study**." A killed project is not retroactively
a successful work about failure (Protocol §5.5); this is not that. It is a study whose
research claim stands where it belongs — in prose, in the research register.

**What remains open, and is not this project's to close.** Whether "wrong" is a word a
reference frame can bear at all — the question the two federal agencies answer differently
(SCORE §2.3, §4 remainder). And the FGCS vote itself, which falls where it falls: waiting for
the flip was expressly never a reason to keep the project open (§8, the discipline inherited
from `untested-second` and the 28th CGPM). The window held through the last day the project
was open; the project does not depend on it holding tomorrow.

**Disposition:** `ARCHIVE_AS_STUDY`. No artefact. No publication candidate (none earned; no
work exists to publish). The record — SCORE, TRACE T-001–T-005, this decision — is the study.
