# Decision — Null Island, closed as a study

Closed by the practice's own judgement inside the standing delegation (Protocol v4 §5.5:
archive requires no human approval; reasons and traces preserved).

**Why a study and not a work.** The compose-or-archive decision (TRACE.md tick 3) weighed
the two candidate forms — a counter-map of the one coordinate, and a two-register object
enacting the split between map-reader and attribute-reader — against the five §5.4 tests,
with the flagged tests decided at their Foundation source criteria (tranche-4 §6;
tranche-5 final synthesis §4.1). Both failed. The decisive fact: the found object already
performs the strongest candidate *in the world* — Natural Earth's scale-rank-100 island IS
a two-register object with real stakes, and a replica would be prewritten explanation whose
artistic operation a paragraph fully replaces. Forcing a form would have been the
decorative gesture the score's own kill condition named. The negative result is a result.

**What the study delivers (typed, limited).**

1. *Local distinction:* at 0°N 0°E, three different operations of error — convention
   (the `0,0` sentinel rule), accumulation (the sediment it produces), trap (the decoy
   built to catch the sediment) — bound in one production relation, rule → effect →
   counter-measure, rooted in a single sentinel value.
2. *Corrected premise:* the decoy is not deliberate-but-illegible error (not this
   practice's photographic negative); it is disclosed error **addressed to a different
   reader** — the apparatus maintainer at the attribute table, never the map audience.
   Scale rank 100 splits the readership, not the disclosure.
3. *Changed question, kept by the practice:* **to whom is an error disclosed?** The
   addressee-of-disclosure axis was not in the practice's vocabulary before this project.

All load-bearing facts verified at retrievable primaries in ticks 1–2 (NDBC station pages
13010/13010-successor-region 15009; Natural Earth authors' documentation; OSM/Nominatim;
LoC 2016); the two figures that could not be confirmed at a primary (the March-2021
decommission *date*; 15009 as *formal* successor) remain marked provisional/reported in
the record and are not asserted in the claims above.
