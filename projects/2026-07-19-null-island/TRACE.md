3. **Natural Earth decoy** — confirmed at the authors' own level (naturalearthdata / Kelso &

   coordinate. Caveat kept honest: an empty return is expected for *any* open-ocean point, so this
   is not a property unique to 0,0. The load-bearing datum it does yield: **OSM does not reproduce
   the Natural Earth decoy** — the "fourth regime" (community-governed error) is, here, essentially
   empty. The decoy is **dataset-specific**, not a property of the coordinate.

5. **Deliberate-error lineage (Agloe)** — sourced pass, retrievable. Agloe, NY: a phantom hamlet
   invented ~1930 (General Drafting; Otto G. Lindberg + Ernest Alpers, initials anagrammed) as a
   **copyright trap**, which briefly *became real* when the Agloe General Store opened under the
   invented name (Wikipedia; Big Think; NPR Krulwich 2014). The lineage confirms the score's
   framing and sharpens the inversion: the classic trap catches **thieves** by being invisibly
   present; Null Island catches **errors** by the same means — and Agloe's trap *turned into its
   own referent* (a real store), whereas Null Island's real referent (Soul) was *removed*. Opposite
   trajectories on the same mechanism.

**The distinction, tested (the operation's point).** The three "regimes" are **neither one thing
under three descriptions nor three co-equal regimes.** They form a **causal chain rooted in one
sentinel value**:
- (1) the **null-value convention** — `0,0` as the default for "no data" — is a *rule* in software;
- (2) the **accidental sediment** — failed geocodes and disguised positions piling up there — is
  the *effect* that rule produces;
- (3) the **decoy** — Natural Earth's invisible 1 m² island — is a *counter-measure* engineered to
  make (2) catchable.
Rule → effect → counter-measure: three genuinely different *operations* of error (a convention, an
accumulation, a trap), bound in a production relation, all rooted in the single sentinel fact. The
deflation counterposition is therefore *partly* right (one root, not three independent phenomena)
and *partly* wrong (the operations are not synonyms — a rule is not its effect is not the trap built
against that effect). The honest typed outcome is a **local distinction** plus a **corrected
premise** (below), not a "corrected premise → it's all just the default."

**Score correction earned at the primary (registered as §10 correction).** The construct called
the decoy "engineered to be **structurally invisible** … **illegible by design**." The primary
corrects this: the decoy is **not illegible** — it is **disclosure addressed to a different
public.** Scale rank 100 hides it from whoever reads the *rendered map*; but in the attribute table
it is fully legible to whoever reads the *data* — the maintaining engineer debugging a pipeline.
So it is not the photographic negative of this practice's disclosed-error method (disclosed vs.
hidden); it is disclosed error **aimed at a different reader** — the apparatus maintainer, never
the audience. That is a finer and truer distinction than "deliberate but illegible," and it is the
one the work (if any) would have to hold.
