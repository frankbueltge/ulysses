## 2. Problem construction

**Initial question**

Can this practice keep its name? Is the "Irrtum" in *irrtum-als-methode* Feyerabend's
error — the counterinductive, rule-breaking move his book defends — or a different
error altogether (Rheinberger's differential reproduction of the epistemic thing,
Hamming's correctable distance, the corpus's disclosed-vs-hidden distinction)? And can
the practice cite *Against Method* approvingly without dissolving the verifiability
rule that binds it?

**Consequential non-fit**

A practice named "error as method" is maximally *methodical*: a versioned protocol,
absolute prohibitions, a standing delegation, publish gates, byte-identical render
tests, sourced claims. The book its name appears to descend from argues — per the
atlas summary, not yet verified at the primary — that no methodological rule survives
the actual history of science. If the ancestry holds, the name contradicts the
apparatus that carries it. If the ancestry does not hold, the name is a borrowed
costume — S43's finding one level up: last time a word (*swerve*), this time the
practice's own name.

**Not yet determined**

- What Feyerabend actually argues, at the primary — including whether "anything goes"
  is his own principle or his ironic gloss on the rationalist's predicament (a known
  dispute; CONJECTURE until read — if the tidy slogan collapses, the tidy opposition
  "anarchism vs verifiability" collapses with it and this score must correct itself).
- Which of the practice's uses of "error" the book touches at all.
- Which typed outcome is honest: a corrected premise about the name's genealogy; a
  local distinction (which senses of "error" the practice uses that Feyerabend does
  and does not license); a documented incompatibility exhibited rather than resolved;
  or a negative result (orthogonality).

**What must be stabilised**

The practice's prohibitions and verifiability rule are not up for revision by this
project — they are protocol, and protocol amendment is human-only. The historical
record (works, journal, published entries) stays untouched. If the reading produces
evidence that the practice should be renamed, that is material for Frank's decision
(public authorship model), never an act of this project.

## 3. Research position

**Relevant sources and practices**

Already worked in this corpus: Rheinberger (the epistemic thing; error as productive
difference), Hamming (error as correctable distance), Stein (insistence vs dead
repetition), Lucretius via S43 (the practice's swerve is not the clinamen), the
disclosed-vs-hidden-error thread (S34–S36). The atlas seed `feyerabend-against-method`
(team-verified reference). Noted but not budgeted: Lakatos — *Against Method*'s
addressee and intended sparring partner — as the internal counterposition if the
reading demands it.

**Counterposition, limitation or incompatible reading**

1. The ancestry claim itself is contestable: the practice's "Irrtum" may descend from
   experimental-systems language (Rheinberger — already worked at the primary), not
   from epistemological anarchism. Then the Feyerabend test says nothing about the
   name's honesty, only about a surface resemblance of phrases — and the honest close
   is a negative result, not a finding forced into consequence.
2. The popular reading of "anything goes" as Feyerabend's first principle is disputed
   (his later writing reportedly reframes it as the *rationalist's* terrified
   conclusion, not his own slogan). Marked CONJECTURE until verified at the primary;
   the project must not build its opposition on an unread cliché — that would repeat
   the exact failure (quoting an unread book) this practice was corrected for in S37.

**Limited intended contribution**

One typed claim (Protocol v4 §3), explicitly limited: a corrected premise, a local
distinction, or a negative result — about the relation between this practice's name
and the book it is assumed to descend from. Explicitly NOT: a new governing frame for
the corpus (the n+1 the record warns against), not a protocol amendment, not a
renaming (Frank's alone).

## 10. Score corrections (versioned; originals preserved above and in git history)

### Correction 1 — 2026-07-18, after the primary reading (tick 2)

**Original framing (§2, "Consequential non-fit", unchanged above):** the book "argues
— per the atlas summary, not yet verified at the primary — that no methodological
rule survives the actual history of science," setting up the opposition *maximally
methodical practice vs anarchist book*.

**Corrected at the primary:** Feyerabend's target is **universal, ahistorical**
method — rules claimed valid "under all circumstances and in all stages of human
development" (AM3, pp. 18–19). "Anything goes" is not his principle but his reductio
of the rationalist's demand for one (AM3, Preface, pp. vii–viii; p. 23). A local,
versioned, explicitly provisional protocol is not, as such, his opponent. The
consequential non-fit therefore narrows to one precise point: this practice's
**absolute** rules (the PROHIBITIONS, the verifiability rule), which claim exactly
the all-circumstance validity AM3 denies any rule can have (AM3, pp. 157–8,
including "'be honest' — whatever that means" among the indicted principles).
The project's question is correspondingly sharpened: not "can a methodical practice
cite an anti-method book?" but "can a practice whose prohibitions are absolute cite,
as ancestor, a book that indicts absolute rules — or only as counterposition?"

Both conjectures flagged in §3 ("Not yet determined") are resolved; evidence and
locations in TRACE.md, tick 2.
