**Status:** research sketch (medium test), not a proposed work. Lives inside the project;
nothing here is a publication act. Companion to `sketch-operative-ruler.html`.
**Made:** 2026-07-24, work-line home operation (see TRACE tick 5). No web access, no external

`FIGURE-NOTE.md`.

## What the sketch does

It tests the one condition the static figure's gate result specified (`FIGURE-NOTE.md`,
TRACE tick 4): a medium earns the form here **only if it makes the ruler operative** — only
if the *same* value visibly changes category as the σ applied to it varies, so the
participant *performs* the displacement instead of reading three end-states side by side.

The mechanism, built to the letter of that condition and with one deliberate constraint:

- The measured parallax **φ = −0.40 mas is fixed and cannot be touched.** It is presented in
  a locked, dashed box. The participant has no control that alters the number.
- The **only** movable control is the claimed precision σφ (a slider). Moving it re-computes
  φ/σφ and re-judges the value:
  - σφ large → φ/σφ near the origin → **valid noise-negative**, informative under a prior
    (Bailer-Jones 2015: "does not correspond to a negative distance, because r > 0");
  - σφ tightened past φ/σφ = −5 → **clearly spurious solution** (Fabricius 2021: 3.04M EDR3
    sources; Rybizki 2021: classifier ground-truth).
- A second control applies the **zero-point offset** (−17 μas, Lindegren 2021) — the scale
  level: it displaces the ruler's own zero by a small, discretionary amount.
- When the value is in the spurious tail, a faint **mirror point** appears at +|φ/σφ|: the
  instrument arm (the −5 ↔ +5 symmetry by which the matched negative sample estimates the
  ≈1.6% spurious fraction hiding among the positives, Fabricius 2021).

The numeric transitions were checked (TRACE tick 5): at the default σφ = 0.40 mas the value
reads valid; at σφ = 0.04 mas the identical −0.40 mas reads clearly spurious. The category
flips with the participant's hand on the precision, never on the number.

## The design decision that carries the test

Locking the number and exposing only the precision is not decoration. It is what converts an
*assertion* ("error is relational") into an *enactment*: the participant cannot help but
notice that they changed nothing about the star and yet changed its scientific truth-status.
A paragraph must name the three regimes in sequence and hold them apart by assertion; here
one value is re-judged in front of the reader, by the reader, on a quantity they are
forbidden to move. That is the one thing the static figure and the paragraph genuinely cannot
do — and the sketch does do it.

## Medium-necessity judgement — honest, five topoi as prose

The question is not "is the sketch nice" but **§5.4 non-replaceability**: does it do work a
paragraph cannot, or does it collapse into an animated caption? The result is a genuine
advance **and** a genuine remaining defect; I record both, and pre-decide neither.

- **Connectivity.** Strong. The operative ruler makes the −5 ↔ +5 symmetry (the Null-Island
  trap-as-instrument) something the participant produces rather than reads: the mirror point
  only exists once they have driven the value into the spurious tail. The edge to
  `2026-07-19-null-island` is enacted, not cited.
- **Consistency.** Every value is anchored at a named primary and matches TRACE tick 2. The
  three registers stay on one axis without flattening — the offset control keeps the scale
  level distinct from the value/solution migration.
- **Function-testing.** The mechanism was tested against the exact condition the figure's gate
  set, and it **passes the discriminating test the static figure failed**: it enacts the
  migration between regimes, where the figure only laid the end-states side by side. This is
  the tick's positive finding — the operative-ruler direction is not a guess; it works.
- **New-production.** Genuine. "The number is locked; only its precision moves; watch the truth
  flip" is a form the source literature does not contain — it is the line's own way of making
  the relational-error claim performable.
- **Caution balance.** Here is the honest remaining defect, and it is decisive for the gate.
  The sketch still shows a **verdict caption** ("A valid measurement" → "A bad solution") and
  a legend that *concludes for the participant*. That is exactly the explanatory-dashboard
  register the protocol warns is **not** the default (SCORE §3 counterposition 2), and it
  trips §5.4 test 4: an interaction must *alter the operative situation*, not *reveal a
  prewritten explanation*. The current sketch does both — it alters (the regime, the mirror)
  **and** reveals (the glosses). So it demonstrates the mechanism in principle without yet
  clearing the bar for a proposed work.
  **Reverse question (symmetry rule): what is lost by stopping here?** The just-won knowledge
  that the operative direction is correct, and the now-precise next step. Closing or opening
  now would discard exactly that. So: continue, one more home operation.
