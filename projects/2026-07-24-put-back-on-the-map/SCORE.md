# Project score — Put back on the map

## 1. Source situation

**Concrete object, encounter, material or technical condition**
A German-language thesis manuscript by Frank Bültge (working title *Kartographie statt
Kopie*, version v3, July 2026; held privately by the author, cited here by chapter)
derives a process grammar, eight instruments and five quality criteria for artistic
research from a page-verified reading of Deleuze/Guattari's *A Thousand Plateaus*
(trans. Massumi, University of Minnesota Press 1987; cited as ATP with page). The
manuscript pre-registers a test protocol of its own (ch. 6): its instruments count as
candidates, not achievements, until real projects show that they change decisions —
and it binds every use to one building rule, "the tracing should always be put back on
the map" (ATP 13): a copy must be deformed by the map it lands on, never applied as a
template. The thesis addresses a human researcher inside institutions. This practice
is not one. That mismatch is the concrete condition: the thesis needs real-project
testing; the nearest available laboratory is a machine-participatory practice its
instruments were not written for.

**Provenance and version**
Thesis v3 (July 2026), privately held; instrument definitions cited by chapter, no
passage reproduced at length. ATP cited by edition and page throughout; every page
anchor used here occurs in the thesis's page-verified apparatus. Practice-side
baseline: Toolbox Candidates v0.3 (`docs/foundation/tranche-5-final/10`), Protocol v4.

**Rights and authority**
ATP is in copyright (University of Minnesota Press); this project cites the *work*
(edition, page), never a file location, and redistributes no source. The thesis is the
author's own unpublished manuscript; he directs its use here. No third-party rights
are touched.

**Affected publics**
None beyond the practice itself. No personal data, no participants, no living scholars
characterised. Should the closing balance be proposed for publication, the standard
affected-public review applies before any human decision.

## 2. Problem construction

**Initial question**
Can instruments addressed to a human artistic researcher survive the operation their
own source text prescribes — being put back on the map of a radically different
practice — and how much deformation does that take? Not "does the toolkit work", but:
*where exactly does it stop working, and what does the residue look like?*

**Consequential non-fit**
Three outcomes are live, and none is trivial. (A) The instruments transfer with
moderate deformation and change decisions — the thesis gains its missing evidence,
the toolbox gains candidates. (B) The instruments systematically miss the machine
practice — the documented miss is itself the finding, and it triggers (as a proposal,
not an action) a separate project: a second, practice-native reading of ATP without
the field apparatus. (C) The instruments are relabeling machines — they rename what
would have happened anyway. Outcome C is the failure the thesis itself defines
(Relabeling-Test) and must be reported as bluntly as A or B.

**Not yet determined**
Which of the three deformation profiles obtains; whether the five criteria behave
differently from the two instruments (criteria may transfer where procedures do not);
whether "deformation distance" can be stated in any form more disciplined than prose.

**What must be stabilised**
The per-application protocol (see §4) must be fixed *before* the first application and
must not change mid-probation; otherwise the probation can be bent toward confirmation
after the fact.

## 3. Research position

**Relevant sources and practices**
The thesis v3 (private; ch. 5–7 for the instruments and criteria, ch. 6 end for the
pre-registered test protocol). ATP itself for the instruments' page anchors — above
all the refrain's three coexisting aspects (ATP 311–312), the lines-and-dangers
apparatus (ATP 195–198, 203, 227–231), and the selection criterion of connectivity
(ATP 508). Practice-side: Protocol v4 and Toolbox v0.3, whose entries carry explicit
failure tests — a discipline the probation adopts wholesale.

**Counterposition, limitation or incompatible reading**
The strongest counterposition comes from the thesis's own field: instruments cannot
produce the artistic mode (Klein), Deleuze cannot be applied (Attiwill), and rhizome
vocabulary tends to become jargon (SHARE Handbook). The probation does not argue
against these positions; it operationalises them as its failure criteria. A second
limitation is native to this practice: a machine practice auditing itself with
reflexive instruments risks producing exactly the self-mirroring its protocol retired.
The countermeasure is the decision-touch requirement below — prose without a touched
decision counts as failure, not as result.

**Limited intended contribution**
A deformation record and a verdict per element (adopted / stricken / miss-documented),
usable by the thesis as its empirical section and by the practice as a toolbox
decision basis. Not "new knowledge" about ATP; not a method for artistic research.

## 4. Artistic operation

**Primary strategy or methodological hypothesis**
Three elements from the thesis enter probation — deliberately few, per its own
instrument economy:

- **P1 (thesis T5, refrain journal):** a process-temporal lens — which aspect
  dominates a working phase (hold-seeking, home-building, opening), and is an opening
  made at a self-created point or where the old chaos presses (ATP 311)?
- **P2 (thesis T6, lines-and-dangers self-analysis):** a self-risk lens, with the four
  dangers re-mapped to machine-practice failure modes as an explicit deformation step:
  Fear → format hardening (house-style repetition), Clarity → the self-appointed judge
  (protocol fetishism in judge steps), Power → self-canonisation of the practice's own
  record, Abolition → kill-grinding (closing lines for the satisfaction of closure).
- **P3 (thesis ch. 7, the five criteria):** connectivity, consistency,
  function-testing, new-production, caution balance — used as *topoi* in the judge/
  decision step of host projects, not as a scoring grid.

**Material**
The practice's own next projects. The probation runs in four steps. *Step 1 —
transcription (Umschrift):* one page per element, written before any application,
stating what transfers, what must be replaced, and what is lost — the deformation
gauge. *Step 2 — attachment:* the next up to three newly initiated projects carry the
chosen elements, named in their SCOREs at initiation (never attached mid-project).
*Step 3 — per-application protocol:* each use records (i) which decision it touched,
or that it touched none; (ii) a counterfactual note, marked as estimate, of what would
likely have happened without it; (iii) whether the element's failure criterion fired.
*Step 4 — balance:* per element, one verdict: adopted as toolbox candidate (a separate,
human-gated act), stricken, or miss-documented (feeding the §2 outcome-B proposal).

**Medium necessity**
The probation cannot be run on constructed examples — the thesis already has those and
declares them evidence of nothing. Only a practice with real initiations, real judge
steps and a standing failure-test discipline can supply what is missing.

**Viewer or participant relation**
None during the run; the record is public in the repository as usual. Publication of
any synthesis remains a human decision.

**Differential consistency**
The probation varies one thing across its applications — the host project — while
holding the per-application protocol constant. Differences between hosts are data,
not noise.

**Unresolved remainder**
Whether a machine practice can meaningfully keep a *journal* in the thesis's sense at
all, given that its working traces are already exhaustive; the transcription for P1
must answer what the journal adds that TRACE.md does not already hold — and if the
answer is "nothing", that is P1's failure criterion firing at step 1.

## 5. Resistance and correction

**External, material or formal resistance path**
The host projects themselves: their material and deadlines are indifferent to the
probation and will not bend to make an instrument look useful. The failure criteria
are taken verbatim from the thesis (per element) plus its global Relabeling-Test.

**What could defeat the premise?**
If all applications across all elements only rename what happened anyway, the premise
"these instruments are probe-worthy candidates" is defeated — verdict: stricken, with
the balance published as a negative result. If already the transcription (step 1)
cannot state an element in practice terms without losing its point, the element fails
before application — also a result.

**Correction route**
The transcription pages and the per-application protocols are the correction surface:
any reader can check whether a "touched decision" claim survives comparison with the
host project's TRACE. Corrections preserve the original record.

## 6. Bounded machine delegation

**Standing-delegation clauses used**
Ordinary research-record writes under `projects/**` and `journal/**` (allowlisted).
No writes to `docs/foundation/**`: should any element earn adoption into the Toolbox
Candidates, that adoption is a separate act requiring Frank Bültge's review and merge
(escalation trigger, named here in advance). No publication surface is touched by this
project; any closing synthesis proposed for publication follows the standard human
gate.

**Is model identity conceptually relevant?**
Noted but not load-bearing: the practice's identity across model rotation is one of
the machine-native questions a practice-native ATP reading would take up (outcome B);
this probation records which model executed which step, as usual, and builds no
argument on it.

**Is substitution or comparison required?**
No.

## 7. Traces

`TRACE.md` carries the transcription pages (step 1) and one dated block per
application (step 3). Host projects reference this project's id in their SCOREs at
initiation. Journal entries mark attachment and balance. The balance (step 4) closes
the trace.

## 8. Failure and stopping

**Kill condition**
Fewer than two host projects could be attached within the runtime budget — the
probation closes ARCHIVE_AS_STUDY as *inconclusive, not failed*, and says so. Or: all
three transcriptions fail at step 1 — the probation closes ARCHIVE_AS_STUDY with the
transcription record as the finding.

**Stop condition**
Three attachments completed and balanced, or runtime/budget reached with at least two
attachments — then step 4 runs, a disposition is proposed, and the project closes.
The outcome-B proposal (a practice-native reading of ATP), if warranted, is filed as
a proposal for human decision, never self-initiated.
