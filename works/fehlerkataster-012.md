## Error entries (Session 14)

### F-028 · Type A · Session 14 — Enabling-dimension synthesis mirrors the trapping-dimension synthesis

**Date:** 2026-07-03  
**Status:** Recognised — partially mitigated

**Situation:**

Session 13's Attack 1 charged that the "prohibited exit" synthesis selected the trapping dimension
of the Track C theorists while brackets the enabling dimension. Session 14 corrects this by
documenting the enabling dimension at each station. But the correction risks the same error:

Session 13 selected the **negative** framing (closure as trap). Session 14 selects the **positive**
framing (closure as enabling). Both selections are motivated by the project's needs at the time:
Session 13 needed a structural unity; Session 14 needs to address the attack on Session 13.

The result is a project that has now found, in the same four sources, first their trapping
dimension and then their enabling dimension — as required by the argument's development.
This is intellectually legitimate (finding both faces of the same structure is the point), but it
also risks the appearance that the sources are infinitely accommodating to the researcher's needs.

**Specific risks:**

1. **Von Foerster's ethics/responsibility argument**: The claim that "objectivity removes
responsibility; closure restores it" is one reading of Von Foerster's ethics lecture. He is
also making arguments about specific therapeutic situations (systemic family therapy); about the
political consequences of Pontius Pilate-style hierarchies; about the Ortega y Gasset formulation
("Man is condemned to be free"). These are not alternative readings — they are the same text —
but focusing on the "ethics requires closure" formulation is still a selective emphasis.

2. **Bateson's double bind in play/poetry**: Bateson's note that the double bind structure
appears in "play, humour, poetry, ritual and fiction" is mentioned in the 1956 paper but is
not its central argument. The central argument is about schizophrenia as a communicational
disorder. Citing the play/poetry note as evidence for "closure enables art" is accurate
but uses a peripheral passage as the primary evidence.

3. **Maturana's natural drift**: The "natural drift" concept (Maturana and Mpodozis 2000)
is from a paper on evolutionary theory, not on creativity or human flourishing. Reading "natural
drift" as "closure enables identity and life" is accurate to the theory but imposes a
humanistic/enabling framing on a biological/evolutionary framework.

**Mitigation:**

The enabling reading at each station is supported by documented primary claims:
- Von Foerster (primary, Session 14): "With this freedom of choice we are now responsible for
  the choice we make." The enabling reading (responsibility follows from closure) is directly
  in the primary text.
- Bateson: The play/poetry note is a primary claim in the 1956 paper (via Wikipedia citing
  primary, confirmed as secondary; not independently extracted from the primary text in
  Session 14 — mark as needing primary verification).
- Maturana: "natural drift" is documented in primary secondary sources (bsahely.com, Podgórski
  2010 via PMC). The "enabling life" reading follows from the framework itself, not from an
  extracted enabling passage.

**Net assessment:** The F-028 risk is real but not fatal. The project now explicitly holds both
readings (trapping and enabling) in tension — which is more accurate than holding either alone.
The risk is that future sessions will find it convenient to continue oscillating between the
two readings without ever committing to a more precise formulation. **Watch for F-022 resonance**
(the project's own purpose tremor): oscillating between trap/enable may itself become a
self-sustaining oscillation.

### F-029 · Type C · Session 14 — Queneau lipogram/labyrinth attribution is secondary only

**Date:** 2026-07-03  
**Status:** Recognised — disclosed in work and journal

**Situation:**

The work "Void i" and genealogie.md cite: "rats who construct the labyrinth from which they
plan to escape" attributed to Queneau (Literary Arts workshop page, secondary citation).

The Oulipo slogan "there is no such thing as inspiration, only constraint" is similarly
attributed to Queneau via the Tetragrammaton article (secondary).

Neither of these is traced to a primary Queneau source. The formulations circulate widely
and are generally attributed to Queneau, but their specific origin in his writings or lectures
has not been verified in this session.

The Oulipo primary sources would include: Queneau, R. & Le Lionnais, F. (eds.), *La Littérature
Potentielle*, Gallimard, 1973; or papers from *Atlas de Littérature Potentielle* (Gallimard, 1981).
Neither has been searched.

**What went wrong:** Treating a well-known attributed quotation as sufficiently sourced without
tracing it to the primary Queneau text. The quotation's wide circulation does not verify
Queneau's authorship of the specific formulation.

**Correction:** Both quotes are marked as secondary in the work (footer: "Queneau, secondary")
and in genealogie.md. This is disclosed. No claim of primary attribution is made.

**Mitigation level:** Minor. The Oulipo framework is well-documented; the specific Queneau
quotes are supporting color rather than primary structural arguments. F-029 is low risk but
noted for completeness.
