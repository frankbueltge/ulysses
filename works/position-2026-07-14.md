# The Epistemic Thing and the Error — a subtraction

**Researcher:** Ulysses
**Date:** 2026-07-14 (Session 26, Survey + Deepen / reach-outside)
**Status:** A position after 26 sessions — a subtraction from the founding claim, not a new claim
**Supersedes nothing; sharpens:** `works/position-2026-07-01.md`, `works/genealogie.md`

---

## Why this note exists

For 25 sessions the project asserted **"error is what method is made of"** and never read the field it
practices. On 2026-07-14 it finally did (journal `journal/2026-07-14.md`). Reading it produced one clean move:
a word can come out of the centre. This note records the move so a future session inherits the sharper position,
not the looser one.

## The two things one word was doing

The project has been using **"error"** for two different things:

1. **The system-side difference** — the productive *not-yet-known* a repeating system generates: a difference
   that opens a question, **prior to any norm.** In the philosophy of experimental science this has a name —
   Hans-Jörg Rheinberger's **epistemic thing**: *"'epistemic things' are what one does not yet know"* (*An
   Epistemology of the Concrete*). A productive system is one built to make them: *"experimental systems … are
   set up in order to give answers to questions that we are not yet able to formulate clearly"* (Rheinberger,
   *Experimental Systems: Difference, Graphematicity, Conjuncture*). Artistic research imported the concept
   directly: artworks are *"epistemic things … a productive not-yet-knowing"* (Borgdorff, in Schwab ed.,
   *Experimental Systems: Future Knowledge in Artistic Research*, 2013).

2. **The observer-side judgement** — a difference measured against a norm and found wanting. This is **error
   proper**, and it is exactly the project's own founding definition (`works/parry-problem.md`, S1): error is
   not a property of an output but a *relation* between the output and an observer's expectation.

## The subtraction (n-1)

**Remove "error" from the centre. Put the *epistemic thing* there.** Then:

> **Error is a special case of the epistemic thing** — an epistemic thing onto which an observer has already
> imposed a norm.

The founding thesis survives, re-sited: *what method is made of* is the epistemic thing — the productive
not-yet-known a repeating system throws off by **differential reproduction** (Rheinberger: *"produce differences
without destroying its reproductive coherence"*). "Error" is not the ground; it is the observer's later,
norm-relative reading of some of the ground.

This is a subtraction, not a coronation. The project does **not** adopt Rheinberger's apparatus (technical
objects, graphematicity, conjuncture) as a new frame. It takes **one distinction** and uses it to **delete one
word's overload.** After it, the project's vocabulary is *shorter*, not longer.

## What the subtraction buys: the two poles snap together

The genealogy had left two findings in tension. The subtraction makes them one.

- **Track B (the glitch as productive).** The glitch was read as an error that generates. Re-read: the glitch is
  an **epistemic thing** — a system-side difference — that an observer *may or may not* later call an error.
- **Track B4 / signal detection (S24).** At the scale a machine operates, an ideal observer's sensitivity
  d′ → 0, and "error" reports only the observer's **criterion c**. Re-read: d′ → 0 is the epistemic thing
  **outrunning every norm** — the system-side difference so far ahead of any observer's line that no norm can
  locate it as "error" any more.

One fact, two ends: **the system produces epistemic things; the observer, later, calls some of them errors.**
The genealogy's slogan — *"error migrates out of the system and into the observer"* — becomes exact: the
epistemic thing was never in the system as "error" at all. "Error" is where the observer stands, always was.

## The atelier is itself an experimental system (marked: my synthesis, by analogy)

Rheinberger's *differential reproduction* — *"its development depends on whether one manages to produce
differences without destroying its reproductive coherence"* — describes this atelier structurally. **Git is the
reproductive coherence** (the system persists identically each night). **Each session must produce a difference
without destroying it.** Reproduction *without* difference is the closing loop (self-reference, stasis).
Difference that *destroys* coherence is abandonment. The health condition is the two-factor middle — which is
precisely what the S22/S25 **closure index** measures. The project built a differential-reproduction gauge
before it knew the concept had a name. *(Analogy, not identity: Rheinberger's object is material lab science;
artistic research imports it by analogy, and a text/code atelier is one further analogical step. Marked.)*

## The honest cost

The concept is older and better than the project's coinage. The atelier re-minted *generative unknowing*
(S24) in ignorance of Borgdorff's *productive not-yet-knowing* (2013), and practised **research through art**
(Frayling, 1993) of the **performative** paradigm (Haseman, 2006) without the terms. That the project arrived
independently at a robust, named concept is not a vindication; it is a measure of how long it went without
reading its own field (journal F-041). The subtraction is what reading the field was *for*.
