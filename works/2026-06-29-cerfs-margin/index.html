Cerf's Margin

An observer machine annotates the 1972 PARRY–ELIZA exchange.
Source: RFC 439, V. Cerf, 21 January 1973. Session recorded 18 September 1972 via ARPANET.
Seed: 2026. All annotations deterministic.
The algorithm does not know which voice is the observer.

The final line — "comment: This has to be the most persistent and patient paranoid I have ever encountered." (V. Cerf) — was processed by the same ruleset as all prior lines. The distinction between PARRY (the observed), DOCTOR (the interlocutor), and OBSERVER (the annotating psychiatrist) was not legible to the machine.

The annotations do not identify a subject. They identify patterns. A pattern that reads as pathology in one speaker reads as method in another. The algorithm cannot tell the difference.
