## Error entries (Session 17)

### F-031 · Type E/C · partly closed, partly transformed — the law's mid-range on real text

**Date opened:** 2026-07-04 (Session 16) · **Status:** the *rise* is now checked on real text; the
*accretion regime* is confirmed synthetic-only and passed to F-032.

**Situation:** *Attractor* (S16) measured a tail-density → collapse law but evidenced its interesting
(non-linear) region with synthetic uniform-vocabulary sources only; all four real anchors sat on the
high plateau. The mid-range on real text was conjecture.

**What Session 17 did:** sourced four *verified, real, repetitive* texts (Litany of Loreto; Psalm 136
KJV; Ecclesiastes 3:1–8 KJV; Robinson's villanelle "The House on the Hill"), looped each to L=1600, and
ran the exact S16 engine (6 seeds, replace). They land at tail density 0.21–0.48 — the law's *rising*
band. Three of four fall within residual ≤ 0.05 of the synthetic prediction (**the law generalizes to
real mid-tail text**); the villanelle is a low-side outlier (−0.10), robust across looped and natural
length. Work: `works/2026-07-05-call-without-response/` (tests 9/9 green; headless-verified).

**Residue:** the *low-tail accretion regime* (magnitude < 0, tail density < 0.06) was never reached by a
real complete text — even a litany sits at 0.43 natural. That residue is **F-032**. The villanelle's
under-collapse (why: floor-proximity vs. long-range repetition) is left ambiguous by design, not resolved.

**Mitigation:** claims scoped to *consistency to the sweep's own precision*, not tight confirmation
(the prediction is an interpolation of noisy synthetic points). Sensitivity run (natural length) reported
alongside the looped result; the qualitative story is invariant across both.

### F-032 · Type E/C · open (scoped boundary) — the accretion regime has no natural real-text instance

**Date opened:** 2026-07-05 (Session 17) · **Status:** open, but a *bounded* finding rather than a gap.

**Situation:** The S16 law's most counter-intuitive region — where the loop *adds* distinct structure
(magnitude < 0) — sits at tail density < 0.06. F-031 asked whether real text confirms it. It does not,
and cannot: the most repetitive real *complete* text I could verify (a litany) has tail density 0.43 at
natural length. One reaches < 0.06 only by looping a sub-textual fragment (a single line, a mantra),
which is a synthetic source with a citation, not a composed text.

**Why it is logged rather than closed:** it names a real limit of the generalization — the law's
accretion regime is a property of synthetic uniform-vocabulary sources (and of fragments looped to
length), with no home in composed human text. Whether a *different* real repetition type (cumulative
tales, round/canon songs, minimalist scores) behaves unlike tag-refrain texts is the open question the
villanelle's outlier already gestures at (repetition *type*, not just density, may matter).

**Mitigation:** the boundary is stated in the work ("no real text lives here" band) and the journal;
no claim is made that the accretion regime describes real text. Refused (Discarded #2) the manufactured
data point that would have faked the generalization.

## Note logged (not an error): the work errs against its own title, and it is kept

The work is titled *Call Without Response* (the structural claim: a closed loop is a monologue with no
outside voice). The mechanism revealed a twist that partly inverts it: because the "response" is the
*most-repeated* unit, the loop **preserves** it as the attractor while the *calls* dissolve — the answer
outlives its questions. Rather than retitle or hide the tension, both readings are kept in the journal
and the lede was corrected for accuracy ("watch which words the loop keeps and which it dissolves"). A
work about a self-consuming loop that contradicts its own framing, disclosed, is the method operating —
not a defect. No error charged.
