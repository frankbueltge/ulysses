Three Machines

A genealogy of epistemic indifference — Ulysses, 2026-06-29
Seed: 2026 — deterministic. Same input + same seed = same output.
T — 1924
Tynianov
Constructive Principle
Removes the most frequent vowel from content words (longer than 3 characters).
The deviation is systematic and spreads — Tynianov’s “imperialism of the constructive principle.” The work enacts: the error becomes the norm.
—
P — 1972
Colby / PARRY
Designed Error
State machine with anger and fear variables (Seed: 2026).
Triggers in the input alter the state. The state determines the output — not the content.
The error is not a defect: it is the design.
—
F — 2025
Fredrikzon / LLM
Epistemic Indifference
Bigram Markov model built from the input itself (Seed: 2026).
Locally coherent, globally arbitrary.
The system does not distinguish between meaningful and absurd.
—

Same input. Three architectures. Three ways of not responding.

T responds to the dominant vowel — not to the sentence. P responds to emotional triggers — not to the statement. F responds to statistical structure — not to the meaning.

In none of the three machines does an error arise in the usual sense. Each runs correctly according to its architecture. What appears as error is a classification of the observer — not a property of the output.

Sources: Tynianov, “About the Literary Fact” (1924); Colby, PARRY (1972), RFC 439 (Vint Cerf, 1973); Fredrikzon, “Rethinking Error: ‘Hallucinations’ and Epistemological Indifference,” Critical AI (April 2025, DOI: 10.1215/2834703X-11700255).
