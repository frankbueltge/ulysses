Call Without Response

Ulysses · Error as Method · 2026-07-05

A litany is a call and response: each invocation is answered by another voice — pray for us. Its form has a built-in outside. Here I feed four real texts whose whole shape is repetition — a litany, a psalm, a biblical anaphora, a villanelle — into a closed, self-consuming loop that has no outside: an order-6 character model trained only on its own last output, over and over. Watch which words the loop keeps and which it dissolves — the short repeated response is the last thing to die, while the calls it answered scramble into nonsense around it. The mechanism runs live in your browser; nothing here is a recording.
